Auction report Trailers, tankers and more change hands in CastlereaA clearance auction - involving a significant fleet of contracting machinery - took place on Saturday (February 2).
The sale was held close to Castlerea, Co. Roscommon. The entries came chiefly from one contracting fleet - namely that of David Connolly.
The auctioneer was Micheal Doyle.
Also Read: Auction report: Foragers and mowers go under the hammer in Castlerea
We've already brought you reports detailing what the tractors, foragers and trailed mower-conditioners fetched. Now, in this article, we focus on the other machinery.

Some of the lots were subject to VAT; i.e. VAT must be added to the hammer price. It's specified in each caption (below) whether or not VAT applied.
Buyer's commission was 5% (plus VAT) on lots with a hammer price of up to EUR10,000. It was 3% (plus VAT) on lots with a hammer price over EUR10,000.
Click on a thumbnail in the gallery (below) to open up a full-size image; once opened you can scroll sideways to see the next picture.









This 2000 Chieftain low-loader (16t) sold for EUR5,800 (no VAT; plus 5% commission)








This 2001 Chieftain dump trailer (14t) sold for EUR3,900 (no VAT; plus 5% commission)








The two Kane trailers on the left (both 18ft/14t models) each sold for EUR7,900 (no VAT; plus 5% commission). The 2009 Kane 'Halfpipe' (20ft/16t) on the right sold for EUR11,700 (plus VAT; plus 3% commission)








This Kelly silage trailer sold for EUR1,500 (no VAT; plus 5% commission)








This 2001 Abbey slurry tanker (2,000-gallon) sold for EUR5,600 (no VAT; plus 5% commission)








This 2014 Fitzpatrick turf machine didn't sell. The top bid was EUR25,000 (plus VAT; plus 3% commission)








This John Deere 131 (front-mounted) mower-conditioner sold for EUR3,000 (no VAT; plus 5% commission)








This Kverneland plough (four-furrow) sold for EUR1,850 (no VAT; plus 5% commission)








This 1988 Fraser muck spreader sold for EUR950 (no VAT; plus 5% commission)
