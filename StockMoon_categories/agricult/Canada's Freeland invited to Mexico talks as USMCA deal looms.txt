Canada's Freeland invited to Mexico talks as USMCA deal looms1 Min ReadMEXICO CITY, Dec 9 (Reuters) - Mexico's government has invited Canadian Deputy Prime Minister Chrystia Freeland to talks in Mexico along with U.S. officials, a senior Mexican official said on Monday, as a final agreement on a new North American trade deal appeared to draw nearer. U.S. Trade representative Robert Lighthizer and White House adviser Jared Kushner are attending the meeting in Mexico on Tuesday, a U.S. administration official said earlier. Freeland's office did not immediately provide comment. It was not clear if she had accepted the invitation. Reporting by Frank Jack Daniel; Additional reporting by David
