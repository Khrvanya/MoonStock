Indonesia govt proposes levy on sweetened drinks, CO2 emitting vehicles, plastic bags1 Min ReadJAKARTA, Feb 19 (Reuters) - Indonesia's Finance Minister Sri Mulyani Indrawati on Wednesday submitted a proposal to parliament to levy excises on sugar- and artificially sugar-sweetened drinks as well as vehicles that emit carbon dioxide. Indrawati also asked parliament to reconsider her proposal to impose an excise on plastic bags to cut plastic waste, which she first brought up in 2017 but has not yet been approved.
