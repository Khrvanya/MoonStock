Fidelity Set to Launch Crypto Trading in the Coming Weeks ReportBoston-headquartered investment firm Fidelity is planning to launch cryptocurrency trading services for institutional clients, according to a Bloomberg report.Though no timeline is specified, the anonymous source of the publication thinks that the buying and selling of digital currencies will start within the next few weeks.Discover Barcelona Trading Conference - A Top Tier Crypto Trading EventThis news surfaced days after Fidelity published a survey report which concluded that 22 percent of the institutional investors have some form of crypto investments while 47 percent of the participants are willing to add digital assets in their portfolio.The trading platform will operate under Fidelity Digital Asset Services (FADS), a subsidiary created by the company last October. Earlier this year, the investment introduced a crypto custodian service for the same set of customers - hedge funds, family offices, trading firms, and any other high-volume clients."We currently have a select set of clients we're supporting on our platform," Arlene Roberts, a spokesperson from Fidelity, confirmed Bloomberg. "We will continue to roll out our services over the coming weeks and months based on our clients' needs, jurisdictions, and other factors. Currently, our service offering is focused on Bitcoin."
