Grayscale to Bring Ethereum Trust to Retail BuyersCrypto asset management firm Grayscale has received approval from the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority (FINRA) to list shares of its Grayscale Ethereum Trust on the retail market.According to the New York-based company, it is "the first U.S. publicly quoted security solely invested in and deriving value from the price of Ethereum."Discover the Barcelona Trading Conference - A Top Tier Crypto Trading EventLaunched in 2017, Grayscale operates ten crypto-based funds, including its Ethereum-based fund, most of which are only available to accredited investors with a minimum purchase limit of $25,000 through private listings. Similar to its Bitcoin and Ethreum Classic trusts, the Grayscale Ethereum Trust can now be traded in the open over-the-counter (OTC) market like securities."The secondary market really opens up the opportunity for any and all investors," Michael Sonnenshein, managing director of Grayscale Investments, told Bloomberg.Though the exact timeline of the listing is not specified by the asset management company, it could hit the open market in the next two weeks. The shares will be traded under the ticker symbol of ETHE, and each unit will be equivalent to around one-tenth of an Ether."ETHE will not generate any income and regularly distributes Ethereum to pay for its ongoing expenses.
