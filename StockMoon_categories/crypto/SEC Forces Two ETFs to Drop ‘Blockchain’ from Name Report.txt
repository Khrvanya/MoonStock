Bloomberg noted that the regulator had also altered the names of other funds that included buzzwords of emerging technologies such as 5G, artificial intelligence, and electric cars.At the peak of the blockchain hype in 2017, Long Island Ice Tea, a New York-based beverage company, rebranded itself to Long Blockchain Corp. Although the company was not involved in any dealings with blockchain technology, the mere addition of the word "blockchain" resulted in a massive surge in its share price. Within days after the name change, the market capitalization of the company went up by 500 percent.Unlike Amplify and Reality Shares, many firms are trying to introduce a crypto-specific ETF on the US market. The SEC previously rejected applications of multiple promoters pushing for Bitcoin ETFs. Currently, two applications - one by CBOE and another backed by Bitwise - are pending with the financial regulator for approval.
