Companies opting to operate as the first three have to apply for a license from the finance ministry, while ICO portals need the SEC's approval."The legislation also aims to protect investors from risk of fraud and deception by dishonest persons, money laundering and exploitation of digital assets to facilitate illegal financial transactions, while ensuring regulatory clarity to facilitate legitimate uses of digital assets," Suwanmongkol added.To date, five companies received Thai authorities nod to run digital asset exchanges.
