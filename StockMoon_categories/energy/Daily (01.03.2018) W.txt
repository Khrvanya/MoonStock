Daily (01.03.2018) WTI fell 2.2% on Wednesday due to higher U.S. crude inventories and a hike in gasoline stocks 
01032018

1107
DailyOil prices continued to decline on Wednesday after official data showed a larger-than-predicted hike in U.S. crude inventories and an unexpected rise in gasoline stocks. Brent crude was down 85 cents, or 1.3%, at $65.78 a barrel. WTI futures ended 2.2% lower at $61.64 a barrel. British prompt gas prices soared on Wednesday due to freezing temperatures across the country. NBP spot price jumped by 14.6% to end at 102.00 p/therm. Meanwhile, 2019 delivery contract settled 0.3% down at 44.07 p/therm, on warmer weather forecasts.
 
French prompt power prices rose on Wednesday because of very low temperatures. Meanwhile, increasing wind power supply in Germany pressured the day-ahead price. Spot electricity price in France soared by 5.3% to end at 84.28 euros/MWh. The German equivalent contract traded 20.4% lower at 30.02 euros/MWh.
 
Further along the curve, power contracts were almost flat. German Cal 19', the European benchmark, settled at 34.10 euros/MWh, while the French equivalent contract ended at 39.26 euros/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices settled above EUR10 level for the second consecutive day on Wednesday as a strong auction reverted an early fall into single digits. The carbon contract expiring in 2020 traded 0.4% lower at 10.26 euros/tonne.
