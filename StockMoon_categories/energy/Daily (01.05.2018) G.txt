Daily (01.05.2018) German year-ahead power price rose by 1.3% to new peak on Monday due to higher coal prices 
01052018

1048
DailyOil prices surged on Monday, recovering after early losses as Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu announced that Iran lied about its nuclear capabilities. Brent crude gained 53 cents, or 0.7%, to close at $75.17 a barrel. WTI futures added 0.47 cents, or 0.7%, to settle at $68.57 a barrel.British wholesale gas prices surged on Monday as cooler weather bolstered demand and imports from Norway fell because gas was re-routed to the continent. NBP spot price rose by 1.3 pence, or 2.4%, to end at 54.65 p/therm. Meanwhile, 2019 delivery contract added 1.6%, to settle at 50.91 p/therm.

The European power prices were mixed on Monday. German day-ahead price fell by almost 120% to -5.70 euros/MWh, and French spot price tumbled by 46% to 11.80 euros/MWh, although wind output is expected to decline.

Further along the curve, German Cal 19' European benchmark hit the new peak due to higher coal prices, traded 1.3% higher to 39.33 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract settled 1.2% up at 44.03 euros/MWh, new high.

EU carbon prices soared on Monday calmed by European public holiday as observers grew wary over the capacity of the market to maintain recent gains with the annual compliance season closed. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 ended 0.2% higher at 13.75 euros/MWh.
