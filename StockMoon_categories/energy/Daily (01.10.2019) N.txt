Daily (01.10.2019) NBP spot tumbled by nearly 15% on Monday amid higher Norwegian flows 
01102019

1019
DailyCrude oil prices continued to decline on Monday, amid receding worries over tighter supply and tensions in the Middle East after the last month's attacks on Saudi's oil sites. Uncertainty over U.S.-China trade negotiations added pressure on prices. Against that backdrop, Brent crude fell by 1.8% to $60.78 a barrel, posting the biggest 8.7% loss since the fourth quarter 2018. WTI futures dipped by 3.3% on a daily basis to end at $54.07 a barrel, plunging by 7.5% for the quarter.UK wholesale gas prices were predominantly weaker on Monday amid increased pipeline flows from Norway and robust storages. Traders' expectations for rising supply due to the upcoming end of the maintenance season also added to market bearishness. NBP spot slumped by 14.7% to 21.75 p/therm, while the February 2020 delivery contract traded 1% lower at 53.52 p/therm.
 
European day-ahead electricity prices rebounded on Monday, boosted by firmer demand and a steep fall in wind generation. The price for the German day-ahead power contract skyrocketed by 76.6% to 42.55 EUR/MWh. The equivalent French contract price soared by 40.9% to 39.97 EUR/MWh.
 
Forward electricity prices slightly diverged on Monday. The German power contract for delivery in 2020 edged 0.3% higher at 48.23 EUR/MWh, following the bullish moves in several forward gas contracts. In the meantime, the equivalent French contract was little changed on the downside, settling at 50.82 EUR/MWh, under the influence of an overall sluggish energy complex.
 
EU carbon prices extended losses amid a bearish energy complex on Monday. Worries over a potential Brexit impact curbed demand, keeping the market under pressure. The price for the 2021-expiry contract dropped by 2.2% to 25.36 EUR/tonne.
