Daily (02.08.2018) Brent crude plummeted by 2.5% on Wednesday, as OPEC stocks reached maximum capacity 
02082018

1132
DailyOil prices diminished severely on Wednesday, following a report that OPEC's stockpiles reached maximum capacity due to ample supplies during the month. WTI crude slipped by 1.6% to close at $67.66 a barrel. Brent crude tumbled by 2.5% to $72.39 a barrel.Strong Norwegian flows and forecasts for increased wind generation led to a reduction in gas contracts on the curve, as the system was slightly oversupplied on Wednesday. Accordingly, the bearish trend in oil prices led to the downward evolution. While NBP spot price edged 0.2% higher at 58.10 p/therm, the 2019 delivery contract traded 0.8% lower at 55.88 p/therm.
 
European power prices increased on Wednesday, due to higher demand and reduced nuclear capacity in France. The Germany's baseload day-ahead electricity price for Thursday delivery rose by 5.6% to end at 59.48 euros/MWh, due to lower wind output.
 
Meanwhile, extended maintenance at two nuclear reactors in France led to a hike in the French day-ahead electricity price, which gained 5.7% at 59.51 euros/MWh.
 
Along the forward curve, the electricity contract for 2019 delivery in Germany closed 0.9% higher at 44.34 euros/MWh, while the equivalent French contract edged 0.3% higher at 48.87 euros/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices reached a new 7-year high on Wednesday, after strong UK auction with a small number of bidders. The carbon contract expiring in 2021 closed 1.9% higher at 19.09 euros a tonne.
