Daily (02.09.2019) WTI crude fell 2.8% ahead of a hurricane near the Florida coast 
02092019

1145
DailyAn approaching hurricane near the Florida coast that could reduce demand weighed on oil prices. The bearish movement was amplified by easing U.S.-China trade disputes. Brent crude dropped by 1.1% to close at $60.43 a barrel. WTI futures lost 2.8% to settle at $55.10 a barrel.British wholesale gas prices declined on Friday due to an oversupplied system, as expectations for high output from the country's wind farms curbed gas-for-power demand.. The spot gas price plunged by 2.8% to 26.50 p/therm, while the October 2019 delivery contract ended 1.7% lower at 33.03 p/therm.
 
European prompt power prices decreased on Monday on lower demand and higher power generation from wind and nuclear sources. The German spot power price tumbled by 18.6% to close at 33.66 euros/MWh, while the French equivalent contract settled 19.2 % down at 32.26 euros/MWh.
 
Along the curve, power contracts posted gains on Friday tracking gains in the carbon markets. The German Cal 2020 delivery contract added 2.1% to close at 48.62 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract gained 2.2% to end at 50.34 euros/MWh. 
 
European carbon prices fell slightly after hitting an eight-session peak on Friday amid bearish economic prospects, Brexit risks, and a sharp hike in auction supply. The contract expiring in 2021 traded 0.6% lower at 26.95 euros/tonne.
