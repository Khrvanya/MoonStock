Daily (03.04.2019) European spot power prices added over 20% on Tuesday, due to a drop in renewables output 
03042019

1035
DailyOil prices inched up on Tuesday, hitting their peak in 2019, supported by additional sanctions against Iran and Venezuela. Brent crude edged 0.5% higher at $69.37 a barrel, while WTI futures gained 1.6% to end at $62.58 a barrel.British gas prices extended losses on Tuesday, as the system was oversupplied due to strong LNG arrivals and storage withdrawals. NBP spot plunged by 5.6% to 31.80 p/therm, while the contract for delivery in Winter 2019 lost 0.3% to close at 50.40 p/therm.
 
European day-ahead power prices were strongly bullish on Tuesday. The price for the German day-ahead power contract increased by 24.8% to 42.24 EUR/MWh, supported by a fall in wind and solar electricity generation. At the same time, the equivalent French contract traded 20.2% higher at 42.93 EUR/MWh, influenced by steady nuclear power availability.
 
Along the curve, electricity prices edged lower, tracking weaker gas and carbon markets. The price for the German power contract for delivery in 2020 dropped by 0.4% to 45.71 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract traded 0.7% down at 48.76 EUR/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices gained ground on Tuesday, on technical buying and supportive activity from speculators. The price for the 2021-expiry contract traded 0.4% higher at 22.74 EUR/tonne.
