Daily (03.05.2018) European carbon prices declined by 2.4% on Wednesday 
03052018

1610
DailyOil prices increased on Wednesday as the U.S. dollar dropped from its peak for the year after the Federal Reserve left its benchmark interest rate constant. Brent crude was 23 cents, or 0.3%, up at $73.36 a barrel. WTI futures added 1% to end at $67.93 a barrel.British wholesale gas prices fell on Wednesday due to forecasts for lower demand. NBP spot price edged down by 0.40 pence, or 0.8%, to settle at 53.00 p/therm. Meanwhile, Q3 18 delivery contract closed 1.9% lower at 50.98 p/therm.

European electricity prices were mixed on Wednesday. German spot power price rose by 0.1% to settle at 39.99 euros/MWh, driven by expectations for increased demand after the May 1 holiday. The French equivalent contract traded 0.9% lower at 39.96 euros/MWh, due to increased nuclear power availability following the restart of EDF's 1,300 MW Golfech 1 reactor on May 1.

Electricity prices along the forward curve dropped on Wednesday on forecasts for higher demand. German Cal 19' European benchmark ended 1.4% lower at 38.76 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract closed 1.2% down at 43.51 euros/MWh.

EU carbon prices prolonged its bearish movement on Wednesday, as weaker power prices weighed despite several improved signs of auction demand. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 closed 2.4% lower at 13.22 euros/tonne.
