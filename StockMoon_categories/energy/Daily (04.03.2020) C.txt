Daily (04.03.2020) Crude oil prices end on a mixed note on Tuesday despite an unexpected Fed rate cut 
04032020

1102
DailyCrude oil futures ended on a mixed note on Tuesday, with investors remaining skeptical whether the unexpected cut in the Fed's interest rates could indeed revive global demand hit by the new coronavirus. Brent crude for May delivery edged 4 cents, or 0.08% lower at $51.86 a barrel, after a volatile session. U.S. WTI crude soared by 43 cents, or 0.9%, to end at $47.18 a barrel.An undersupplied system caused by weaker flows from the UK continental shelf boosted British gas prices on Tuesday.

NBP spot surged by 6.9% to 26.20 p/therm.
 
Along the forward  curve, prices were also supported by a recovery in equities, with the gas price for Summer 2020 around  4% higher at 23.34 p/therm. At the same time, the gas price for year-ahead delivery increased by 3.5% to 36.90 p/therm.
 
European spot electricity prices gained ground on Tuesday, spurred by  stronger demand and weaker wind power output in Germany ,  as well as lower nuclear availability in France. German day-ahead power contract rallied by 9.3% to 39.61 EUR/MWh, while the similar contract in France soared by 7.4% to 39.34 EUR/MWh.

Along the forward curve, prices also were bullish tracking higher gas and coal prices. German Cal'21 power contract firmed by 1.9% to 40.96 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent contract in France advanced  by 1.6% to 42.78 EUR/MWh.

Carbon prices decreased on Tuesday despite an unexpected U.S. interest rate cut, as traders continued to worry about the COVID-19 coronavirus impact.EUAs expiring in 2021 lost 0.7% at 23.55 EUR/tonne.
