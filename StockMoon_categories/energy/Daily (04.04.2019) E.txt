Daily (04.04.2019) EU carbon prices soared by over 5% on Wednesday on positive Brexit progress 
04042019

1036
DailyOil prices slipped on Wednesday, on data showing an unexpected rise in U.S. inventories. Meanwhile, US sanctions against Iran and Venezuela and OPEC-led supply cuts limited losses. Brent crude edged 0.1% lower at $69.31 a barrel, while WTI futures traded 0.2% down at $62.46 a barrel.British gas prices gained strong momentum on Wednesday, as colder weather established in the region increased demand for gas used for heating. NBP spot rose by 4.4% to 33.20 p/therm, while the contract for delivery in Q4 2019 added 3% to close at 49.24 p/therm.
 
European day-ahead power prices were mixed on Wednesday. The price for the German day-ahead power contract edged 0.5% lower at 42.02 EUR/MWh on rising wind power generation. Meanwhile, the equivalent French contract traded 2% higher at 43.80 EUR/MWh, on lower-than-usual nuclear power availability.
 
Along the curve, electricity prices were bullish on Wednesday, following stronger gas and carbon prices. The price for the German power contract for delivery in 2020 rose by 2.5% to 46.87 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract traded 2.4% up at 49.91 EUR/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices extented gains on Wednesday, amid current Brexit progess for a softer deal to avoid a hard exit out of the EU. The price for the 2020-expiry contract traded 5.2% higher at 23.48 EUR/tonne.
