Daily (04.05.2018) French spot power contract down 8.9% on Thursday due to higher wind and solar generation 
04052018

1122
DailyOPEC output cuts and the potential for new U.S. sanctions on Iran bolstered oil prices on Thursday. Brent crude gained 26 cents, or 0.4%, to close at $73.62 a barrel. WTI futures added $0.5, or 0.7%, to end at $68.43 a barrel.British prompt gas prices were pressured by higher temperatures and lower demand on Thursday. NBP spot price lost 0.75 pence, or 1.4%, to settle at 52.25 p/therm. Further along the curve, prices rose on weak supply. Winter 2018 delivery contract closed 0.2% higher at 57.80 p/therm.

European power prices were mixed on Thursday. French spot price fell by 8.9% to close at 36.42 euros/MWh on reduced consumption before the weekend and increased in wind and solar power generation. German spot price surged by 1.3% to end at 40.51 euros/MWh, due to solid demand.


On the forward curve, European prices slid on milder weather forecast for the next two weeks. German Cal 19' European benchmark traded 0.3% lower at 38.65 euros/MWh. Meanwhile, the French equivalent contract edged 0.3% lower at 43.40 euros/MWh.

EU carbon prices declined for the third consecutive session on Thursday to settle below EUR13 level, or almost a two-week low. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 closed 0.8% down at 13.12 euros/tonne.
