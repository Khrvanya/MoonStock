Daily (05.03.2020) Brent down 1.4% on Wednesday as Russia resists to OPEC calls for drastic oil cuts 
05032020

1042
DailyCrude oil futures fell on Wednesday after Russia opposed Saudi Arabia's plans requesting OPEC+ deeper oil production cuts in order to offset an eroding demand due to the coronavirus outbreak.Brent crude for May delivery declined by 73 cents, or 1.4%, to settle at $51.13 a barrel on ICE London. U.S. WTI crude slipped by 40 cents, or around 0.9%, to end at $46.78 a barrel on NYMEX.
 
A supply glut caused by a healthy LNG send-out weighed on the British day-ahead gas price on Wednesday. NBP spot shed 3.6% at 25.25 p/therm.
 
A bearish trend was also noticed in forward gas prices, tracking losses in equity and energy markets.
 
The gas price for Summer 2020 delivery dipped by 2.7% to 23.39 p/therm, while the gas for year-ahead delivery decreased by 0.3% to 36.77 p/therm.
 
European spot electricity prices moved downwards on Wednesday due to lower demand.
 
The German day-ahead power price decreased by about 9% to 36.07 EUR/MWh, while the price for the similar contract in France dipped by about 14% to 33.86 EUR/MWh.
 
Year-ahead power contracts firmed on Wednesday following the bullish evolution in carbon prices.
 
German Cal'21 power increased by 0.4% to 41.12 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent contract in France added 0.09% at 42.82 EUR/MWh.
 
Carbon prices rebounded on Wednesday, as the market reacted to the UK's first auction in more than a year. EUAs expiring in 2021 soared by 2% to 24.02 EUR/tonne.
