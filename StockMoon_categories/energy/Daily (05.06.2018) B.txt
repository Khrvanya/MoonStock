Daily (05.06.2018) Bullish coal, gas and carbon markets boosted European power prices on Monday 
05062018

1429
DailyOil prices continued the downward evolution on Monday, as investors kept selling amid rising U.S. output, probable global supply growth and trade concerns. Brent crude futures declined by $1.50 a barrel, or 2 percent, to end at $75.29 a barrel. U.S. West Texas Intermediate crude dropped by 1.6% to close at $64.75.British wholesale gas prices rose on Monday because of lower temperatures forecast, maintenance at Norway's gas export facilities and an undersupplied system. NBP spot gas price jumped by 1.80 pence, or 3.2%, to settle at 58.60 p/therm. Meanwhile, Q3 2018 delivery contract ended 1.4% higher at 55.51 p/therm, as strong coal prices weighed on curve prices.
 
European wholesale electricity prices surged on Monday following the upward trend in gas, coal and carbon markets. Prompt power prices increased due to a forecasted rise in demand. The German baseload contract for Tuesday delivery traded 10.79 euros, or 26.8% higher at 51.01 euros/MWh compared with the price paid on Friday for Monday delivery. The French equivalent contract for Tuesday delivery gained 23.39 euros to 52.08 euros/MWh.
 
Further along the curve, the German Cal'19 baseload electricity price for 2019 delivery, the European benchmark, increased by 3.9 percent to 41.75 euros/MWh. The equivalent French contract rose 3.6 percent to settle at 46.39 euros/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices exceeded again the EUR16 level on Monday as higher energy prices and a stronger auction offered bullish support in defiance of cautions from observers about a pull-back in EUAs. The carbon contract expiring in 2020 closed 5.5% up at 16.64 euros a tonne.
