Daily (06.02.2018) German Spot Electricity Price Hiked by 17% on Monday Due to Higher Demand 
06022018

1153
DailyOil prices ended lower on Monday as U.S. output increases, a weaker physical market and recent dollar strength added to the pressure from an extensive decline across equities and commodities markets. Brent crude slumped by 1.4 percent, at $67.62 a barrel. WTI tumbled by 2% to $64.15 per barrel.British wholesale gas prices dropped on Monday due to strong flows from Norway which kept the system oversupplied and on forecasts for slightly higher temperatures later in the week. The NBP spot fell by 2.1% to close at 49.80 pence per therm. The Summer 2018 delivery contract was traded 1.3% lower at 48.62 p/therm.

European spot electricity prices for day-ahead delivery soared on Monday encouraged by an increased demand at the beginning of a forecast cold week across western Europe. German spot price jumped by 17% to 47.12 euros/MWh, while the French equivalent surged by 23% to 50.56 euros/MWh. 

European future contracts declined as prices on fuels and carbon fell. The German Cal'19, the European benchmark, dipped by 0.8 percent to 33.89 euros/MWh. The French 2019 year-ahead contract was down 0.6 percent to 38.95 euros/MWh.

EU carbon prices hiked following bearish forecasts from higher auction supply and weak fundamentals. Carbon contract expiring in 2020 settled 1.3% lower at 9.21 euros per tonne.
