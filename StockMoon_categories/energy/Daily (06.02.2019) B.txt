Daily (06.02.2019) Brent crude dropped by nearly 1% on stronger dollar and concerns over a global economic slowdown 
06022019

1042
DailyOil prices continued the bearish trend on Tuesday, as concerns over a global economic slowdown stepped in. At the same time, a stronger dollar also put pressure on crude futures. Brent crude fell by 0.9% to 61.98 USD/bbl, while WTI crude dipped by 1.7% to close at 53.66 USD/bbl.British gas prices declined on Tuesday, as an oversupplied system weighed on the market. Moreover, milder weather forecasts also decreased demand for gas used for heating. As a result, NBP spot price plunged by 2.4% to 50.25 p/therm, while the contract for Q2 2019 delivery traded 0.2% lower at 48.07 p/therm.
 
European prompt electricity prices tumbled on Tuesday on milder weather forecasts and higher wind, and solar power output. The German day-ahead power price dove by 4.6% to 51.32 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract traded 9.2% lower at 53.00 EUR/MWh.
 
On the other side, forward electricity prices added gains as coal and carbon emissions markets firmed. The German power contract for delivery in 2020 traded 0.9% higher at 48.55 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract was 1.2% up at 51.21 EUR/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices recorded small gains on Tuesday due to bullish trading activity. The price for the 2021-expiry contract added 0.5% closing at 24.12 EUR/tonne.
