Daily (06.08.2018) French day-ahead power price tumbled by 20% on Friday, following the drop in carbon and coal markets. 
06082018

1045
DailyOil prices edged lower on Friday, as increased concerns over China's denial to admit the sanctions against Iranian crude supported the fears over falling demand on U.S supplies. Consequently, Brent crude slipped by 0.3% to $73.21 a barrel. WTI crude dropped by 0.7% to $68.49 a barrel.British wholesale gas prices jumped on Friday, as the planned maintenance at the Forties platform started and led to an undersupplied system. The exports to Europe have increased and contributed to a hike in NPB spot price, which rose by 1% to 59.00 p/therm. Along the forward curve, September 2018 delivery contract traded 1.5% higher at 58.77 p/therm. 
 
The Germany's baseload day-ahead electricity contract for Monday delivery settled 18.5% lower at 51.12 euros/MWh, on expectations for stronger wind power generation and reduced demand. Meanwhile, the French spot electricity contract had the same downward evolution, trading 19.4% lower at 51.35 euros/MWh.
 
On Friday, European power contracts on the curve were bullish tracking the related coal prices.  The French Cal'19 electricity contract closed at 48.98 euros/MWh, or around 0.6% higher. In Germany, the year-ahead contract settled 1.1% higher at 44.60 euros/MWh.

EU carbon prices rose on Friday, holding tight near the EUR18 level after a bullish UK auction. 
The carbon contract expiring in 2021 edged 0.2% higher at 18.96 euros a tonne.
