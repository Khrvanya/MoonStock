Daily (06.10.2017) Oil prices soared 2% on Thursday on signs of production cut in 2018 
06102017

1110
DailyOil prices increased by about 2 percent on Thursday as signs Saudi Arabia and Russia would cut output through next year. The news offset Wednesday's U.S. data indicating record U.S. exports and the return of production at a major Libyan oilfield. Brent futures closed 2.2 percent, or $1.20, up at $57 a barrel. Meanwhile, U.S. crude rose 81 cents, or 1.6 percent, to end at $50.79.

British wholesale gas contracts edged up on Thursday as cooler weather enhanced heating demand and on predictions of lower wind power generation. NBP spot gas jumped by 1.35 pence, or 2.9%, to 48.15 pence per therm. Further along the curve, November 2017 delivery contract settled 1.05 pence, or 2.2% higher at 50.13 p/therm. 
 
French power prices for the year-ahead delivery hit new record levels on Thursday on continuing concerns over French nuclear power availability following further postponements in the restart of some EDF reactors. The French electricity contract for delivery in 2018 traded 1.2% higher at 42.38 euros/MWh. The German equivalent contract closed slightly up at 35.44 euros/MWh.

In the day-ahead spot delivery market, power prices rose on Thursday, with the German contract jumping as forecasts indicated a sharp decline in wind electricity generation. The German baseload price for Friday delivery hiked 51%, or 9.22 euros, to 27.25 euros/MWh. The equivalent French day-ahead contract fell 5% to 47.15 euros/MWh.
 
European carbon prices were almost constant on Thursday despite a strong auction and after climbing back from a one-week low. December 2018 and December 2019 expiry EU carbon permits were flat, while the carbon contract expiring in December 2020 closed slightly lower at 7.09 euros a tonne.
