Daily (07.05.2018) WTI crude surged by 1.9% on Friday due to tight global supplies 
07052018

1302
DailyOil prices rose on Friday on tight global supplies and because the market waited for the news on possible new U.S. sanctions against Iran. Brent crude added $1.25, or 1.7 percent, to close at $74.87 a barrel. WTI futures settled 1.9% higher at $69.72 a barrel.British wholesale gas prices were mixed on Friday. Warmer weather forecasts over the weekend weighed on day-ahead gas contract. NBP spot edged 0.5% lower to end at 52.00 p/therm. Further along the curve, June 2018 delivery contract traded 3.3% higher at 52.28 p/therm, due to lower gas imports from Norway which left the system undersupplied.

European prompt electricity prices dropped on Friday on forecasts for increased wind and solar output. German power spot price fell by around 30% to 28.41 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract lost 23.2% to close at 27.95 euros/MWh.

Along the forward curve, prices rose on Friday tracking gains in coal and carbon prices. German Cal 19' European benchmark traded 0.7% up at 38.90 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract ended 0.7% higher at 43.70 euros/MWh.

European carbon prices soared again above EUR13 level on Friday, stopping a series of three consecutive daily losses. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 settled 0.5% higher at 13.18 euros/tonne.
