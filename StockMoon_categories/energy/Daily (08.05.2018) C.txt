Daily (08.05.2018) Carbon prices jumped by 4.6% on Monday on long-awaited announcement of EU aviation allowance auctions 
08052018

1129
DailyOil prices hiked on Monday due to forecasts for higher prices on gasoline and consumer goods. Brent futures rose $1.3, or 1.7%, to end at $76.17 a barrel. WTI crude surged by 1.5% to settle at $70.73 a barrel, for the first time above $70 level since late 2014.British wholesale gas prices remained unchanged on Monday due to a bank holiday in the UK. The 2019 delivery contract was at 50.67 p/therm.

European power prices were mixed on Monday. German spot power price dropped by 3.3% to close at 27.47 euros/MWh on forecasts for increased renewable generation. Meanwhile, French equivalent contract traded 8.4% higher at 30.30 euros/MWh, supported by gains in carbon permits and oil prices.  Further along the curve, prices soared on stronger coal prices. German Cal 19' European benchmark added 1.7% to end at 39.54 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract closed 1.4% up at 44.31 euros/MWh.

European carbon prices surged on Monday as the European Commission announced the long-awaited dates for 2018's EU aviation allowance auctions. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 gained 4.6% to end at 13.78 euros/tonne.
