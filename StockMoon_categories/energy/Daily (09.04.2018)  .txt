Daily (09.04.2018)  Milder weather and low demand made German day-ahead power contact to plunge by 53.2% 
09042018

1323
EnergyOil prices declined on Friday as tensions in trade war between U.S. and China strengthened, following U.S. President's threats to impose new tariffs on China.Brent crude decreased by $1.22, or around 1.8%, to settle at $67.11 a barrel. U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude futures dropped by $1.48, or 2.33%, to $62.06 a barrel.
 
UK wholesale gas prices fell on Friday due to oversupply and low demand. Day-ahead gas price shrunk by 0.40 pence, or 0.8%, to end at 48.65. Winter 2018 delivery contract edged 0.15% down to 53 p/therm.

European prompt electricity prices were mixed on Friday. German day-ahead power contract as well as its French equivalent plunged due to forecast for milder weather. 
German baseload contract for day-ahead delivery diminished by 53.2% to 16.48 euros a megawatt hour (MWh). French spot price settled 28% down at 26.31 euros.
 
Year-ahead power curve prices went slightly up. Germany's Cal '19 baseload contract for 2019 delivery, the European benchmark, grew by 0.4 percent to 36.62 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract gained 0.4 percent to close at 41.21 euros/MWh.

EUAs raised on Friday after a stronger auction. The carbon contract expiring in 2021 inched 0.3% up to 13.45 euros a tonne.
