Daily (09.08.2018) WTI Crude Plummeted by 3.2% on Wednesday, due to Increased Trade Tensions Between U.S. and China 
09082018

1108
DailyOil crude prices fell on Wednesday, due to increased trade tensions between U.S. and China, who is set to impose retaliatory 25% tariffs on $16 billion of American goods. Both Brent crude and WTI prices dropped by 3.2% to close at $72.28 a barrel and $66.94 a barrel respectively.On Wednesday, an undersupplied system due to an unexpected outage at the Vesterled pipeline boosted British gas prices on the curve. Winter 2018 delivery contract traded 0.3% higher at 65.46 p/therm. Nonetheless, flows from Norway are getting stronger, nudging NBP spot price by 0.3% to 59.00 p/therm.
 
European spot power prices fell on Wednesday due to high solar generation, although gains in oil, coal and carbon markets led to a bullish trend in power prices.
 
The German baseload electricity contract dropped by 1.9% to 55.06 EUR/MWh, while the German Cal'19 European benchmark lost 0.5% to end at 44.95 EUR/MWh. 
 
French spot power price fell by 3.2% to 54.71 EUR/MWh, while the French electricity contract for 2019 delivery ended 0.6% lower at 49.24 EUR/MWh. 
 
European carbon prices were bearish on Wednesday, following the downward evolution of the power market and on worries over the trade war between world's major economies. The 2020 expiry carbon contract closed 0.8% lower at 18.08 euros a tonne.
