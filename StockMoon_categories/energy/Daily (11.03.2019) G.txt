Daily (11.03.2019) German spot power price plummeted by nearly 70% due to strong renewables output

DailyCrude oil prices fell on Friday, weighed by downbeat U.S. job growth data. The bearish equity markets, influenced by ECB's announcement about the continued weakness of the European economy coupled with lower Chinese exports and imports data, put more pressure on prices. Brent crude ended 0.8% down at $65.74 a barrel, while WTI futures dropped by 1% to $56.07 a barrel.Friday saw rather steady British wholesale gas prices in a balanced system. The NBP spot price was 0.1% up at 42.85 p/therm, as demand is expected to rise due to short-term forecasts for colder weather and lower LNG supply. In the meantime, the contract for delivery in Winter 2019 settled 0.3% down at 56.34 p/therm.
 
European day-ahead power prices plummeted on Friday, due to abundant wind and solar generation. The price for the German spot power contract plunged by 69.8% to 7.98 EUR/MWh. At the same time, the equivalent French contract sank by 40% to 24.03 EUR/MWh, amid increased nuclear availability and lower consumption.
 
Along the curve, electricity prices were mixed on Friday. The German power contract for delivery in 2020 added 0.2% to close at 48.16 EUR/MWh, with gains capped by lower coal and carbon prices. In the meantime, the equivalent French contract price declined by 0.2% to 51.26 EUR/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices posted slight losses in a choppy trading on Friday, amid traders' expectations for next week's UK Brexit votes and ETS compliance deadline. The price for the 2020-expiry contract ended 1.1% down at 23.44 EUR/tonne.
