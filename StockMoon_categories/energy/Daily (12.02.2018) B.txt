Daily (12.02.2018) Brent futures fell by 3.1% to reach its lowest level since December 13 
12022018

1138
DailyOil prices declined on Friday after renewed concerns regarding rising crude supplies and a weak stock market. Brent crude slumped by 3.1% to close at $62.79 a barrel, its lowest level since December 13.UK wholesale gas prices hiked on Friday as forecasts for last weekend and this week indicated slightly lower temperatures. Day-ahead gas price surged by 1.2% to end at 50.15 p/therm. Along the forward curve, Summer 2018 delivery contract increased by 1% to 41.68 p/therm.
 
European wholesale electricity prices fell on Friday after rising wind power supply. German baseload delivery decreased by 3.5 percent to end at 39.38 euros a megawatt-hour (MWh). The French equivalent tumbled by 11% to settle at 42.02 euros/MWh.
 
Along the forward curve, German 2019 delivery contract, the European benchmark, edged 0.8% lower to close at 33.05 euros a megawatt-hour(MWh). The same French delivery contract slipped by 0.3% to end at 38.7 euros/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices jumped to their peak of the week on Friday due to strong auction demand signs that eclipsed the impact of a bearish energy complex. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 soared by 1.2% to settle at 9.29 euros/tonne.
