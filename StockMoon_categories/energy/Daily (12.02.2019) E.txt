Daily (12.02.2019) European power prices for 2020 delivery tumbled on Monday tracking weaker coal and carbon markets 
12022019

1139
DailyUncertainty about the restart of the trade talks between U.S. and China outweighed the support from OPEC-led supply cuts on Monday. A strengthening dollar put more pressure on prices. Consequently, oil prices retreated, with Brent oil trading nearly 1% down at $61.51 a barrel. WTI futures edged 0.6% lower at $52.41 a barrel.Most of the British wholesale gas prices declined on Monday due to mild weather, ample LNG supplies and increased Norwegian flows. NBP spot price decreased by 0.5% to 48.25 p/therm. On the curve, the NBP contract for delivery in Summer 2019 lost 0.2% ending at 45.78 p/therm.
 
A steep drop in wind power generation coupled with a boost in heating demand due to falling temperatures contributed to a rise in European spot power prices on Monday. The German day-ahead power price skyrocketed by 586.3% to 46.74 p/therm. The equivalent French contract soared by 52.1% to 51.39 p/therm.
 
Further on the curve, electricity prices dipped, tracking low coal, carbon and gas prices. The German power contract for delivery in 2020 was 1.7% down at 47.02 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract traded 1.6% lower at 49.73 EUR/MWh.
 
European carbon prices posted slight gains on Monday amid a weaker energy complex and moderate trading. The contract expiring in 2019 added 0.1% to settle at 22.40 EUR/tonne.
