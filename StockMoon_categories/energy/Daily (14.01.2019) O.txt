Daily (14.01.2019) Oil prices down by around 2% on Friday on concerns over global economic growth 
14012019

1157
DailyOil prices lost nearly 2% as the recent pessimistic economic data added to persisting worries about global economic growth. Nevertheless, oil markets posted gains on a weekly basis, due to OPEC-led output cuts and expectations for progress in U.S.-China trade talks. Brent crude fell by around 2% to $60.48 a barrel, while WTI futures settled about 2% down to $51.59 a barrel.British gas prices on the curve rose on Friday due to long-term forecasts for lower temperatures. The NBP contract for Q2 19 delivery ended 1.3% higher at 53.66 p/therm. However, additional LNG arrivals and flows from Norway weighed on the NBP day-ahead price, which closed 0.5% down at 57.90 p/therm.
 
European spot electricity prices plunged on Friday due to weaker demand in the region. The German spot power contract slumped by 18.3% to 40.80 EUR/MWh on stronger wind output. The equivalent French contract lost 18.1% to settle at 53.54 EUR/MWh, as comfortable thermal supply coincided with slowing demand.
 
Further on the curve, electricity prices edged higher, driven by rising prices on fuels and carbon markets. German power contract for delivery in 2020 closed 1.5% higher at 48.72 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French power contract traded 1.1% up at 52.01 EUR/MWh.
 
European carbon prices resumed gains on Friday following a recent sell-off auction. The 2020-expiry contract price soared by 2.7% to 23.21 euros a tonne.
