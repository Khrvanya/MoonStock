Daily (15.03.2018)  German day-ahead power price plunged by 47.3% on Wednesday as temperatures increased 
15032018

1413
DailyOil prices rose on Wednesday, despite bigger-than-expected U.S. crude stock build, being backed by the huge draw of fuel stockpiles.  
Brent crude ascended by $0.25, or 0.4%, to settle at $64.89 a barrel. U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude futures grew by 25 cents, or 0.4%, to $60.96 a barrel.
 
British wholesale gas prices slipped on Wednesday due to strong deliveries from Norway and forecasts for milder weather. NBP gas day-ahead price decreased by 7.20 pence, or about 11%, to end at 59.10 p/therm. April 2018 delivery contract traded around 6% lower at 49.54/therm.
 
European prompt electricity prices dropped on Wednesday due to higher temperatures forecast and increased renewable power availability. Germany's day-ahead power price tumbled by 47.3% to 24.63 euros a megawatt hour (MWh). The French equivalent contract ended 23.6% down at 36.31 euros.

European forward power prices slumped on Wednesday tracking coal, gas and carbon emission prices. The German electricity price for delivery in 2019, the European benchmark, dropped by 1.5% to 34.64 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract settled 0.8% down at 39.59 euros/MWh.

European carbon prices were bearish following Wednesday's auction. The carbon contract ending in 2019 dipped by 2% to 11.24 euros a tonne.
