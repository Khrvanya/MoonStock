Daily (15.12.2017) EU carbon contracts recovered slightly from two-month lows on Thursday 
15122017

1126
DailyOil prices surged on Thursday as a pipeline outage in Britain continued to support prices despite forecasts showing global crude surplus in the beginning of next year. Brent crude futures ended 1.4% higher at $63.31 a barrel. Meanwhile, U.S. West Texas Intermediate futures settled 44 cents, or 0.8 percent, to $57.04 a barrel.British prompt gas prices fell on Thursday, as supply worries reduced and robust imports as well as lower demand contributed to an oversupplied system. NBP day-ahead gas price declined by 6.75 pence, or 10%, to 60 p/therm. 
 
Along the forward curve, gas contracts traded higher. Q1 2018 delivery contract was 3.6% up at 61.06 p/therm, due to a pipeline outage in the UK. North Sea operator Ineos has declared force majeure on all shipments of crude oil, natural gas and condensates via its Forties pipeline system.
 
Europe's wholesale spot electricity prices were mixed on Thursday, with Germany responding firmer to a fall in wind power generation and France edging slightly down as higher demand was more than offset by operators' supply planning. German day-ahead baseload was up 49 percent at 46.26 euros/MWh, while the equivalent French contract shed 0.3 percent at 58.14 euros/MWh.

Along the forward curve, electricity contracts were off the new peaks attained earlier in the week. German 2018 delivery contract, the European benchmark, lost 0.7 percent to 37.67 euros/MWh. Meanwhile, the less liquid French power contract for year-ahead delivery fell 0.2 percent day-on-day to 43.70 euros.

European carbon prices firmed slightly on Thursday after purchasers have been attracted by a new two-month low. The contract expiring in 2018 increased by 0.3% to 7.13 euros a tonne.
