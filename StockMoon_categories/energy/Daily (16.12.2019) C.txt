Daily (16.12.2019) Crude oil surged by over 1.5% on Friday on U.S.-China trade hopes, UK election outcome 
16122019

1121
DailyCrude oil prices settled higher on Friday, rallying to their highest level since around three-months on Friday, buoyed by progress on trade between the U.S.-China and the outcome of the general election in Britain.Brent crude for February delivery advanced $1.02, or 1.6% to settle at $65.22 on the London-based ICE Futures Europe Exchange. WTI crude for January delivery rose 89 cents or, 1.5% to stand at $60.07 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange.
 
British prompt gas prices were on back foot on Friday, reversing previous gains on forecasts for milder weather and thus, weaker consumption. NBP spot tumbled by 7.2% to 31.00 p/therm. Forward prices also declined. The January 2020 gas dipped by 7.8 % to 35.17 p/therm, while the gas contract for year-ahead delivery plunged 6.2% at 35.31 p/therm.
 
European spot electricity prices fell on Friday, driven down by low consumption ahead of weekend. German spot price for day-ahead delivery plunged 36% to trade at 23.23 EUR/MWh, while the French equivalent contract lost 18% at 29.25 EUR/MWh.
 
The year-ahead contracts eased on Friday due to a milder weather outlook and weaker coal and gas prices. The price for the 2020-delivery German contract fell slightly by 0.1% to 43.20 EUR/MWh, while the one for the French equivalent contract slipped 0.8% to stand at 45.75 EUR/MWh.
 
European carbon prices lost ground on Friday as EU leaders have failed to achieve consensus on targeting net zero emissions by 2050, and realized that Poland, strongly dependent on coal, was unable to implement the target at this point. EUAs expiring in 2020 declined by 4.2% to 24.17 EUR/tonne.
