Daily (17.10.2018)  UK gas prices dropped on Tuesday due to an oversupplied market 
17102018

1104
BiofuelsGlobal oil prices were slightly up on Tuesday, as forecasts for U.S. supply growth were offset by concerns over the Middle East crude supply due to geopolitical tensions, and the approaching U.S. sanctions on Iranian exports. U.S. West Texas Intermediate futures edged 0.2% higher on Tuesday, to trade at $71.92 a barrel. Meanwhile, Brent crude oil futures rose by 0.8% to end at $81.41 a barrel.  British wholesale gas prices were mostly bearish on Tuesday in an oversupplied market, due to high outflows from liquefied natural gas (LNG) terminals and increased North Sea gas output. NBP spot price fell by 0.7% to close at 68.05 p/therm. Further along the curve, November 2018 delivery contract settled 2.3% lower at 70.60 p/therm.

European spot power prices diverged on Tuesday. The German electricity price for day-ahead delivery lost 1.5% to settle at 70.91 EUR/MWh, due to stable wind power generation and lower demand. On the other side, the French equivalent contract rose by 1% to end at 74.82 EUR/MWh, as EDF's Chooz 1 reactor will extend its outage by one day, set to reopen on October 30.

Along the forward electricity curve, prices fell slightly, tracking gains in European carbon market. The French Cal'19 contract edged 0.4% down to settle at 56.63 euros/MWh. Meanwhile, the German 2019-delivery contract ended slightly lower at 52.72 EUR/MWh. 
 
European carbon contracts prices gained momentum on Tuesday in a volatile session as the market fretted over the impact of UK's exclusion from the EU emissions trading. The contract expiring in 2021 gained 2.3% to settle at 21.34 euros a tonne.
