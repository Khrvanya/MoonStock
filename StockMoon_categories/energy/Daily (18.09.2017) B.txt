Daily (18.09.2017) British gas contracts dipped on Friday due to weaker energy complex 
18092017

1057
DailyOil prices were firm on Monday and stayed close to multi-month peaks hit late last week as the number of U.S. rigs drilling for new output fell and due to the restart of the refineries after Hurricane Harvey. The price for Brent crude slightly increased by 0.27% to settle at $55.62 a barrel. Meanwhile, U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude futures was constant at $49.89 barrel.
 
Wholesale gas prices in the United Kingdom had a decreasing trend on Friday, following the decline in other energy prices which put bearish pressure. UK day-ahead gas price fell 0.35 pence, or 0.7% to end at 47.00 p/therm. Further along the curve, October gas contract closed 3.2% down at 44.99 p/therm. 
 
European spot electricity prices for early next week delivery surged on Friday, boosted by an estimated decline in wind power generation and strong demand. The German baseload spot power contract for Monday delivery increased 5.7 percent to 37.03 euros/MWh compared with the price paid on Thursday for Friday delivery. The French equivalent contract slightly fell to 37.66 euros/MWh.
 
Along the forward curve, electricity prices cut previous gains, following the lower coal and carbon emissions prices. The German Cal '18, a benchmark for the European market, retreated from its 3-1/2 year high attained on Thursday, diminishing by 0.69 percent to 35.69 euros/MWh. The equivalent French contract dipped 1.9 percent to 41.896 euros/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices continued the decline in another choppy session on Friday, as purchasers emerged to cut substantial selling, while less support from electricity prices led to a weekly loss of 1.7% for EUAs. December 2018 expiry EU carbon allowances dropped by 2.2% to end at 6.97 euros a tonne.
