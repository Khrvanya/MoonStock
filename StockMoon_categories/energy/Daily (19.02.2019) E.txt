Daily (19.02.2019) EU carbon prices declined by 2% on Monday, tracking bearish gas markets 
19022019

1054
DailySupply cuts led by the OPEC and Russia have boosted oil prices on Monday. However, gains were limited by the announcement of weak car sales data in China. Brent crude edged 0.4% higher at 66.50 USD/bbl, whereas WTI futures rose by 0.9% to 56.06 USD/bblBritish spot gas price dropped on Monday, influenced by stable flows from Norway and ample gas amounts in storages that left the system oversupplied. As a result, NBP spot price decreased by 2.1% to 45.92 p/therm, whereas the Summer2019 contract traded 3% down at 44.91 p/therm.
 
European spot power prices soared on Monday, as demand is expected to hike. The German day-ahead power contract settled 5.3% higher at 41.31 EUR/MWh. At the same time, the equivalent French contract price soared by 6.8% to 47.02 EUR/MWh, influenced by weak nuclear supply
 
Along the curve, electricity prices posted losses tracking lower gas, coal and carbon prices. The German power contract for delivery in 2020 ended 0.8% down at 46.11 EUR/MWh. The equivalent French contract edged 0.7% lower at 49.45 EUR/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices lost ground on Monday, influenced by the bearish trend in gas prices. The price for the 2020-expiry contract dropped by 2.1% to 20.41 EUR/tonne.
