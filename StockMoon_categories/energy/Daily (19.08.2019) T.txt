Daily (19.08.2019) The German spot power price tumbled by 27% due to lower demand 
19082019

1133
DailyOil prices rebounded from previous losses on Friday asfurther support from central banks might alleviate recession worries. Brent crude rose $0.41, or 0.7% to $58.64 a barrel. WTI futures added $0.40, or 0.7% to $54.87 a barrel. British wholesale gas prices had a downward trend on Friday due to an oversupplied system. NBP day-ahead gas price slipped by 0.1 pence, or 0.4%, to end at 26.40 p/therm. Meanwhile, the Winter 2020 delivery contract edged 0.6% lower at 44.39 p/therm.
 
European power prices were bearish on Friday due to weaker demand. German base spot electricty price sinked by 27% to 26.22 euros/MWh. The French baseload power contract settled 23% lower at 24.13 euros/MWh.
 
Further along the curve, electricity prices fell on Friday due to a decline in carbon and gas contracts. The German year-ahead power contract closed 0.3% down at 48.48 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract ended 1.3% lower at 50.26 euros/MWh.
 
European carbon prices extended losses on Friday trading below the EUR26 level. The carbon contract expiring in 2020 ended 0.1% lower at 26.23 euros/tonne. The 2021-expiry contract closed 0.2% lower at 26.67 euros/tonne.
