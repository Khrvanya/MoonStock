Daily (19.12.2017) European carbon prices soared by over 3% on Monday 
19122017

1123
DailyBenchmark Brent crude prices edged higher on Monday, buyout by a North Sea pipeline outage and a workers' strike in the Nigerian energy industry, but U.S. prices slid slightly, highlighting worries about increasing U.S. production. Brent crude futures traded 18 cents, or 0.3%, up at $63.41. U.S. crude futures fell 14 cents to $57.16.British wholesale gas prices dropped on Monday as forecasters predicted milder weather and more nuclear power supply was made available. NBP day-ahead gas price decreased by 0.90 pence, or 1.5%, to 60.20 p/therm. The January contract traded 1.45 pence, or 2.4%, down at 59.92 p/therm. 

European spot electricity prices for day-ahead delivery soared on Monday, supported by forecasts for a sharp decline in wind power generation, while power consumption was likely to rise. 

German baseload contract for Tuesday delivery gained 23.23 euros to 60.67 euros/MWh compared with the price paid on Friday for Monday delivery. The equivalent French contract added 8.64 euros to 64.39 euros/MWh compared with the Monday delivery contract.

Along the forward curve, electricity contracts fell. German year-ahead baseload, the European benchmark, edged lower by 0.2 percent to 38.18 euros/MWh. The French 2018 delivery contract traded 0.4 percent down at 44.06 euros/MWh.

European carbon prices surged by over 3% on Monday, as a weak final 2017 auction was dominated by short-covering before the expiration of the benchmark futures. Emissions allowances expiring in 2021 settled at 7.75 euros a tonne.
