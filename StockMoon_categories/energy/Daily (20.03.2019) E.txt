Daily (20.03.2019) EU carbon prices fell by more than 3% on Tuesday, due to a bearish energy complex and continued Brexit uncertainty 
20032019

1012
DailyCrude prices diverged slightly on Tuesday. On one side, prices were supported by OPEC-led supply cuts OPEC. On the other side, rising U.S. output put pressure on the market. Brent crude edged 0.1% higher at $67.61 a barrel, while WTI crude traded 0.1% lower at $59.03 a barrel.British gas prices were bearish on Tuesday, as strong LNG imports offset an unplanned outage in the system that reduced flows from Norway. NBP spot fell by 0.9% to 37.75 p/therm, while the contract for delivery in Summer 2019 lost 0.6% to close at 37.70 p/therm.
 
European day-ahead power prices calmed on Tuesday on rising wind and solar power output, as well as lower demand due to milder weather. The price for the German day-ahead power contract fell by 8.5% to 41.50 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract traded 7.4% lower at 41.96 EUR/MWh.
 
Along the curve, electricity prices firmed, tracking higher coal prices. The price for the German power contract for delivery in 2020 nudged up by 0.6% to 46.05 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract added 0.8% to close at 49.32 EUR/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices slumped on Tuesday, amid a bearish energy market and continued Brexit uncertainty. The price for the 2020-expiry contract traded 3.2% lower at 21.48 EUR/tonne.
