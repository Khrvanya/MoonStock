Daily (21.01.2020) Crude oil hits the highest level in a week due to supply disruption in Libya 
21012020

1155
DailyCrude oil prices climbed to their highest level in more than a week on Monday, due to a halt in the crude production at two large Libyan oilfields after forces loyal to commander Khalifa Haftar closed a pipeline, bringing back the focus on supply risks.Brent crude for March delivery soared 35 cents, or 0.5%, to settle at $65.20 a barrel on ICE London. U.S. WTI crude for February delivery increased as much as 12 cents, or 0.2%, to end at $58.76 a barrel on NYMEX.
 
Robust LNG injections into Britain gas market weighed on UK's spot gas price on Monday. NBP gas for day-ahead delivery declined by 3.5% to end at 27.70 p/therm.
Along the forward curve, prices were also bearish, due to a supply glut. The gas price for March delivery shed 3.3% at 26.57 p/therm, while the gas price for year-ahead delivery dropped by 2.3% at 38.60 p/therm.
 
European spot electricity prices skyrocketed on Monday, spurred by a higher demand. German day-ahead power jumped by 31% to trade at 41.60 EUR/MWh, while the French equivalent contract rallied 31.3% at 46.85 EUR/MWh.
Year-ahead contracts lost ground, following weaker gas, carbon prices. German year-ahead power decreased by 1.8% to end at 43.15 EUR/MWh, while the same contract in France shed 1% at 44.91 EUR.
 
EUAs expiring in 2021 tumbled by 1% to stand at 25.38 EUR/tonne, due to a weaker auction demand and a bearish energy complex.
