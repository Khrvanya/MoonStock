Daily (21.02.2020) German spot power down 15% on Thursday due to higher wind output, lower demand 
21022020

1013
DailyCrude oil futures edged higher on Thursday after an EIA report showed U.S. crude inventories rose less than expected last week. However,  gains were capped by worries about the outbreak of coronavirus outside China.Brent crude for April delivery rose by 19 cents, or 0.32%, to settle at $59.31 a barrel. U.S. WTI crude increased by 1.1% to close at $53.88 a barrel on NYMEX.

A sluggish demand due to warmer weather led to a supply glut, weighing on the British spot gas price on Thursday. NBP spot dipped by 7.8% to 23.40 p/therm.
 
Along the forward curve, prices also decreased, tracking a bearish energy complex. The gas price for the Q2 2020 delivery shed 0.3% to 23.29 p/therm, while the gas price for year-ahead delivery decreased by 0.9% to 36.98 p/therm.

European spot electricity prices ended on a mixed note on Thursday, with German prices declining on higher wind power output and lower demand. Meanwhile, in France prices firmed on stronger demand and falling wind power output.
 
German day-ahead power price plunged by 15.2% to 24.33 EUR/MWh, while the price for the French equivalent contract surged by 1.6% to 30.59 EUR/MWh.

 
Year-ahead power contracts slipped on Thursday, tracking lower coal and carbon prices. German Cal'21 power contract lost 0.4% at 42.98 EUR/MWh, while the similar contract in France shed 0.5% to end at 44.25 EUR/MWh.
 
The carbon prices moved downwards on Thursday on reduced concerns about the coronavirus spread. EUAs expiring in 2021 declined by 0.3% to 25.83 EUR/tonne.
