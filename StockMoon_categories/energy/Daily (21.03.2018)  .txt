Daily (21.03.2018)  EUAs rebounded by 4.3% to hit a six-year high on Tuesday due to a strong auction 
21032018

1143
DailyOil prices hiked by more than 2% on Tuesday as tensions in the Middle East deepened and due to a potential fall in Venezuelan oil output.  
Brent crude soared by $1.37, or 2.1%, to settle at $67.42 a barrel. U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude futures rose by $1.48, or 2.4%, to $63.54 a barrel.
    
British wholesale gas prices jumped on Tuesday due to a decrease in supply from both the IUK interconnector from Belgium and the BBL Interconnector from the Netherlands, and the failure along the Vesterled pipeline which reduced imports from Norway. NBP day-ahead price surged by 2.15 pence, or 4 percent, to close at 56.65 p/therm. April 2018 delivery contract ended 2.3% higher at 51.02 p/therm.

European prompt electricity prices rose on Tuesday on forecasts for lower temperatures and increased demand. German baseload contract for day-ahead delivery hiked by 21% to 51.83 euros a megawatt hour (MWh). French spot price gained 13% to settle at 55.02 euros.
 
Year-ahead power curve prices went slightly up. Germany's Cal '19 baseload contract for 2019 delivery, the European benchmark, was up by 2 percent at 34.82 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract added 1.7 percent to reach at 40.07 euros/MWh.

EUAs hit a new six-year peak on Tuesday following a strong auction which attracted a huge number of bidders. The carbon contract expiring in 2021 closed 4.3% higher at 11.86 euros a tonne.
