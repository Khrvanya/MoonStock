Daily (22.01.2018) EU carbon prices hit a 5-year high on Friday, posting a 11.2% gain for the week 
22012018

1037
DailyOil prices slumped on Friday and broke a four-week winning streak after a rally that had taken benchmarks to three-year peaks, as investors sold positions on re-emerging U.S. output worries.Brent crude futures dropped by 70 cents, or 1 percent, to settle at $68.61 a barrel after hitting a session low of $68.28. Meanwhile, U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude futures closed at $63.37 a barrel, down 58 cents, or 0.9 percent. 
 
Most of the British wholesale gas prices fell on Friday, due to milder weather forecasts. NBP day-ahead gas price decreased by 0.75 pence, or 1.5%, to end at 49.25 p/therm. Meanwhile, contracts on the curve were almost constant. February delivery contract traded slightly higher at 50.13 p/therm.
 
European electricity prices for prompt delivery fell on Friday as milder weather predictions led to lower demand, while more thermal capacities were being prepared. German baseload for Monday delivery, at 35.87 euros/MWh, was 11.4 percent down from the Friday delivery price. The equivalent French contract, at 36.85 euros, was off by 9.4 percent.

Along the power curve, Germany's Cal '19 baseload contract, the European benchmark, rose 28 cents to 35.55 euros/MWh. The equivalent French contract was 21 cents up from its close at 40.39 euros/MWh close.

EU carbon prices reached their highest level in five years on Friday, though observers continued to question if the gains were maintainable amid bearish indicators and still distant market reforms. The carbon contract expiring in 2021 closed 3.2% up at 9.09 euros a tonne.
