Daily (22.11.2017) EU carbon contracts posted 0.7% losses on Tuesday 
22112017

1115
DailyOil prices surged on Tuesday, buyout by expectations of an extension next week to OPEC production cuts, but were under pressure from signs of increased output in the United States. Brent crude traded 35 cents, or 0.6%, higher at $62.57 a barrel. U.S. light crude was at $56.83, up 41 cents. Analysts declared Brent was likely to vary in a narrow range, from $61 to $63, as the market awaited the outcome of the Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries' meeting on November 30.
 
British wholesale gas prices continued the bullish trend on Tuesday, supported by outages in the UK Continental Shelf gas production. The day-ahead gas price soared by 1.2% to 53.25 p/therm. Along the forward curve, next quarter delivery contract traded 1.4% up at 55.34 p/therm.

European spot electricity prices for day-ahead delivery slumped on Tuesday on predictions of a sharp increase in power generation from wind turbines and a decline in consumption because of milder weather. German baseload spot electricity for Wednesday delivery plunged over 35 percent, or 17.58 euros, to 31.72 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract fell 2.7% to 59.43 euros/MWh.

Along the year-ahead curve, power prices edged higher on Tuesday following gains in fuels. Germany's electricity contract for delivery in 2018 was up 0.3 percent at 36.13 euros/MWh, while the same French contract added 0.3 percent to 41.61 euros/MWh.

EU carbon prices extended losses on Tuesday as news that an important ETS reform vote may still be hanging in the balance contributed to a late sell-off. The carbon contract expiring in 2018 settled 0.7% lower at 7.42 euros a tonne.
