Daily (23.02.2018) WTI crude soared by 1.8% to two-week peaks on Thursday after an unpredicted draw in U.S crude stocks 
23022018

1121
DailyOil prices surged to two-week peaks on Thursday following data showing an unexpected draw in U.S. crude stocks. Brent crude added 97 cents, or 1.5 percent to end at $66.39 a barrel. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) futures jumped by 1.8 percent to close at $62.77 a barrel. British prompt gas prices hiked on Thursday as forecasts for cooler temperatures boosted demand. UK day-ahead gas price increased by 3.1% to settle at 61.37 p/therm. The Q2 2018 delivery contract ended insignificantly higher at 44.55 p/therm.

German electricity prompt price declined on Thursday on forecasts for increased wind power supply. German baseload contract for day-ahead delivery closed 6.6% lower at 44.27 euros/MWh. French spot price edged 0.3% higher at 52.12 euros/MWh, on colder weather predictions.

Further along the curve, electricity contracts have slightly changed. German Cal 19' European benchmark ended 0.1% up at 34.65 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract was down by 0.1% at 39.73 euros/MWh.

EU carbon prices recovered from a one-week low on Thursday ahead of an average government auction and despite analysts' concerns of a correction. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 traded 1.7% higher at 9.78 euros/tonne.
