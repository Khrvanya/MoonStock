Daily (23.05.2018) European carbon prices soared by 2.4% on Tuesday to hit new seven-year peaks 
23052018

1123
DailyOil prices were mixed on Tuesday. Brent futures rose close to $80 a barrel level on Tuesday, adding $0.35, or 0.4%, on concerns that decreasing Venezuelan crude output and a potential decline in Iranian exports could further reduce global supply. Brent crude settled at $79.57 a barrel. WTI futures slightly fell due to expectations that OPEC may raise supplies as early as June, to settle at $72.20 a barrel.British wholesale gas prices surged on Tuesday, before the scheduled closure of a major Norwegian supply route this week and potential higher exports to Europe until mid-June. UK day-ahead gas price gained 0.80 pence, or 1.4%, to end at 57.80 p/therm. Meanwhile, June 2018 delivery contract closed 3.2% higher at 58.31 p/therm.

European prompt electricity prices dropped on Tuesday due to strong solar supply. German day-ahead power price was 1% down at 42.33 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract ended 5.1% lower at 44.01 euros/MWh.

Along the forward curve, prices soared driven by gains in fuels and carbon emissions markets. German Cal 19' European benchmark settled 2.4% higher at 43.20 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract closed 2.1% up at 48.00 euros/MWh.

European carbon prices prolonged their bullish evolution to new seven-year peaks on Tuesday, exceeding the EUR16 level for the first time since June 2011 on a strong auction. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 closed 0.3% up at 16.12 euros/tonne.
