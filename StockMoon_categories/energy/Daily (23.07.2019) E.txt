Daily (23.07.2019) European spot power prices soared by over 32% on Monday, due to an expected heatwave in the region 
23072019

0922
DailyCrude oil prices strengthened on Monday on concerns for possible supply disruptions in the Middle East after Iran captured a British tanker last Friday. As a result, Brent crude rose by 1.3% to $63.26 a barrel, while WTI futures added 1% to settle at $56.22 a barrel.British gas prices fell on Monday, as lower gas-for-powerdemand due to high wind power generation led to an oversupplied system. As a result, NBP day-ahead plunged by 4.4% to 27.50 p/therm, while the contract for Q2 2020 delivery closed 1.6% down at 46.96 p/therm.
 
European prompt power prices soared on Monday, due to warmer weather in the region as air conditioning demand picked up. As a result, the price for the German day-ahead power contract surged by 32.8% to 46.60 EUR/MWh. At the same time, the equivalent French contract settled 39% higher at 46.07 EUR/MWh, on tighter nuclear availability.
 
Forward electricity prices added slight gains, moving in tandem with higher carbon prices. The price for the German power contract for delivery in 2020 edged 0.1% up at 52.66 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract traded 0.4% higher at 54.56 EUR/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices were in a bullish mood on Monday for the fourth consecutive day, resisting to stay close to the 29 euro/tonne mark. Hence, the price for the contract expiring in 2020 rose by 0.3% to 29.39 EUR/tonne.
