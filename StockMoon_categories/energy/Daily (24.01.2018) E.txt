Daily (24.01.2018) EU carbon prices jumped by almost 3% to hit more than 5-year high on Tuesday 
24012018

1054
DailyOil prices increased on Tuesday, buyout by strong world economic growth prospects and forecasts for continued output reductions by OPEC, Russia and their allies.Brent crude settled 1.4% higher at $69.96 a barrel. Meanwhile, U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude futures settled 90 cents up at $64.47 a barrel, for a gain of 1.4 percent. 
 
British wholesale gas prices fell on Tuesday in reply to near-term oversupply because of lower demand. NBP day-ahead gas price dropped by 0.75 pence, or 1.5%, to 48.55 p/therm. Gas contract for Summer 2018 delivery traded down 0.50 pence, or 1.2%, at 43.1 p/therm.
 
European spot electricity prices for day-ahead delivery tumbled in the wholesale market on Tuesday on an expected increase in wind power generation, while consumption was likely to fall. The German baseload power price for Wednesday delivery slumped by 13.4 euros, or 36%, to 23.88 euros/MWh. The equivalent French contract declined by 8.59 euros, or 22.4%, to 29.78 euros/MWh.

Further along the power curve, Germany's Cal '19 baseload contract, the European benchmark, dipped 0.1 percent to 35.63 euros/MWh. The less-liquid French contract lost 15 cents, or 0.4%, to 40.40 euros/MWh.

EU carbon prices continued their strong start to 2018 by prolonging the previous session's five-year peak and trading above EUR9 for the first time since November 2012. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 closed almost 3% up at 9.07 euros a tonne.
