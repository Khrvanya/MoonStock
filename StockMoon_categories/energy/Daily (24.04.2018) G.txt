Daily (24.04.2018) German spot power contract traded 14.4% higher on Monday due to reduced wind generation 
24042018

1124
DailyOil prices soared on Monday to reach new 3.5-year peaks, after Iran tempered hopes that OPEC would extend its production cut agreement and on worries that U.S. sanctions could limit Iran's output. Brent crude added $0.65, or 0.9%, to $74.71 a barrel. WTI futures edged 0.4% higher at $68.64 a barrel.British wholesale gas prices rose on Monday on forecasts for colder weather this week. NBP day-ahead price surged by 0.40 pence, or 0.8%, to end at 51.00 p/therm. Meanwhile, May 2018 delivery contract closed 0.6% higher at 51.62 p/therm.

European prompt electricity prices hiked on Monday due to lower-than-expected German wind output and cooler weather. German power spot price soared by 14.4% to end at 34.32 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract traded 42% higher at 35.62 euros/MWh.

Further along the curve, German Cal 19' European benchmark gained 0.3% to settle at 38.06 euros/MWh. Meanwhile, the French equivalent contract was almost flat at 42.88 euros/MWh.

EU carbon prices dropped for the third consecutive session on Monday ending close to EUR13 level, as observers predicted further losses. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 settled 1.2% lower at 13.04 euros/tonne.
