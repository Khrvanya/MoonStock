Daily (24.12.2019) HO-HO-HO! UK spot gas down 9% on Christmas Eve, after Russia-Ukraine transit gas deal 
24122019

1149
DailyOil prices settled slightly higher on Monday, due to thin trading despite a report indicating that a producer group led by the OPEC may consider easing output restrictions.Brent crude for February delivery gained 25 cents, or 0.4%, to settle at $66.39 a barrel on ICE Futures Europe. WTI crude rose by 8 cents, or 0.1%, to close at $60.52 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange.

British prompt gas price tumbled on Monday after a new transit deal between Russia and Ukraine, under which Moscow will provide gas to Europe for at least five years via Ukraine. An oversupplied British market also weighed on gas prices. NBP spot gas contract fell 9% to 29.25 p/therm.
 
Forward contracts also lost ground, with gas for delivery in January down 7.9% at 34.20 p/therm, while gas for delivery in Summer 2020 was traded 6.4% lower at 32.38 p/therm.
 
European spot electricity prices diverged on Monday, with German power down due to lower electricity consumption ahead of Christmas.  German power for Tuesday delivery was 27% down at 20.52 EUR/MWh, while the French equivalent contract rose 3.4% to 20.70 EUR/MWh.

In terms of curve contracts, prices declined on Monday. Germany's Cal '20 delivery contract lost 1.9% at 42.20 EUR/MWh, while the French equivalent contract shed 2.4% at 44.59 EUR/MWh.
 
European CO2 emission allowances declined on Monday in thin trading ahead of Christmas holiday. EUAs for December 2020 delivery decreased by 0.5% to end at 26.54 EUR/tonne.
