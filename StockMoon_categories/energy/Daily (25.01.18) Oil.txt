Daily (25.01.18) Oil prices rose 0.8% after record 10th straight weekly decline in U.S. inventories 
25012018

1148
DailyOil prices were boosted by robust volumes on Wednesday, after the record 10th consecutive weekly decline in U.S. crude inventories, even though reduced refining activity and increasing output signaled U.S. stocks might surge. Brent crude rose by 57 cents, or 0.8%, to settle at $70.53 per barrel. U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) futures increased by 1.7 percent to $65.61 a barrel.British wholesale gas prices declined on Wednesday, due to predictions of strong production from the country's wind farms, while higher temperatures reduced demand. NBP day-ahead gas price closed 1.7% down at 47.72 p/therm. Meanwhile, Summer 2018 delivery contract traded 249% lower at 42.08 p/therm.
 
European spot electricity prices for day-ahead delivery jumped on Wednesday on forecasts for a decline in power generation from renewable wind turbines. The German day-ahead power price for Thursday delivery rose 38.1% to attain 32.97 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract ended 15.45% higher at 34.38 euros/MWh.
 
Further ahead on the power curve, Germany's electricity baseload contract for 2019 delivery, the European benchmark, fell 0.6% to 35.54 euros/MWh. The less-liquid French contract settled at 40.21 euros/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices continued to increase on Wednesday to hit a six-year peak, on gains that many attribute to speculators, several brand new to the market. The carbon contract expiring in 2021 closed almost 5% higher at 9.82 euros a tonne.
