Daily (26.02.2020) Oil prices at their lowest level in two weeks as the coronavirus outbreak hits demand expectations 
26022020

1045
DailyCrude oil futures fell sharply on Tuesday, under pressure of the accelerating spread of the coronavirus outside China, which overshadowed OPEC output reductions and supply disruptions in Libya.Brent crude for April delivery declined by $1.35, or 2.4%, to settle at $54.95 a barrel, the lowest settlement since Feb. 11. U.S. WTI crude slipped by $1.53, or 3%, to close at $49.90 a barrel on the NYMEX, the lowest settlement since Feb.10.

British gas prices were on a mixed note on Tuesday, with the spot price edging higher due to a colder weather forecast.
NBP spot surged by 0.8% to 24.75 p/therm.
 
Along the forward curve, prices tumbled due to a bearish energy complex, with the gas price for Summer 2020 trading 1.9% lower at 22.27 p/therm, while the gas for year-ahead delivery fell by 1% to 34.66 p/therm.
 
European spot electricity prices soared on Tuesday due to a considerable decline in wind power output in Germany and lower nuclear availability in France.
 
The German day-ahead power price rallied by 31.4% to 31.67 EUR/MWh, while the French equivalent contract soared by 17% to 31.00 EUR/MWh.
 
Year-ahead power contracts lost ground following weaker oil, gas, and carbon prices. The German Cal'21 power contract dropped by 0.6% to 41.27 EUR/MWh, while the same contract in France shed 0.4% to 42.80 EUR/MWh.
 
The carbon prices extended losses on Tuesday as the spread of the COVID-19 virus weighed on the market sentiment. EUAs expiring in 2021 shed 1.6% to 24.38 EUR/tonne.
