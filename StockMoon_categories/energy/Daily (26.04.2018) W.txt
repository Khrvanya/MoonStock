Daily (26.04.2018) WTI crude ended 0.5% higher on Wednesday due to geopolitical tensions 
26042018

1108
DailyOil prices surged on Wednesday near three-year highs hit in the previous session on geopolitical worries and potential new sanctions against Iran. Brent crude added $0.14, or 0.2%, to close at $74.00 a barrel. WTI futures edged 0.5% higher at $68.05 a barrel.British wholesale gas prices hiked on Wednesday due to an undersupplied system. UK day-ahead gas price gained 0.9 pence, or 1.8%, to end at 51.70 p/therm. Meanwhile, Winter 2018 delivery contract settled 0.3% higher at 56.94 p/therm.

European spot electricity prices for day-ahead delivery soared on Wednesday, bolstered by forecasts for a higher demand due to cold weather, and a decline in wind output. German spot power price jumped by 11.6% to close at 30.45 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract was 10.6% up at 31.49 euros/MWh.

Further along the curve. German Cal 19' European benchmark traded slightly higher at 38.10 euros/MWh. Meanwhile, the French equivalent contract ended 0.1% up at 42.83 euros/MWh. 

EU carbon prices rose to their highest peaks so far this week as recent auction weakness seemed to diminish, following several bearish sales. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 closed 0.2% higher at 13.42 euros/tonne.
