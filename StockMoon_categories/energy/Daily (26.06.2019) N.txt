Daily (26.06.2019) NBP day-ahead plunged by 7% on Tuesday due to warmer temperatures 
26062019

1015
DailyCrude oil prices were mixed on Tuesday.  Brent crude edged 0.3% higher at $65.05 a barrel on expectations for declining U.S. crude stocks. Meanwhile, WTI futures lost 0.1% to settle at $57.83 a barrel as U.S.-China trade tensions could lead to lower global fuel demand.British gas prices dropped on Tuesday, amidst warmer weather. NBP day-ahead plunged by 7% to 25.20 p/therm, while the contract for Summer 20 delivery closed 1.4% down at 44.96 p/therm.
 
European day-ahead power prices lost ground on Tuesday on forecasts for a rise in wind output in both Germany and France. The price for the German spot electricity contract decreased by 4.8% to 38.56 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract settled 12.6% lower at 29.33 EUR/MWh.
 
Forward electricity prices were rather constant, tracking mixed signals from gas, oil and carbon prices. The price for the German power contract for delivery in 2020 rose by 0.1% to 49.06 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract traded 0.1% down at 51.86 EUR/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices changed slightly on Tuesday, as the first cancelled auction in 10 months calmed the bullish trend. The price for the contract expiring in 2019 inched 0.2% up at 26.41 EUR/tonne.
