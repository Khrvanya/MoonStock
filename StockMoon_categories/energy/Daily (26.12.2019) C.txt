Daily (26.12.2019) Crude oil prices soared in pre-Christmas trade on supply cuts commitments 
26122019

1008
DailyCrude oil prices soared on Tuesday in thin pre-Christmas trade on Russia's pledge to honor production cuts promised to OPEC and after U.S. President Donald Trump reiterated the conclusion of the first phase of the U.S.-China trade deal.Brent crude rose by 81 cents, or 1.2%, to settle at $67.20 a barrel after a shortened session ahead of the Christmas holiday. U.S. West Texas Intermediate firmed 59 cents, or around 1%, to close at $61.11 a barrel.

A weaker demand outlook, expectations for an oversupplied system and high gas storage levels in Europe put pressure on British prompt gas price. NBP spot gas price fell by 6.6% to 27.33 p/therm on Tuesday.
 
Along the forward curve, prices also weakened. The gas contract for February 2020 delivery shed 2.5% to trade at 33.85 p/therm, while the gas for year-ahead delivery slipped 2.5% at 34.69 p/therm.
 
European spot power prices diverged on Tuesday. German spot contract for Thursday delivery, the next trading day after Christmas, rocketed 33% at 20.52 EUR/MWh, supported by a forecast for a sharp decline in wind generation. Meanwhile, the French equivalent contract dipped 30% at 14.52 EUR/MWh, due to a lower demand.

European carbon prices declined on Tuesday in a thin trade ahead of the Christmas holiday.  EUAs expiring in 2020 lost 1.7% to settle at 26.08 EUR/tonne.
