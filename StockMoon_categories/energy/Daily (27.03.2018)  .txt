Daily (27.03.2018)  NBP day-ahead gas price dropped by 6.4% due to milder weather 
27032018

1104
DailyOil prices edged lower on Monday as investors obtained some profits from last week's hike, but worries about Saudi-Iran tensions kept losses under control. 
Brent crude slipped by $0.33, or 0.5%, to settle at $70.12 a barrel. U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude futures settled $0.33, or 0.5%, down at $65.55 a barrel.
 
British wholesale gas prices dropped on Monday due to higher-than-expected temperatures and low demand. NBP day-ahead gas price tumbled by 3.30 pence, or 6.4 percent, to close at 48.20 p/therm. Further along the curve, Summer 2018 delivery contract traded 1.2% lower at 44.84 p/therm.
 
European prompt electricity prices surged on Monday tracking carbon evolution. German spot power price jumped by 8.9% to 45.07 euros/MWh, while the French equivalent contract ended 1.5% higher at 46.30 euros/MWh.

Year-ahead power prices surged on Monday supported by forecasts for a rise in electricity generation from renewable wind turbines. Germany's Cal '19 baseload contract, the European benchmark, slid by 0.3% to 35.53 euros/MWh. French equivalent contract lost 0.4% to end at 40.35 euros/MWh.
 
EUAs continued their bullish trend to reach a fresh 6.5-year peak on Monday as observers eyed further gains ahead of auction supply constriction due to the Easter holiday. The carbon contract expiring in 2021 closed 3% higher at 13.36 euros a tonne.
