Daily (27.09.2018)  NBP gas contracts lost around 3% on Wednesday, due to warmer weather and an oversupplied system 
27092018

1043
DailyOil crude prices lost momentum on Wednesday after U.S. data revealed higher-than-expected domestic crude inventories. Moreover, the U.S. officials promised investors that the oil market will not experience supply shortages and Trump has once again urged OPEC to pump more oil in his speech at the United Nations on Tuesday. Nevertheless, a substantial drop in Iranian exports kept Brent price above $80. 
U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude futures plunged by 1% down, or 71 cents, to settle at $71.57 a barrel. Meanwhile, Brent crude edged 0.7% down at $81.34 a barrel. 
British wholesale gas prices were strongly bearish on Wednesday, as higher temperatures capped demand for gas, leaving the market oversupplied. NBP spot price tumbled by 3.9% to end at 74.90 p/therm. Further along the curve, Winter 2018 delivery contract closed 3.2% down at 79.41 p/therm.

European spot power prices rose on Wednesday on forecasts for a decline in wind generation in Germany and increased consumption in France. The electricity price for day-ahead delivery jumped by 18.8% to 53.89 EUR/MWh in Germany. Meanwhile, the equivalent contract in France gained 1.3% to end at 64.44 EUR/MWh.

Along the forward electricity curve, contracts lost ground on Wednesday, tracking the downward evolution in gas and carbon markets. The German baseload 2019-delivery contract traded 2.4 % lower at 53.66 EUR/MWh. Meanwhile, the French equivalent contract settled at 59.25 EUR/MWh, posting a 2.6 % loss.

European carbon contracts dipped by a euro on Wednesday, as Poland's spot auction cleared at a significant discount to the secondary market. The price for the contract expiring in 2019 was 4.5 % lower at 20.84 euros a tonne.
