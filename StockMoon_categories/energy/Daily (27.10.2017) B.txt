Daily (27.10.2017) Brent crude price hit a 27-month peak on Thursday 
27102017

1103
DailyBrent crude settled at a 27-month peak on Thursday as the market concentrated more on comments from Saudi Arabia about stopping a global supply glut instead of an unpredicted rise in U.S. crude inventories, increased U.S. output and exports. Brent futures surged 86 cents, or 1.5 percent, to close at $59.30 a barrel, its highest close since July 3, 2015. Meanwhile, U.S. West Texas Intermediate crude, soared 46 cents, or 0.9 percent, to end at a six-month peak of $52.64, its highest close since April 17.

British wholesale gas prices fell on Wednesday due to an oversupplied market. The day-ahead gas price dropped by 1% to settle at 46.10 p/therm. Further along the curve, the NBP gas contract for delivery in Summer 2018 traded 0.2% down at 42.25 p/therm.
 
German wholesale electricity prices were dragged down on Thursday by forecasts for huge wind power volumes and declining demand before the weekend and a public holiday on October 31. The price of German baseload power for Friday slumped by 24.7 percent to 30.45 euros/MWh. The equivalent French contract was 2.9 percent down at 55.25 euros.

Along the forward electricity curve, German baseload power for delivery in 2018, the European benchmark, traded at 36.31 euros/MWh, or down 0.6 percent. The equivalent French year-ahead electricity contract lost 0.2 percent to 41.78 euros.

European carbon prices continued to decline on Thursday, falling to a two-week low after a key technical support was breached. December 2018 expiry EU carbon permits stood 2.7 percent lower at 7.23 euros a tonne.
