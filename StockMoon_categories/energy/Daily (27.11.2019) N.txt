Daily (27.11.2019) NBP spot tumbled by 5% on Tuesday due to an oversupplied system 
27112019

1021
DailyCrude oil prices traded higher on Tuesday as top U.S. and Chinese trade negotiators made efforts to reach a so-called Phase 1 deal in a trade war that has dragged on for 16 months.  Consequently, Brent crude price increased by 0.6 cents or almost 1% to end at $64.27 a barrel. The WTI contract added 0.4 cents, or 0.7%, to close at $58.41. 
 
An oversupplied system in the UK due to robust flows from Norway and expectations for more LNG cargoes at the beginning of December dragged down British wholesale gas prices on Tuesday. The NBP spot price tumbled by 4.8% to 40.72 p/therm. Forward contracts were also bearish. The gas contract for 2020 delivery ended 1.7% lower at 41.85 p/therm.
 
European spot electricity prices fell on Tuesday as power output from wind turbines is expected to hike, while consumption is expected to tumble due to milder weather. The German contract for Wednesday delivery dropped by 16.7% to 39.50 euros/MWh, while the equivalent French contract price slumped by 10.7% to 42.31 euros/MWh. 
 
Further along the curve, the year-ahead power contracts also posted losses on Tuesday, tracking lower coal, gas and carbon emissions prices. The German Cal '20 baseload fell 1.6% to 45.71 euros/MWh, while the French equivalent contract lost 1.4% at 48.43 euros/MWh.
 
The EUAs continued their downward trend on Tuesday following a weak auction. The carbon contract expiring in 2020  shed 0.2% to 24.53 EUR/tonne.
