Daily (28.05.2019) Brent crude climbed by 2.1% on Monday, due to ongoing tensions in the Middle East and OPEC-led supply cuts 
28052019

1019
DailyOil prices extended gains on Monday, supported by ongoing tensions in the Middle East and OPEC-led supply cuts. Brent crude surged by 2.1% to $70.11 a barrel, while WTI futures increased by 0.9% to $59.13 a barrel. 
British wholesale gas prices remained untraded on Monday, due to a national holiday in the UK.
 
European prompt power prices varied on Monday. The price for the German day-ahead power contract soared by 18% to 43.33 EUR/MWh, due to a sharp drop in renewables output and stronger demand. Meanwhile, the equivalent French contract traded 5.9% lower at 34.52 EUR/MWh, on rising wind power supply.
 
Along the curve, electricity prices added ground, influenced by higher carbon and oil prices. The price for the German power contract for delivery in 2020 nudged 0.5% higher at 48.77 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract closed 0.4% up at 51.68 EUR/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices added momentum on Monday, as the recent EU Parliament election results might lead to more ambitious climate policies. The price for the 2019-expiry contract traded  0.5% higher at 25.59 EUR/tonne.
