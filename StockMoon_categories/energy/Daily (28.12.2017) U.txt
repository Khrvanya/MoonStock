Daily (28.12.2017) UK gas price for delivery in January 2018 dipped on Wednesday due to forecasts of milder weather 
28122017

1033
DailyOil prices fell on Wednesday, as a rally ran out of momentum a session after crude attained a near 2-1/2-year peak on supply outages in Libya and the North Sea. Brent crude futures ended at $66.44 a barrel, down 0.9 percent, or 58 cents. U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude futures closed at $59.64 a barrel, down 33 cents, or 0.6 percent. 
 
British wholesale gas prices dropped on Wednesday because of higher temperatures. The January 2018 contract traded 1.88 pence, or 3.3%, down at 54.97 p/therm, on forecasts of milder weather and normal consumption. The Summer 2018 contract was down 0.28 pence at 44.50 p/therm. 
 
European spot electricity prices for day-ahead delivery surged on Wednesday due to an expected increase in demand in France following the Christmas holiday, while contracts in Germany declined on bearish weather forecasts. The German baseload electricity contract for Thursday delivery lost 2.68 euros to 32.49 euros/MWh, compared with the price paid on Friday, the last trading day before Christmas. The French spot price for Thursday delivery gained 8.5 euros to 46.75 euros/MWh, compared with the price paid last Friday for Tuesday delivery. 
 
Along the forward curve, contracts edged lower due to a mild weather outlook for early January. The German year-ahead contract, the European benchmark, decreased by 1 percent to 37.72 euros/MWh. The French year-ahead contract traded 0.5% down at 43.90 euros/MWh.
 
European carbon prices were almost unchanged on Wednesday, after having increased in a pre-Christmas rally that has added almost 20% to the price. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 ended at 8.23 euros a tonne.
