Daily (29.01.2018) EU carbon prices dropped by 1.6% on Friday 
29012018

1136
DailyOil prices closed higher on Friday after hitting three-year peaks, because of a weaker U.S. dollar. Brent crude increased by 0.1%, or 10 cents, to end at $70.52 per barrel. Meanwhile, WTI crude settled 1% up at $66.14 a barrel.Most of the British wholesale gas prices slumped on Friday due to high wind generation which limited demand from power-to-gas plants. The NBP spot price dipped by 1.4 percent, or 0.68 pence, to close at 47.15 p/therm. March 2018 delivery contract surged by 1 % to 48.05 p/therm, on colder weather forecasts.

European prompt power prices fell on Friday due to bearish fundamentals. Germany's baseload price for day-ahead delivery dropped by 27.3% to 32.81 euros a megawatt hour (MWh). French spot baseload contract settled down by 18.5% at 36.79 euros. 

Electricity prices on the curve rose due to forecasts for lower temperatures and plans for French nuclear outages. Germany's electricity price for 2019 delivery, the European benchmark, hiked by 0.3 percent to close at 35.71 euros/MWh. The same French contract gained 0.5 percent to end at 40.39 euros/MWh.

EU carbon prices declined by 1.6% on Friday after a rally that ended in EUAs hitting their maximum level in six years. The carbon contract expiring in 2021 traded at 9.47 euros a tonne, registering nevertheless a 3.9% weekly gain.
