Daily (29.11.2017) German spot power prices jumped by 91% on Tuesday due to forecasts for lower wind production and increased demand

DailyOil prices edged lower on Tuesday on predictions of higher output, although global markets were slightly better buyout by expectations an OPEC-led supply reduction would be prolonged. Brent crude futures settled 23 cents, or 0.4 percent, down at $63.61 a barrel. U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude futures were at $57.99 a barrel, down 12 cents, or 0.2 percent, from their last settlement.British wholesale gas prices mostly fell on Tuesday as warmer weather forecasts are likely to limit consumption and reduce strain on the system. Even though day-ahead gas price slightly rose to 57.60 p/therm, further out on the curve prices slumped. Summer 2017 delivery contract ended 1.3% down at 45.11 p/therm. 
 
German and French power prices for day-ahead delivery hiked on Tuesday on forecasts for lower German wind power production and higher demand, driven by falling temperatures. The German baseload electricity price for Wednesday delivery was up by 91 percent at 71.50 euros/MWh and the equivalent French price gained 18 percent to 77.50 euros/MWh.

Prices on the electricity curve declined, tracking fuel and carbon prices. Germany's benchmark power contract for 2018 delivery was down 0.9 percent at 36.61 euros/MWh, while the equivalent French contract shed 0.9 percent to 42.45 euros/MWh.

EU carbon prices lost ground on Tuesday as lawmaker approval of ETS reforms was pushed into next year, while gains in the dollar reduced margins for coal-fired electricity producers. The 2018 expiry contract ended 1.4% lower at 7.62 euros a tonne.
