Daily (30.01.2020) Carbon prices tumbled by around 2.6% on Wednesday due to a weak Polish auction 
30012020

1143
DailyCrude oil prices increased on Wednesday, due to a potential extension of crude output cuts by OPEC, which counterbalanced fears about the coronavirus spread.Brent crude rose by 30 cents, or 0.5%, to settle at $59.81 per barrel. U.S. WTI crude ended at $4.02, or 1% higher.
 
A milder weather outlook and strong wind power output weighed on UK's gas prices on Wednesday. The NBP day-ahead gas price plunged by 5.5% to end at 27.05 p/therm.
Along the forward curve, the gas price for Summer 2020 delivery decreased by 5% to 25.47 p/therm, while the gas for year-ahead delivery dropped by 2.8% to stand at 36.65 p/therm.
 
Weaker demand due to warmer weather put pressure on European spot power prices in mid-week trading. The German day-ahead power price dropped by 1.2% to 32.82 EUR/MWh, while the same contract in France slipped by 1.5% to 35.66 EUR.
Year-ahead power prices had a downward evolution, tracking bearish coal and gas prices. The German Cal'21 contract lost 0.6% at 42.14 EUR/MWh, while the French equivalent contract ended 0.6% lower at 43.82 EUR.
 
EUAs expiring in 2021 tumbled by 2.6% to 24.17 EUR/tonne following a dull Polish auction.
