Daily (30.05.2019) German spot power price plummeted by over 61% ahead of holidays due to lower demand in the region 
30052019

1126
DailyCrude oil prices declined on Wednesday, as China claims to put further pressure on the trade war with the United States, by using its dominant position in rare earths - a group of 17 chemical elements used to produce various electronics, industrial and military equipment. However, recent supply disruptions in U.S. limited gains. Brent crude decreased by 0.9% to $69.45 a barrel, while WTI futures closed 0.6% down at $58.81 a barrel.British gas prices lost momentum on Wednesday due to weaker demand and an oversupplied system. NBP day-ahead tumbled by 5.4% to 28.85 p/therm, while the contract for Q1 20 delivery traded 0.2% down at 58.35 p/therm.
 
European prompt power prices were strongly bearish on Wednesday ahead of a holiday in the region on Thursday weighing on demand. The price for the German day-ahead power contract plunged by 61.5% to 16.09 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract settled 48.6% lower at 19.29 EUR/MWh.
 
Forward electricity prices inched up, tracking higher carbon prices. The price for the German power contract for delivery in 2020 edged 0.1% up to 48.76 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract ended 0.4% higher at 51.78 EUR/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices nudged up on Wednesday, following a a stable auction trade that could soon result in a sudden price movement. The price for the 2021-expiry contract traded 0.2% higher at 26.10 EUR/tonne.
