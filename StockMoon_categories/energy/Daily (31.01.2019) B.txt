Daily (31.01.2019) British spot gas price tumbled by 1.6% on Wednesday due to an oversupplied system 
31012019

1141
DailyOil prices were up on Wednesday after U.S. government data indicated a lower-than-expected rise in crude oil stockpiles.  Moreover, U.S. sanctions against Venezuelan exports and a decline in gasoline inventories supported prices. Nevertheless, the trade dispute between the United States and China is still weighing on the market. Brent crude rose 0.5% higher to close at 61.65 USD/bbl, while WTI crude surged by 1.7% to settle at 54.23 USD/bbl.British gas prices tumbled on Wednesday, despite colder weather forecasts. Prices are under pressure as the system is well supplied. NBP spot price fell 1.6% to 54.50 p/therm, while the April 2019 delivery contract traded 1.8% down at 50.83 p/therm.
 
European prompt electricity prices rose on Wednesday supported by lower wind supply and stronger demand. German spot power price was 12.8% higher at 59.84 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract jumped by 3.3% closing at 61.13 EUR/MWh.
 
Along the curve, prices edged lower on Wednesday, together with gas prices. The German power contract for delivery in 2020 fell by 0.4% to 49.55 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract closed 0.6% lower at 52.68 EUR/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices plummeted on Wednesday, as the auction market noted a sluggish performance and a weak clearance activity. The price for the 2020-expiry contract was 1.5% down at 23.48 EUR/tonne.
