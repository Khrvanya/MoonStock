Daily (4.03.2019) WTI crude plunged by 2.5% on Friday, amid declining U.S. manufacturing data 
04032019

1030
DailyCrude oil prices fell on Friday amidst bearish U.S. manufacturing data that triggered concerns over global energy demand growth. Brent crude fell by 1.5% to 65.07 USD/bbl, while WTI futures plunged by 2.5% to 55.80 USD/bbl.UK wholesale gas prices tumbled on Friday as stronger LNG imports led to an oversupply in the system. NBP spot price declined by 2.7% to 43.80 p/therm, while the Q2 2019 delivery contract traded 2.1% down at 43.05 p/therm.
 
European power prices lost ground on Friday. The German day-ahead power contract slumped by 10% to 41.25 EUR/MWh, due to huge wind output, offsetting the effect of declining temperatures and tighter thermal plant availability. The equivalent French contract traded 8.2% lower at 40.71 EUR/MWh, on falling consumption demand.
 
Along the curve, electricity prices rose influenced by higher carbon emission prices. The price for German power contract for delivery in 2020 increased by 1.4% to 48.79 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract was 1% up at 51.77 EUR/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices extended gains on Friday, on bullish trading activity, completely recouping all of February's losses. The price for the 2021-expiry contract traded 2.7% higher at 23.12 EUR/tonne.
