Daily (6.03.2019) NBP spot fell by nearly 1% on Tuesday on bearish fundamentals 
06032019

1017
DailyBrent crude edged 0.3% higher on Tuesday awaiting a positive trade deal between US and China, closing at 65.86 USD/bbl at the end of the day. Meanwhile, WTI futures traded 0.1% lower at 56.56 USD/bbl in anticipation of U.S. government crude stocks data.British gas prices slipped on Tuesday on bearish fundamentals and stronger flows from Norway. NBP spot price dropped by 0.9% to 43.40 p/therm, while the year-ahead delivery contract traded 0.5% lower at 52.14 p/therm.
 
European day-ahead power prices were mixed on Tuesday. The price for the German spot power contract jumped by 10.9% to 34.77 EUR/MWh, supported by a sharp fall in the country's wind power generation.  Meanwhile, the equivalent French contract traded 11% lower at 35.98 EUR/MWh, due to weaker consumption and stable nuclear availability.
 
Along the curve, electricity prices fell tracking lower coal and carbon prices. The price for German power contract for delivery in 2020 declined by 1.5% to 48.76 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract settled 0.8% down at 51.89 EUR/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices retreated on Tuesday, after seven days of consecutive gains, due to a bearish trading session. The price for the 2020-expiry contract dropped by 0.8% to 23.33 EUR/tonne.
