Daily(13.07.2018) NBP spot price fell by 2.5% on Thursday due to expectations for lower demand 
13072018

1121
DailyOil prices were mixed on Thursday, spurred by news that the U.S. market has been undersupplied due to the loss of some Canadian oil production. Brent crude surged by 1.4% to $74.45 a barrel. Meanwhile, WTI crude edged 0.1% lower at $70.33 a barrel, as inventories at the Cushing delivery hub had fallen by almost 1M barrels.
Predictions for lower demand led to a decline in British gas prices on Thursday. NBP spot price fell by 2.5% to 56.85 p/therm. The contract for 2019 delivery had the same downward evolution, trading 2.6% lower at 55.67 p/therm.
 
European power prices slumped on Thursday, in the light of lower demand for electricity, mainly due to higher solar supplies. German spot power price dropped by 4.7% to 50.83 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract slipped by 3.5% to 51.45 EUR/MWh. 
 
Though decline in wind power generation didn't meet the demand, the German Cal' 19 contract was 2.3% down at 44.66 EUR/MWh. The French contract for 2019 delivery settled 1.9% lower at 49.5 EUR/MWh.
 
European carbon prices slipped on Thursday, putting an end to the 8-day straight bullish motion. The carbon contract expiring in 2021 closed 1.3% lower at 17.19 euros a tonne.
