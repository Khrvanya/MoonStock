Daily(27.07.2018) Brent crude increased by 0.8% as Saudi Arabia decided to halt oil exports 
27072018

1336
DailyOil prices increased on Thursday, as Saudi Arabia decided to stop oil exports after two ships which traversed the Bab el-Mandeb strait have been attacked by Yemen's Iran-aligned Houthis. Brent crude rose by 0.8% to $74.54 a barrel, while WTI crude edged 0.5% higher at $69.61 a barrel.Gas prices varied on Thursday. The day-ahead price declined by 0.8% to 57.70 p/therm, because of high temperatures and an oversupplied system. Meanwhile, the April 2019 delivery contract traded 0.2% higher at 56.26 p/therm, due to scheduled summer maintenance at several fields, terminals and the SAGE system in August.

Spot electricity prices were bearish on Thursday due to reduced consumption and more renewable and thermal power supply. The German contract for Friday delivery closed 2.6% down at 53.63 euros/MWh. The equivalent contract in France traded 6.4% lower at 60.04 euros/MWh.
 
On the forward curve, electricity prices followed the downward trend in carbon and coal markets. The German electricity contract for 2019 delivery lost 1.7% to settle at 44.66 euros/MWh, while the French equivalent contract ended 1.3% down at 49.35 euros/MWh.
 
European carbon prices slipped on Thursday, mainly because of a weaker auction and lower power prices, but still held above the EUR17 level. The carbon contract expiring in 2021 settled 0.3% lower at 18.60 euros a tonne.
