Dubai, Shanghai Electric and ACWA Power cut a solar power deal worth $3.9 billion 
18092017

1024
Solar Shanghai Electric and Saudi Arabia's ACWA Power will construct and manage a 700-megawatt solar power plant under a $3.9 billion contract awarded by Dubai's state energy utility.The United Arab Emirates officials stated that a 260-metre (850-foot) tower obtaining focused sunlight will be built. 
 
The tower will be the world's tallest one.  
 
The conglomerate submitted an offer to provide electricity to Dubai at a price of 7.3 U.S. cents per kilowatt hour. 

The first phase of the project should be completed in 2020. 
 
The solar park is likely to produce 1,000MW by 2020 and 5,000MW by 2030. 

Dubai's authorities are committed to use the solar park and other energy sources to get a 7% share of renewable energy in Dubai's power output by 2020, 25% by 2030 and 75% by 2050.
