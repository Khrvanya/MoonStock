MOSCOW, Jan 23 (Reuters) - Russia's number two oil producer Lukoil expects the deal between OPEC and other large producers to limit oil output to be extended beyond March, Interfax news agency cited its chief executive as saying on Thursday.The pact is currently due to expire at the end of March.CEO Vagit Alekperov also said the company's investments currently stand at 650-670 billion roubles ($10.5-$10.8 billion).($1 = 61.9355 roubles) (Reporting by Anton Kolodyazhnyy; Writing by Vladimir Soldatkin; Editing by Jan Harvey)
