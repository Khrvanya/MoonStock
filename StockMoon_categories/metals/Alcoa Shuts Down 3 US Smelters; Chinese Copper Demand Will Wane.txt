Alcoa Shuts Down 3 US Smelters; Chinese Copper Demand Will WaneCopper demand has picked up in China recently but it's unlikely to last and low prices have forced Alcoa to shut down three US smelters, the aluminum giant said.Stronger Chinese demand for copper in recent weeks is unlikely to last or to signal a broader recovery for the metal often regarded as a bellwether of economic growth, according to industry sources and a delve into data.Free Sample Report: Our Annual Metal Buying OutlookA surge in Chinese imports of copper is mainly due to buyers taking advantage of low prices to restock, rather than representing increased industrial consumption of the metal widely used in the power and construction sectors.Alcoa, Inc. said on Monday it will idle three of its four active US aluminum smelters, slashing annual capacity by 500,000 metric tons.Free Download: The October MMI ReportThe company said in a statement it will suspend its Intalco and Wenatchee smelters in Washington state and the Massena West smelter in New York state.
