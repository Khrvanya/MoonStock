Aluminum Supply Tightening, Drought Stalls Alcoa Inc. Brazilian ProductionProducing aluminum in Brazil has become so expensive this year, as electricity prices surge to record highs, that Alcoa has idled its Pocos de Caldas smelter and now sells the facility's power instead of metal, Bloomberg News reported.FREE Download: The Monthly MMI(r) Report - covering the Aluminum market.The worst drought in decades has drained reservoirs used to run hydroelectric generators that supply power to extract aluminum, boosting costs already inflated by more spending on labor and transportation. Cities may be forced to ration water, and last week the government cut taxes on imported aluminum to help ease shortages as demand grows for beverage cans.
