Central Bank Shakiness Finally Hits SteelThe continuing sluggish Chinese economy took its toll on steel prices this week as the European Central Bank failed to impress investors and the US Federal Reserve neared the end of its quantitative easing bond-buying program. Moves to increase production may have been too much too soon. Hot-rolled steel, a benchmark grade that typically is processed into cars, building structures or appliances, cost about $600 to $620 a metric ton in August. Now it can be had for about $550-$570 a ton, a drop of about 8%, according to World Steel Dynamics, a steel research firm.
