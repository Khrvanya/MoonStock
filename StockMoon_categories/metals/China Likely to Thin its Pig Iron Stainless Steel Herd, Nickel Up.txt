China Likely to Thin its Pig Iron Stainless Steel Herd, Nickel UpProducers of an alternative to refined nickel in China are expected to cut output as prices for ore imports have nearly doubled since the end of February after the ban of shipments by top exporter Indonesia, Reuters reported. Reduced supply of nickel pig iron could mean the world's top consumer of nickel would increase its use of higher-grade and possibly imported refined metal, underpinning global prices that have already risen about 16 percent this year.
