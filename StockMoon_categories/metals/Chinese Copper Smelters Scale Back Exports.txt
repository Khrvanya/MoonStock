Chinese Copper Smelters Scale Back ExportsChina's smelters have cut exports of spot refined copper over the past week to sell more in the domestic market where prices have risen, easing a supply shortage in the world's top consumer of the metal, Reuters reported.FREE Download: The Monthly MMI(r) Report - covering the Copper market.Reflecting the easier availability, premiums copper buyers in China pay on top of the Shanghai Futures Exchange front-month contract to secure physical metal have halved to around 500-600 CNY ($80.28-$96.34) per ton this week from more than 1,000 CNY in April, traders and sources at smelters said.With a decline of 0.8 percent to CNY 49,800 ($7,985) per metric ton on Thursday, May 8, Chinese copper bar recorded the biggest price drift of the day. The cash price of Chinese copper fell 0.8 percent to CNY 50,000 ($8,017) per metric ton. The price of Chinese copper wire remained essentially flat at CNY 49,690 ($7,967) per metric ton.
