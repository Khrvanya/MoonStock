Copper MMI® Heavy Losses for Smelters, China Purchases 200,000 Tons for StorageThe weekly copper MMI saw China's state stockpiler buy 200,000 tons of the beleaguered, red metal in hopes of stocking and forcing prices of the metal up. Prices are generally still down for copper. Both Freeport and Jiangxi Copper reported heavy losses this week due to the ongoing price erosion.FREE Download: The Monthly MMI(r) Report - covering the Copper market.The week's biggest mover on the weekly Copper MMI(r) was the price of Chinese copper wire, which saw a 4.4 percent increase to CNY 49,680 ($7,950) per metric ton. This week marked the fifth in a row of rising prices for the metal. At CNY 50,900 ($8,145) per metric ton, the price of Chinese copper bar finished the week 4.3 percent higher. The Chinese copper cash price rose 4.3 percent this week, closing out the third week at CNY 51,100 ($8,177) per metric ton.
