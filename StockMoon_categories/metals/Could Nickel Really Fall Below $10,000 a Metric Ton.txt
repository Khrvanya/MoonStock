We can't really put all the blame on nickel since this is not the only metal falling. Weakness in China and a strong dollar keep punishing commodities and, even more stridently, industrial metals.The monthly Stainless MMI(r) registered a value of 68 in July, a decrease of 6.8% from 73 in June.Nickel's supply and demand fundamentals, however, agree with the bearish picture the market is painting. We see a couple of factors weighing on prices:As nickel free-falls, prices are approaching the record low levels of 2009. Nickel could be the first base metal hit that floor.
