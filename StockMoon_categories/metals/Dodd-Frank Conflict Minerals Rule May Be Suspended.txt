Dodd-Frank Conflict Minerals Rule May Be SuspendedThe Trump administration is reportedly considering an executive order that would suspend the conflict minerals rule of the Dodd-Frank banking regulation bill.Click Here for Current Metal PricesThe conflict mineral rule requires reporting of supply chains to enforce a ban on tin, tantalum, tungsten and gold from the Democratic Republic of the Congo. It's backed by human-rights groups but many businesses say the rule, as is, requires a swath of industries to investigate whether their products contain metals that could have been sold by armed groups so far down their supply chains that it's impossible to tell where it came from. Reporting has been spotty even under the current rules.The proposed executive action, drafted last week and reviewed Wednesday by The Wall Street Journal, would suspend the conflict-minerals rule for two years.
