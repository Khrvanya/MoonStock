Indian Silver Jumps 3.1%, Speculators Fuel Positive Gains in JapanSilver recovered on Friday thanks to strong speculative demand. Silver has struggled to hold gains this year but a significant jump on the Indian and Japanese markets led to gains for its precious metal cousin, gold as well.On Friday, April 18, Indian silver jumped up 3.1 percent, landing at INR 45,209 ($749.65) per kilogram and making it the day's biggest increase. The price of Japanese silver is back up, rising by 0.8 percent on Thursday to close at JPY 641.00 ($6.26) per 10 grams. The price of Chinese silver fell 0.2 percent to CNY 4,070 ($654.36) per kilogram.
