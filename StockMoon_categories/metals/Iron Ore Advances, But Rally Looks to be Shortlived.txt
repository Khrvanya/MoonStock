Iron Ore Advances, But Rally Looks to be ShortlivedBenchmark Australian ore has advanced almost 8% since Christmas after prices hit a 5-and-a-half-year low of $65.60 a ton in December.FREE Download: The Monthly MMI(r) Report - covering Steel/Iron Ore markets.The Financial Times reported that few are convinced the rally will last. Analysts believe the bounce in prices is being driven by restocking at steel mills ahead of the Chinese new year and changes to reserve requirements for Chinese banks. With more supply set to come on line and demand in China weak, many believe prices will come under further pressure this year, even trading into the $50s.
