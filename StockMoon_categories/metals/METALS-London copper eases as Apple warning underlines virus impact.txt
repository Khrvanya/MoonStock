METALS-London copper eases as Apple warning underlines virus impactFeb 18 (Reuters) - London copper prices fell on Tuesday as a revenue warning by Apple Inc highlighted the impact of the coronavirus outbreak on economic activity in China, the world's top consumer of the red metal. Three-month copper on the London Metal Exchange (LME) was down 0.3% at $5,797 a tonne by 0252 GMT. Apple Inc on Monday became the latest company to flag lower revenue as a result of the epidemic, which has claimed more than 1,800 lives in mainland China and infected about 72,500 people. The number of new infections in the world's second-largest economy fell below 2,000 for the first time since January on Tuesday, but experts said the outbreak has not been contained.
