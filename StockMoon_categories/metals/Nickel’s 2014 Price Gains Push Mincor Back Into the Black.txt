Nickel’s 2014 Price Gains Push Mincor Back Into the BlackMincor's half-year filing showed the miner erasing debts and turning a profit on the back of a rising nickel price, stronger production and lower operating costs.FREE Download: The Monthly MMI(r) Report - covering the Stainless/Nickel markets.The nickel miner this morning posted a full-year profit of $1.85 million, bouncing back from last year's $22.5 million loss. The result was achieved thanks to a 11.3 percent jump in revenue to $110 million. Mincor produced 10,219 tons of nickel over the year up from last year's figure of 9688 tons and cash costs were $4.96 a pound, down from last year's $5.34 a pound.
