Demand for palladium this year will exceed mine output by the most ever, according to London-based Johnson Matthey Plc, which makes a third of the world's catalytic converters. A five-month labor strike crimped output in South Africa, the second-biggest source of the metal, helping boost prices 25 percent this year through yesterday."There is a lot of concern about palladium as imposition of more sanctions against Russia may affect supplies, which is already in a precarious state because of the strike," Mike Dragosits, a senior commodity strategist at TD Securities in Toronto, told Bloomberg Businessweek in a telephone interview.
