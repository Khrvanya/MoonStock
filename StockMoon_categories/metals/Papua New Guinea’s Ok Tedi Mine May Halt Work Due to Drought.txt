Papua New Guinea’s Ok Tedi Mine May Halt Work Due to DroughtBlame the weather... "The Ok Tedi mine, one of Papua New Guinea's largest operating copper mines, is two weeks away from temporarily shutting down due to a drought that has brought river transport to a halt and cut off fuel supplies, a company official said on Tuesday," reports Reuters."The mine, which produces about 150,000 tonnes of copper in concentrate a year, ships the material by a pipeline and barges more than 1,000 km (600 miles) to silo vessels in the Gulf of Papua, before it is loaded on to freighters.""Dry conditions have caused problems to shipping from Ok Tedi a number of times in the past.""'We have maybe 14 days before halting production,' Nigel Parker, managing director of Ok Tedi Pty Ltd, told Reuters.
