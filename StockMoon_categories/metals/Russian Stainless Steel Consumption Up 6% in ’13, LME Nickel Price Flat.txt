Russian Stainless Steel Consumption Up 6% in ’13, LME Nickel Price FlatAccording to SpetsStal, the trade association for steel producers in Russia, the volume of Russian stainless steel consumption rose by 6.2% to 383.9 thousand tons in 2013 over 2012. The volume of domestic output rose 18.4% to 154.8 thousand tons.The share of stainless steel imports in the total amount of consumption decreased to 65.5%. Nickel is the binding metal for stainless steel.The LME saw no movement for the spot price of nickel which kept around $15,720 per metric ton. The nickel 3-month price ended a two-day climb, settling at $15,765 per metric ton on the LME.
