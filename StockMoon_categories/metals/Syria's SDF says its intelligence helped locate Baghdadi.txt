Syria's SDF says its intelligence helped locate BaghdadiBEIRUT (Reuters) - The Kurdish-led Syrian Democratic Forces (SDF) said on Sunday the operation that killed Islamic State leader Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi took place after its intelligence pinpointed his location. Senior SDF official Redur Xelil said at a news conference that the forces would now intensify intelligence operations and efforts to chase down Islamic State sleeper cells. It blamed a Turkish assault on northeast Syria for delaying the Baghdadi operation by more than a month.
