This Morning in Metals U.S. Raw Steel Production Up 3.3%gui yong nian/Adobe StockThis morning in metals news, raw steel production this year through Sept. 9 is up 3.3% compared with the same period last year, U.S. steel shipments in July fell by 3.3% compared with the previous month and Tata Steel's shares surged after the news regarding its separation from its U.K. pension scheme.Benchmark Your Current Metal Price by Grade, Shape and Alloy: See How it Stacks UpAccording to weekly data from the American Iron and Steel Institute (AISI), U.S. raw steel production for the week ending Sept. 9 amounted to 1,733,000 net tons.The week's production marked an 8.9% increase from the same week in 2016, but a 0.8% decrease from the week ending Sept. 2.In the year through Sept. 9, however, production has hit 62,633,000 net tons, up 3.3% from the same period in 2016.According to AISI data, U.S. steel mills shipped 7,480,241 net tons in July, a 3.3% decrease from the 7,736,846 net tons shipped in the previous month. The July total, however, marked a 1.7% increase from the 7,352,698 net tons shipped in July 2016.The AISI report said shipments for the year-to-date are 52,939,327 net tons, up 2.8% increase versus shipments during the first seven months of 2016.After settling its pension liability scheme in the U.K., Tata Steel shares jumped 3%, the Economic Times reported.Free Sample Report: Our Annual Metal Buying OutlookAs part of the agreement in which the Indian company separated the British Steel Pension Scheme from its U.K. business, Tata was required to make a payment of PS550 million and offer a 33% stake in Tata Steel UK to trustees of the pension scheme, according to the Economic Times.Your email address will not be published.
