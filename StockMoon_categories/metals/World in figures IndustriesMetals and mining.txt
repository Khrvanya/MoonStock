World in figures IndustriesMetals and miningThe Economist Intelligence Unit's index of base-metal prices will rise by 9% in 2013, regaining most of the ground lost in 2012. A modest recovery in the European economy and stimulus-fuelled investments in China will support the metals markets.Copper will be the strongest performer in 2013, with prices rising by 12% thanks to tight supplies. A vigorous automotive industry will drive up prices of aluminium by around 8%. Despite rising commodity prices, margins for mining companies will shrink because of higher taxes and rising labour costs. Gold will remain a measure of investors' risk appetite; although prices will be volatile, they will fall later in the year as punters take profits. Still, the average gold price in 2013 will fall by only 5%, with steeper declines in store in 2014.
