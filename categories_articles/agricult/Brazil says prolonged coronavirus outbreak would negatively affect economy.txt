Brazil says prolonged coronavirus outbreak would negatively affect economy1 Min ReadBRASILIA (Reuters) - Brazilian Economic Policy Secretary Adolfo Sachsida said on Monday that a prolonged coronavirus outbreak could affect Latin America's largest economy if commodities prices remain low for a long time.  Sachsida added that the government did not see a reason to reduce its GDP growth projection for 2020 from 2.4%, at a time when several economists have slashed forecasts.
