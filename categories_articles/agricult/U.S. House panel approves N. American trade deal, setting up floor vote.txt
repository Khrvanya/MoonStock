U.S. House panel approves N. American trade deal, setting up floor vote1 Min ReadWASHINGTON, Dec 17 (Reuters) - The U.S. House Ways and Means Committee approved a revised North American free trade agreement by voice vote on Tuesday, sending the bill implementing legislation to the full House of Representatives for a vote on Thursday. The panel approved the U.S.-Mexico-Canada Agreement bill one week after senior U.S., Canadian and Mexican officials signed off on revisions to the deal in Mexico City to improve labor enforcement and reduce drug patent protections -- changes aimed at winning the support of Democrats.
