Dutch Authorities Arrest Two for Money Laundering Using CryptoThe Dutch Fiscal Intelligence and Investigation Service (FIOD) has arrested two men for their alleged involvement in money laundering using digital currencies.Per the official statement released on February 18, the two men were arrested in two separate criminal investigations. The press release also detailed that the accused had used Bestmixer.io, a digital currency mixing platform, to hide the past transactions of the ill-gotten cryptos.Cryptocurrency mixing or tumbler services shake up the transaction data of non-private coins to make the transaction sources untraceable, resembling transactions with a privacy-oriented digital currency. These services clear the record of any digital asset that was involved in illegal or shady activity, making them clean.Last year, Finance Magnates reported the forced suspension of Bestmixer.io's services by FIOD along with Europol and Luxembourg authorities.The investigation was a part of J5, an alliance formed by the tax enforcement authorities from Australia, Canada, the Netherlands, the United Kingdom, and the United States to share financial data to nab criminals.
