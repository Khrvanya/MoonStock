Daily (01.04.2019) British spot gas price declined by 4.9% on Friday, before the start of the summer season 
01042019

1054
DailyCrude oil prices added gains on Friday, as the OPEC-led supply cuts and U.S. sanctions on Iran and Venezuela offset concerns over the global economic slowdown. Brent oil rose by 0.8% to $68.39 a barrel, while WTI futures soared by 1.4% to $60.14 a barrel.UK wholesale gas prices extended losses on Friday, as the start of the summer gas season reduced demand. Moreover, an oversupplied system led to a 4.9% decline in NBP spot price to 35.30 p/therm, while the contract for delivery in Q4 2019 traded 0.2% down at 48.55 p/therm.
 
European day-ahead electricity prices posted losses on Friday, on expectations for lower consumption. The price for the German spot power contract plunged by 12.1% to 35.04 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract traded 13.5% lower at 34.05 EUR/MWh.
 
Further along the curve, electricity prices also tumbled, tracking weaker coal, carbon and gas markets. The price for the German power contract for delivery in 2020 decreased by 2.3% to 46.36 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract ended 2.4% down at 49.43 EUR/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices lost ground on Friday, due to failed Brexit negotiations for the third time. The price for the 2021-expiry contract traded 3.2% lower at 22.30 EUR/tonne.
