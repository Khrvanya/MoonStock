Daily (02.02.2018) German spot electricity price hiked by 19.1% on Thursday due to higher demand 
02022018

1259
DailyOil prices surged on Thursday after a survey showed that OPEC's commitment to its supply cuts is still in place, even if U.S. output hit 10 million barrels per day for the first time since 1970. Brent crude settled 0.9% higher at $69.65 per barrel. WTI crude ended at $65.8 per barrel, up 1.7%.British prompt gas prices rose on Thursday due to predictions for colder weather which boosted demand. NBP spot price soared by 3.4% to 50.15 pence per therm. Q2 2018 delivery contract edged 0.1% lower at 42.7 pence per therm, because of higher requests for imports via the InterconnectorUK piprline and more withdrawals from medium-range storage sites.

European day-ahead power prices increased on forecasts for higher demand due to an expected slump in temperatures next week. The German spot contract traded 19.1% higher at 40.48 euros/MWh. Meanwhile, the French day ahead price jumped by 4% to settle at 40.90 euros/MWh. 
 


On the forward electricity curve, French baseload contract for delivery in 2019 insignificantly rose to attain 39.44 euros/MWh. The German equivalent contract slipped by 0.2% to end at 34.4 euros/MWh.

EU carbon prices remained flat on Thursday in a volatile session. The carbon contract expiring in 2020 closed at 9.42 euros per tonne.
