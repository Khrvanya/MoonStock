Daily (02.11.2017) Electricity prices in Europe recovered on Wednesday after the All Saints holiday 
02112017

1111
DailyOil prices dropped on Wednesday after weekly U.S. government inventory data indicated the recent crude stock draw was not as huge as an industry trade group had expected. Brent futures settled down 88 cents, or 1.4 percent, at $60.49 a barrel. Meanwhile, U.S. West Texas Intermediate crude ended 8 cents, or 0.15 percent, lower at $54.30 a barrel.
 
British wholesale gas prices increased on forecasts for lower wind production. The day-ahead gas contract traded 0.68 pence, or 1.4%, higher at 48.33 pence per therm. Further along the curve, the December gas contract was up 0.72 pence, or 1.5%, closing at 51.10 p/therm. 
 
In the day-ahead market, electricity prices surged as consumption increased following the All Saints holiday on Wednesday. The German baseload contract for Wednesday delivery soared 10.3% to 34.07 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract ended 20.8% up at 55.57 euros/MWh. 

Along the forward power curve, prices rose slightly tracking carbon evolution. The German baseload power for delivery in 2018 closed 1.2 percent higher at 37.15 euros/MWh. The equivalent French contract was 0.5 percent up at 42.15 euros/MWh.

European carbon prices continued the bullish trend for a second consecutive day on Wednesday, buyout by another strong auction. The carbon allowances expiring at the end of December 2020 ended 1.9% at 7.71 euros a tonne.
