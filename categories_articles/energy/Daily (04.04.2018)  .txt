Daily (04.04.2018)  Brent crude rose by 0.7% due to the recovery in the equities markets 
04042018

1516
DailyOil prices rose by around 1% on Tuesday supported by a recovery in the equities markets after a significant fall on Monday. Brent crude increased by 48 cents, or 0.7%, to settle at $68.12 a barrel. U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude futures grew by $0.50, or almost 1%, to $63.51 a barrel.
 
British gas prices slumped on Tuesday due to forecasts for a rise in temperatures and strong supplies. NBP day-ahead gas price dropped by 3.30 pence, or 6.4%, to close at 48.45 p/therm. The gas contract for Winter 2018 delivery settled 0.5% down at 53.23 p/therm.
 
European prompt electricity prices were mixed on Tuesday as stronger demand in Germany and milder temperatures in France underpinned the market. German baseload contract for day-ahead delivery surged by 6% to 36.53 euros a megawatt hour (MWh) while French spot price decreased by 1.6% to end at 36.29 euros.
 
On the year-ahead curve, Germany's Cal'19 inched 0.1 % up at 36.53 euros/MWh. French equivalent contract traded 0.1% higher at 41.21 euros/MWh.
 
EUAs were almost constant on Tuesday because of bullish ETS emissions data after having plunged to a one-week low following the weakest auctions of the year. The carbon contract expiring in 2021 closed at 13.75 euros a tonne.
