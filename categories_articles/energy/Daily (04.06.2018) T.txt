Daily (04.06.2018) The French day-ahead power price tumbled by 38.7% on Friday due to lower demand 
04062018

1432
DailyOil prices fell on Friday, after U.S. President Donald Trump's declaration on trade boosted the dollar, reducing greenback-denominated commodities, including crude. Brent futures lost $0.8, or 1%, to end at $76.79 a barrel. WTI crude dropped by $1.23 a barrel to end at $65.81 a barrel.British wholesale gas prices declined on Friday due to an oversupplied system and lower consumption.UK day-ahead gas price was 2.20 pence, or 3.7%, lower at 56.80 p/therm. Meanwhile, July 2018 delivery contract closed 4.5% down at 55.00 p/therm.

European power prices slumped on Friday, pressured by lower fuel and carbon emission prices, and an expected increase in German wind electricity generation and reduced demand in France. The German day-ahead power price tumbled by 21.6% to end at 40.22 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract lost 38.7% to close at 28.69 euros/MWh.

Further along the curve, German Cal 19' European benchmark traded 3.6% down at 40.19 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract settled 3.6% lower at 44.80 euros/MWh.

EU carbon prices soared above the EUR15 level on Friday, recovering from previous day's strong losses, combining with German power to post generator profit margins to their lowest rate in at least a decade. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 ended 2.5% up at 15.47 euros/tonne.
