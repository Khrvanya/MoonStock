Daily (04.10.2017) UK gas contracts settled higher on expectations of solid demand 
04102017

1125
DailyOil prices dropped on Tuesday as speculators took profits for the second consecutive day after big third-quarter gains and on fears that elevated prices could spur rising U.S. shale output.Brent crude futures ended 12 cents, or 0.2 percent, down at $56.00 a barrel. WTI crude price fell 0.3% to $50.42 a barrel.

British wholesale gas prices surged on Tuesday on predictions of solid demand, even though the market was a bit oversupplied. NBP spot gas price increased by 1.95 pence, or 4.4%, to close at 46.45 p/therm. Summer 2018 delivery contract traded 0.64 pence, or 1.6%, higher at 41.79 p/therm.

European spot power prices for day-ahead delivery hiked on Tuesday, buyout by a forecast increase in demand in Germany after a public holiday, and reduced nuclear supply in France. French prompt electricity price hit a seven-month peak, surging by 7.7 euros, or 17.4%, to end at 51.95 euros/MWh. The German equivalent contract settled 16.9% higher at 28.61 euros/MWh.

Along the forward electricity curve, prices increased on Tuesday, following gains in fuel. The French contract for 2018 delivery edged 1.2% up at 41.59 euros/MWh. Meanwhile, the year-ahead price for electricity in Germany closed 1.3% higher at 34.89 euros/MWh.

EU carbon prices climbed back above EUR7 on Tuesday in a rather quiet, auction-free session amid strong gains in the most important electricity markets. December 2020 expiry EU carbon permits closed 0.9% up at 7.19 euros a tonne.
