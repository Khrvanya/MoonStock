Daily (04.12.2017) Power prices on the forwards curve rose along the gains in oil, carbon and coal markets 
04122017

1120
DailyOil prices edged higher on Friday, moving toward 2 1/2 -year peaks after two dozen crude-producing countries decided to reduce their output through the end of 2018. Brent crude rose $0.16, or 0.25 percent, to settle at $63.73, close to last month's high of $64.65 that marked the best intraday level since June 2015. Meanwhile, U.S. West Texas Intermediate crude prices spiked 96 cents per barrel, or 1.7 percent, to $58.36. 
 
British wholesale gas prices surged on Friday because of colder which boosted consumption levels and an undersupplied system. Day-ahead gas price increased by was up 0.80 pence, or 1.4%, to 57.50 p/therm. Summer 2018 contract traded 0.7% higher at 44.65 p/therm.
 
European power prices for Monday delivery came under selling pressure in wholesale market trade on Friday as increased wind generation was forecast from German turbines which should boost supply in the interconnected region. French Monday electricity hiked on signs that lower temperatures will stay in place for the next few days, supporting local demand for electric heating. 

The German baseload price for Monday delivery fell to 34.88 euros ($43.12) a megawatt-hour (MWh) after Friday delivery had closed at 61.03 euros/MWh. The equivalent French contract jumped by 8.15 euros, or 11%, to 81.90 euros.

Prices on the power forwards curve increased along with slight gains in the coal, carbon and oil markets. Germany's benchmark electricity contract for 2018 delivery was 0.2 percent up at 36.86 euros/MWh, while the equivalent French contract gained 1 percent to 43.12 euros/MWh.
 
EU carbon gained ground on Friday as traders were stimulated by robust demand signals, though prices remain stuck within a narrow range ahead of contract expiries and the winter auction pause. The 2018 carbon contract ended 2% up at 7.71 euros a tonne.
