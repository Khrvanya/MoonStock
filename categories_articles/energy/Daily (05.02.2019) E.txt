Daily (05.02.2019) EU carbon prices soared by almost 5% on Monday, ahead of an industry conference this week. 
05022019

1127
DailyOil prices saw a small drop on Monday influenced by the recent positive sentiment that China-U.S. relations will reach a comprehensive trade deal. However, losses were limited by OPEC supply cuts and U.S Sanctions on Venezuelan oil exports. As a result, Brent crude fell by 0.4% to 62.51 USD/bbl, while WTI crude dropped by 1.3% to settle at 54.56 USD/bbl.British wholesale gas prices were mixed on Monday. An oversupplied system put a slight pressure on the market. Consequently, the NBP spot price edged 0.1% lower at 51.50 p/therm. However, forecasts for short-term colder weather have supported the prices. The contract for March 2019 delivery traded 1.2% higher at 50.66 p/therm.
 
European spot power prices soared on Monday on colder weather outlook. The German day-ahead power price hiked by 7.2% to 53.79 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract traded 16.2% higher at 58.36 EUR/MWh.
 
Further along the curve, prices tumbled tracking a decline in coal, gas and carbon prices. The German power contract for delivery in 2020 traded 1.9% lower at 48.10 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract was 1.8% down at 50.61 EUR/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices recovered on Monday as the market sentiment was bullish ahead of an industry conference this week. The price for the 2020-expiry contract traded 4.9% up at 23.48 EUR/tonne.
