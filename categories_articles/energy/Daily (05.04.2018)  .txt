Daily (05.04.2018)  French day-ahead power price hiked by 13.6% on Wednesday due to higher demand 
05042018

1353
DailyOil prices decreased on Wednesday, mainly due to China's proposals for a series of tariffs on U.S. exports.Brent crude slid $0.10, or about 0.2%, to close at $68.02 a barrel. U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude futures declined by around 0.2 percent to 63.37 a barrel. 

UK prompt gas prices rose on Wednesday because of colder weather predictions. NBP day-ahead gas price surged by 1.30 pence, or 2.6%, to settle at 49.75 p/therm. May 2018 delivery contract increased by 0.5% to 47.18 p/therm. Meanwhile, November 2018 delivery contract traded 0.8% lower at 51.40 p/therm, as two LNG tankers are expected to arrive.
 
European day-ahead power prices were mixed on Wednesday. Expectations for stronger wind power generation placed the German contract lower, while the French equivalent traded higher due to increased demand. German baseload contract for day-ahead delivery dropped by 11% to 32.56 euros a megawatt hour (MWh), whilst French spot price jumped by 13.6% to end at 41.23 euros.
 
On the year-ahead curve, Germany's Cal'19 delivery contract edged 0.7 % down at 36.26 euros/MWh. French equivalent contract slid by 0.7% to 40.91 euros/MWh.

EUAs dropped on Wednesday after a weak auction as traders became wary of a potential rally that might continue in the second quarter. The carbon contract expiring in 2021 settled 1.7% down at 13.52 euros a tonne.
