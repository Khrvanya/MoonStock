Daily (06.01.2019) Crude oil prices surged by over 3% on Friday on heightened U.S.-Iran geopolitical risk 
06012020

1236
DailyCrude oil prices surged on Friday, hitting the highest level since September 2019 after a U.S. military strike killed General Qassem Soleimani, head of Iran's elite Quds Force, increasing concerns about a crude oil disruption.Brent crude for March delivery soared by 3.6% or $2.35 to settle at $68.60 a barrel on ICE London. U.S. WTI crude for February delivery climbed by 3.1%, or $1.87 to close at $63.05 on NYMEX.

UK prompt gas price started the new year in a bullish mood, due to higher gas-for-power demand. NBP spot skyrocketed by 11.8% to 29.90 p/therm.
 
In terms of forward contracts, prices were bullish as well. The gas contract for Summer 2020 delivery traded 9% higher at 31.54 p/therm, while the gas price for year-ahead delivery jumped by 4.9% to 43.57 p/therm.
 
European spot power prices lost ground on Friday, spurred by stronger power consumption forecasts in Germany and France. The German day-ahead power contract dipped by 22.2% to 20.32 EUR/MWh, while the French equivalent contract tumbled by 6.8% to 34.34 EUR/MWh.
 
Forward contracts edged higher on Friday tracking a bullish energy complex. German power for year-ahead delivery was traded 2.3% higher at 45.13 EUR/MWh, while the French equivalent contract climbed by 1.8% to 46.48 EUR/MWh.
 
Carbon prices increased on Friday, recovering after post-Christmas losses and hovered around the EUR25-mark on heightened geopolitical risk in the Middle East. EUAs expiring in 2020 gained 2.4% to settle at 25.18 EUR/tonne.
