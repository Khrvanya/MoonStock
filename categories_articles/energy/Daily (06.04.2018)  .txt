Daily (06.04.2018)  UK day-ahead gas price dropped by 1.4% due to additional flows from LNG tankers 
06042018

1350
DailyOil prices slightly increased on Thursday being supported by strong global equities as tensions in trade war between U.S. and China eased. Brent crude surged by $0.31, or 0.5%, to settle at 68.33 a barrel. U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude futures settled $0.17, or 0.3%, higher at $63.54 a barrel.
    
British wholesale gas prices were mixed on Thursday. Day-ahead gas price diminished by 0.70 pence, or 1.4%, to close at 49.05, because of additional flows from LNG tankers. May 2018 delivery contract closed 0.4% down at 46.97 p/therm.
 

 
European prompt electricity prices varied on Thursday. German day-ahead power contract soared on wind power output decrease, while French spot base contract declined on weaker demand. German baseload contract for day-ahead delivery hiked by 8.1% to 35.20 euros a megawatt hour (MWh). French spot price lost 11.5% to settle at 36.49 euros.
 
Year-ahead power curve prices went slightly up. Germany's Cal '19 baseload contract for 2019 delivery, the European benchmark, was up by 0.6 percent at 36.49 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract added 0.4 percent to reach 41.06 euros/MWh.

EUAs continued to fall on Thursday following another weak auction. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 was 3.2% down at 12.73 euros a tonne.
