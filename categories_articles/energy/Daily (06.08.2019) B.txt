Daily (06.08.2019) Brent crude declined by 3.4% on Monday, due to trade war concerns between China and U.S. 
06082019

1018
DailyCrude oil prices extended losses on Monday, after U.S. President Donald Trump threatened to impose more tariffs on China, which could dent crude demand. As a result, Brent crude traded 3.4% lower at $59.81 a barrel, while WTI futures dropped by 1.7% to $56.78 a barrel.British wholesale gas prices fell on Monday, as some domestic fields restarted production after maintenance works, leading to an an oversupply in the system. NBP spot price plunged by 11.6% to 28.10 p/therm, while the contract price for delivery in Q4 2019 inched down by 1.3% to 45.63 p/therm.
 
European spot electricity prices added momentum on Monday. The price for the German day-ahead power contract jumped by 15.6% to 42.06 EUR/MWh, amid stronger consumption. The price for the equivalent French contract soared by 17.3% to 41.19 EUR/MWh, as French utility authorities said that generation at the 3,000 MW Chooz nuclear power station might be curbed from Thursday Aug 8.
 
Further on the curve, power prices continued to decline, influenced by the bearish movements in the carbon, oil, and gas markets. The price for the German power contract for delivery in 2020 fell by 2.6% to 51.10 EUR/MWh, while the price for the equivalent French contract decreased by 2.3% to 52.43 EUR/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices lost ground on Monday, on fears that China-US trade negotiations will weigh down on the energy market. The 2021-expiry contract declined by 1.9% to 29.51 EUR/tonne.
