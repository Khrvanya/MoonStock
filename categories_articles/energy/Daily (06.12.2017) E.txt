Daily (06.12.2017) EU carbon prices fell 1.6% on weaker auction on Tuesday 
06122017

1122
DailyOil prices rose on Tuesday, buyout by strong demand, expectations of a decline in U.S. crude inventories and an OPEC-led agreement to extend oil production cuts. Brent crude futures ended 41 cents, or 0.7 percent, up at $62.86 a barrel, while U.S. West Texas Intermediate crude increased by 15 cents, or 0.3 percent, to $57.62 a barrel.British gas prices were mixed on Tuesday. Day-ahead gas fell 1.55 pence, or 2.6%, to 57.80 p/therm, on milder weather forecasts and greater output from wind farms. Further along the forward curve, Q1 2018 delivery contract traded 0.7% higher at 58.98 p/therm, following an unscheduled outage which curbed flows into Perenco's Bacton terminal in southeast England. Meanwhile, Summer 2018 contract was down 0.8% to 44.31 p/therm. 
 
In the power market, prices for day-ahead delivery fell in reply to forecasts for ample wind power. German day-ahead baseload contract ended at 31.25 euros, down 13 percent, while the equivalent French price dipped 5.4% to 86.90 euros/MWh.

Year-ahead electricity prices eased along with those of coal and carbon. Benchmark French power prices shed 0.9 percent to 43.29 euros/MWh, having hit a contract high on Monday related to the nuclear concerns. The equivalent German contract was 0.5 percent down at 37.06 euros/MWh.

EU carbon prices decreased on Tuesday after a weaker auction, staying nevertheless within their recent narrow range amid strong purchasing support around EUR7.40. Carbon contract expiring in 2018 settled 1.6% down at 7.46 euros a tonne.
