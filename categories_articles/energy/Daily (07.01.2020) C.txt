Daily (07.01.2020) Carbon prices turned bearish again on Monday after the restart of EUA auctions 
07012020

1136
DailyCrude oil price extended gains on Monday as tensions erupted following Soleimani's killing on Friday, with Tehran and Washington exchanging strike threats. Brent crude for March delivery rose above $70 a barrel at one point, but settled only 0.5% higher at $68.91 a barrel. U.S. WTI crude for February delivery gained 22 cents to close at $63.27 a barrel.Strong LNG flows and a mild weather sent British prompt gas prices down on Monday. In consequence, NBP spot gas plunged by 6.2% to 28.05 p/therm. Along the forward curve, prices also fell, with gas for delivery in Summer 2020 down 3.9% at 30.30p/therm. At the same time, the gas price for year-ahead delivery declined by 2.4% to trade at 42.52 p/therm.
 
European spot electricity prices skyrocketed on Monday due to a considerable increase in demand after holidays in Germany and low solar power production in both Germany and France. German power for day-ahead delivery rallied 99.4% to settle at 40.52 EUR/MWh, while the same contract in France soared 22.2% at 41.97 EUR/MWh.
 
Forward electricity prices lost ground tracking lower gas and carbon prices. German power for year-ahead delivery shed 0.7% to 44.83 EUR/MWh, while the French equivalent contract lost 0.5% to 46.27 EUR/MWh.
 
Carbon prices tumbled by 3% on Monday, as traders underrated the perspective of a war in the Middle East, while the restart of EUA auctions this week put more pressure on prices. EUAs expiring in 2021 settled at 24.43 EUR/tonne.
