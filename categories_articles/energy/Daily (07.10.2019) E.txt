Daily (07.10.2019) EU carbon prices hit a new 6-month low on Friday due to bearish fundamentals 
07102019

1141
DailyCrude oil prices firmed on Friday following a 1% decline in U.S. unemployment rate that reached a near 50-year low of 3.5%. Hence, Brent crude rose by 1.1% to $58.37 a barrel, while WTI futures added 0.7% to settle at $52.81 a barrel.British gas prices diverged on Friday. NBP spot plunged by 5.1% to 27.75 p/therm due to higher wind output that trimmed gas demand used for power generation. On the curve, prices inched up influenced by higher oil prices and a drop in Norwegian flows.  The contract for Summer 20 delivery closed 0.3% up at 43.14 p/therm.
 
European prompt power prices dropped on Friday. The price for the German day-ahead power contract slumped by 15.6% to 36.89 EUR/MWh due to a decrease in demand. At the same time, the equivalent French contract settled 6.7% lower at 37.31 EUR/MWh, amid steady nuclear availability.
 
Forward electricity prices added ground, tracking higher gas prices. The price for the German power contract for delivery in 2020 edged 0.4% higher at 46.79 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract traded 0.5% higher at 49.47 EUR/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices were in a bearish mood on Friday, amid bearish technical indicators and fundamentals. As a result, the price for the contract expiring in 2019 fell by 1.3% to 22.94 EUR/tonne.
