Daily (07.11.2019) EUAs fell by around 2.8% on Wednesday due to strong auction supply and concerns over a slowing economic growth in the EU

DailyCrude oil prices retreated on Wednesday, weighed by a higher-than-predicted rise in the U.S. crude inventories. Reports showing a sluggish growth in Germany's services sector and Eurozone business activity close to stagnation put more pressure on the market. As a result, Brent crude traded 1.9% lower at $61.74 a barrel, while WTI futures sank by 1.5% to $56.35 a barrel.Stronger Norwegian flows and robust LNG arrivals weighed on British wholesale gas prices on Wednesday. Moreover, expectations for a surge in wind generation curbed gas demand from power plants. Consequently, the NBP spot price dropped by 2.5% to 33.75 p/therm. In the meantime, the gas price for delivery in Q1 2020 fell by 2.6% to 45.42 p/therm.
 
European spot electricity prices reversed trend to slump on Wednesday, due to higher power output from renewable sources and robust thermal generation plant supply. The price for the German day-ahead power contract tumbled by 17.5% to 42.72 EUR/MWh. At the same time, the French equivalent contract ended 16% lower at 43.52 p/therm. 
 
Forward power prices pared earlier gains on Wednesday, tracking weaker carbon prices and a bearish energy complex. The price for the German Cal2020 power contract inched down by 0.7%, closing at 46.82 EUR/MWh, while the French equivalent contract retreated by 0.5% to 49.29 EUR/MWh.
 
Stronger auction supply led to a decline in the EU carbon prices on Wednesday, while a downbeat Eurozone economic outlook added to the market bearishness. The price for the 2021-expiry contract fell by 2.8% to 25.19 EUR/tonne.
