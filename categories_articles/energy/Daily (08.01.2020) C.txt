Daily (08.01.2020) Crude oil down 1% on Tuesday on reduced fears of an eventual crude supply squeeze 
08012020

1034
DailyCrude oil prices fell about 1% on Tuesday, recording the first decline in four sessions, dragged down by reduced fears of an eventual crude supply disruption in the Middle East after the U.S. killed an Iranian general last Friday.Brent crude for March delivery shed 64 cents, or 0.93%, to end at $68.27 a barrel on ICE London. WTI crude for February delivery declined by 57 cents, or 0.9%, to trade at $62.70 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange.
 
Stronger gas-for-power demand due to weaker renewable energy generation boosted the British spot gas price on Tuesday, which rose by 0.7% to 28.25 p/therm. On the other side, lower oil prices weighted on forward gas contracts. The gas price for Summer 2020 delivery fell 2.9% to trade at 29.42 p/therm, while the gas contract for year-ahead delivery lost 1.5% at 41.86 p/therm.
 
European spot power prices tumbled on Tuesday on weaker demand, stronger wind power generation in Germany and healthy nuclear availability in France. German spot power dipped by 17.1% to close at 33.58 EUR/MWh, while the French contract for day-ahead delivery plunged by 3.7% to 40.43 EUR/MWh.
 
Along the forward curve, prices posted minor losses tracking a bearish energy complex. The German contract for year-ahead delivery was rather flat at 44.79 EUR/MWh, while the French equivalent contract dropped by 0.4% to 46.10 EUR/MWh.
 
The carbon prices rose on Tuesday ahead of the resumption of EUA auctions, rebounding after having hit three-week lows in prior session. EUAs expiring in 2020 soared 1.3% to 24.55 EUR/tonne.
