Daily (08.12.2017) EU carbon prices settled 1.1% higher on Thursday after a week of declines 
08122017

1132
DailyOil prices recovered on Thursday after a data report showed a drop in U.S. crude inventories. Brent crude futures settled at $61.20 a barrel, up 98 cents, or 1.6 percent. Meanwhile, U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude futures closed at $56.51 a barrel, up 55 cents, or 1 percent up from their last settlement.British wholesale gas prices surged on Thursday on expectations of colder weather which has boosted consumption forecasts. NBP day-ahead gas price soared by 1.75 pence, or 3.1%, to 58.65 p/therm. Summer 2018 delivery contract traded 0.2% lower at 43.82 p/therm.
 
European day-ahead power prices tumbled on Thursday despite the gains in oil, gas and coal markets. German spot price was 10 percent down at 30.66 euros/MWh, while the equivalent French price was 7% off at 63.71 euros/MWh. The price premium over Germany reflects the generally tight nuclear situation.

Year-ahead electricity prices increased slightly, tracking coal and oil markets. The French power contract for delivery in 2018 was 0.1 percent up at 42.83 euros/MWh. The equivalent German contract was 0.2 percent higher at 36.84 euros.

EU carbon prices edged higher for the first time this week on Thursday, as purchasers emerged after EUAs failed to decline below November's low. The carbon contract expiring in 2018 settled 1.1% up at 7.38 euros a tonne.
