Daily (09.11.2017) British gas prices on the forward curve up on concerns about nuclear supply instability 
09112017

1108
DailyOil prices settled slightly lower on Wednesday after U.S. government data showed rising domestic crude output, an unexpected build in U.S. stockpiles and a decline in monthly Chinese crude imports, or a triple blow that was offset somewhat by increasing tensions in the Middle East.Brent futures fell 20 cents, or 0.3 percent, to settle at $63.49 a barrel, while U.S. West Texas Intermediate crude dropped 39 cents, or 0.7 percent, to close at $56.81 per barrel.
 
Predictions of higher wind power generation and warmer weather on Wednesday, drove the British day-ahead gas price lower. NBP spot contract traded 0.1% down at 51.45 p/therm. Worries about the stability of long-term nuclear power supply might be the reason that curve contracts are seeing a price increase, according to a trader. Summer 2018 delivery contract was 0.7% up at 44.33 p/therm.
 
Germany's electricity curve lowered after a coal-driven rally on Tuesday, as correlated fuels and carbon prices turned south. German year-ahead power lost 0.1 percent to 37.10 euros/MWh. The equivalent French contract gained 0.2 percent to 42.1 euros.

Wholesale power prices for day-ahead delivery surged sharply on Wednesday in France as nuclear sector continues to see tight supply. The French baseload spot contract was 10.2 percent up at 89.94 euros/MWh. Meanwhile, the equivalent German contract traded 23.4 percent lower at 51.53 euros, tracking carbon and other fuels evolution.

European carbon prices edged slightly down on Wednesday and traders held off making big moves ahead of the ETS reform trilogue negotiations. December 2018 expiry EU carbon permits expiring in 2018 traded 0.5% lower at 7.75 euros a tonne.
