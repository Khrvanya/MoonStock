Daily (10.02.2020) European spot power prices plummeted on Friday on strong wind output, weaker demand 
10022020

1202
DailyCrude oil prices ended on a sour note on Friday, posting a fifth consecutive week of losses, after Moscow declared it needed more time mulling over deeper production cuts proposed by OPEC to deal with a sluggish energy demand. 
Brent crude for April delivery shed 46 cents, or 0.8% to settle at $54.47 a barrel on ICE London. U.S. WTI crude futures slid 63 cents, or 1.2% to settle at $50.32 a barrel on NYMEX.
 
The British spot gas price lost 2.2% to end at 22.60 p/therm on Friday as healthy LNG flows and strong wind output weighed on prices. Further along the curve, prices also slipped, with gas for Q2 2020 delivery trading 0.5% down at 22.78 p/therm.
 
European spot electricity prices fell considerably on Friday due to robust wind output in Germany and lower demand in France. The German day-ahead power price dipped by 23.6% to 28.05 EUR/MWh, while the French equivalent position decreased by 19.8% to stand at 27.98 EUR/MWh. 
 
Year-ahead power contracts firmed on Friday, with German Cal'21 contract 0.7% higher at 41.23 EUR/MWh and the French equivalent contract 0.7% up at 42.71 EUR.
 
European CO2 allowances expiring in December 2021 shed by about 1% due to weak industry data.
