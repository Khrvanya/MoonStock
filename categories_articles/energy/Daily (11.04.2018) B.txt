Daily (11.04.2018) Brent futures rose by 3.5% to hit a peak since November 2014 
11042018

1128
DailyOil prices hiked on Tuesday as Wall Street grew sharply worried over possible military movements in the Middle East. Brent crude futures hit their peaks since 2014 adding $2.39, or 3.5%, to close at $71.04 a barrel. WTI futures rose by 3.3% to end at $65.51 a barrel.British wholesale gas prices soared on Tuesday despite the fact that flows from Norway were redirected to Britain from continental Europe, leading to an oversupplied system. UK day-ahead gas price surged by 1.5%, or 0.75 pence, to close at 50.40 p/therm. Meanwhile, May 2018 delivery contract settled 0.9% higher at 48.56 p/therm.

European power prices were mixed on Tuesday tracking volatile coal and carbon prices. German day-ahead baseload contract was 12.5% down at 31.52 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract ended 5.5% lower at 39.50 euros/MWh.

Further along the curve, German Cal 19' European benchmark traded 0.5% higher at 37.55 euros/MWh. Meanwhile the French equivalent contract gained 0.6% at 42.02 euros/MWh.
 
European carbon prices rose insignificantly on Tuesday as markets strived to absorb units from a stronger-than-normal auction. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 edged 0.4% higher at 13.47 euros/tonne.
