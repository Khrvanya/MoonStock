Daily (11.12.2017) EU carbon prices slumped by around 3% to their lowest level in five weeks on Friday 
11122017

1052
DailyOil prices surged by almost 2 percent on Friday, buyout by increasing Chinese crude demand and threats of a strike in Africa's biggest oil exporter. Brent crude closed $1.20, or 1.9 percent, up at $63.40 a barrel. Meanwhile, WTI crude prices rose 1.2% to settle at $57.36 a barrel.British wholesale gas prices declined on Friday as warmer weather forecast for this week reduced demand for heating. Day-ahead gas price fell 0.35 pence, or 0.6%, at 58.30 p/therm. Further along the curve, Q1 2018 delivery contract traded 2.3% down at 57.50 p/therm.  
 
European prompt electricity prices were higher in Germany on Friday, on expectations of decreasing wind power generation early this week, while French prices fell despite an underlying tightness in available nuclear capacity. German Monday delivery baseload power stood 36 percent above the price paid for Friday at 41.67 euros/MWh, while the equivalent French contract lost 11.5 percent to settle at 56.39 euros/MWh.

Prices along the power forwards curve soared on Friday with coal ticking up. French power contract for delivery in 2018 rose 1.1% to 43.30 euros/MWh, and the equivalent German contract ended slightly higher at 37.16 euros/MWh.

EU carbon prices slumped to their lowest level in five weeks on Friday as another weak auction and technical support breach hit sentiment in a nervous market approaching major contract deliveries. Carbon allowances expiring in 2020 ended 2.9% lower at 7.33 euros a tonne.
