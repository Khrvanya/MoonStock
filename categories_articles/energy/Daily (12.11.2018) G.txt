Daily (12.11.2018) German electricity spot price fell by 27.6% on Friday due to an oversupplied system 
12112018

1448
DailyStronger global supply alongside increased fears over lower pace of demand growth led to bearish oil prices on Friday. Brent crude lost $0.47, or 0.7% to end at $70.18 a barrel. WTI futures traded $0.48, or 0.8% lower at $60.19 a barrel. British wholesale gas prices had a mixed evolution on Friday. NBP day-ahead gas price fell by 4.4% to close at 61.20 p/therm, due to stronger flows from Norway.  Meanwhile, December 2018 delivery contract settled 0.1% higher at 67.10 p/therm, on forecasts for colder weather.
 
European power prices for day-ahead delivery were bearish on Friday due to higher supply and weak demand. German day-ahead power price tumbled by 27.6% to close at 43.07 euros/MWh. The French equivalent price dropped by 15.6% to 51.09 euros/MWh.
 
On the curve, electricity prices tracked the downward trend in fuels and carbon markets. German Cal 2019, the European benchmark, settled 1.1% lower at 51.26 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract ended 0.6% down at 55.75 euros/MWh.
 
After speculators took profits during last week's rally, carbon prices declined on Friday. The carbon contract expiring in 2020 traded 1% lower at 20.38 euros/MWh.
