Daily (13.02.2018) EU carbon prices jumped by 2.7% to attain a three-week peak 
13022018

1145
DailyOil markets were slightly bearish on Monday after world equities began to recover from losses in their biggest one-week decline in two years, while weaker dollar made crude cheaper for other currency holders. Brent crude slipped by 0.3% to close at $62.59. WTI crude increased by 0.2% to settle at $59.29 per barrel. British wholesale gas prices rose on Monday despite forecasts for colder temperatures and an oversupplied system. Day-ahead gas price soared by 1.1% to 50.72 p/therm. The Summer 2018 delivery contract edged 0.5% higher at 41.90 p/therm.

European spot electricity prices for day-ahead delivery increased on Monday due to forecasts for higher consumption and lower wind power generation. The German baseload contract for Tuesday delivery edged 0.6% up at 39.60 euros per megawatt-hour (MWh). The French spot contract surged by 2.2% to 42.93 euros/MWh.

Along the year-ahead curve, Germany's 2019 delivery contract, the European benchmark, slipped by 0.2 percent to 33 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract rose 0.3 percent to 38.80 euros/MWh.

EU carbon prices hiked to a three-week peak on Monday after the day's auction cleared at the biggest premium since late January and after observers predicted further profits this week. The carbon contract expiring in 2020 hiked by 2.7% to 9.63 euros/tonne.
