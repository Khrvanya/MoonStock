Daily (13.02.2019) EU carbon prices fell by over 7% to hit a new 2019 low 
13022019

1149
DailyOil prices reversed trend to hike on Tuesday, stimulated by the steep OPEC supply cuts, led by Saudi Arabia's biggest drop in pumping activity. Brent oil traded 1.5% higher at $62.42 a barrel, while WTI crude increased by 1.3% to $53.10 a barrel.British gas prices dipped on Tuesday as rising temperatures curtailed demand. Moreover, stronger wind output reduced gas-for power consumption. Despite lower flows from the Netherlands and Norway, NBP spot price jumped by 3.5% to 46.55 p/therm. Further on the curve, the NBP contract price for delivery in March dropped by 1.7% to 46.98 p/therm.
 
Tuesday saw a downward evolution in European day-ahead power prices, due to falling demand on milder weather and higher wind generation. The German spot power price declined by nearly 3% to t 45.38 EUR/MWh. The equivalent French contract plunged by 3.3% to 49.70 EUR/MWh, amid stable nuclear availability.
 
Along the forward curve, electricity prices extended losses, influenced by bearish carbon, coal and gas markets. The German power contract for delivery in 2020 lost around 1% to close at 46.53 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract edged 0.6% lower at 49.42 EUR/MWh.
 
European carbon prices posted sharp losses on Tuesday, hitting their lowest level in 2019 after plummeting below the major support level preserved since late 2018. The contract expiring in 2020 tumbled by 7.5% to 21.12 EUR/tonne.
