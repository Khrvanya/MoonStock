Daily (13.03.2018)  UK day-ahead gas price surged by over 30% on Monday due to colder weather forecasts 
13032018

1254
DailyOil prices slumped on Monday amid increasing US output on the back of onshore shale oil production, as indicated in a report published by Energy Information Administration.Brent crude futures dropped by $0.54, or 0.8%, to settle at $64.95 a barrel. Meanwhile, U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude ended $0.68, or 1.1 percent, down at $61.36 per barrel.
 
British prompt gas prices jumped on Monday due to colder weather forecasts and low storage levels. NBP spot price soared by 16.30 pence, or 30.5 percent, to settle at 69.80 p/therm. Further along the curve, contracts traded slightly higher. August 2018 delivery contract closed 0.1% up at 44.43 p/therm.
 
European prompt power prices hiked on Monday on expectations of lower temperatures and higher demand. Germany's day-ahead power price soared by 15% to 39.86 euros a megawatt hour (MWh). The French equivalent contract surged by 38.2% to 47.25 euros. 
 
Further along the curve, power contracts were in uptrend as well. German Cal 19', the European benchmark, increased by 1.3% to 35.39 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract traded 0.8% higher at 40.11 euros/MWh.
 
The EUAs were bearish on Monday nearing their intraday low despite hitting a new 6.5-year peak earlier after a strong auction result. The carbon contract expiring in 2021 decreased by 0.3% to 11.54 euros a tonne.
