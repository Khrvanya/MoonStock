Daily (14.02.2019) Oil prices climbed by 1.8% on Wednesday, as Saudi Arabia plans to reduce crude exports and production 
14022019

1216
DailyOil prices were bullish on Wednesday, after Saudi Arabia announced its plans to reduce crude exports and production. At the same time, rising U.S. crude inventories might still put pressure on the market. As a result, Brent crude increased by 1.9% to 63.61 USD/bbl. WTI crude rose by 1.5% to 53.90 USD/bbl.British gas prices gained ground on Wednesday, amidst weaker flows from Norway and higher oil prices. NBP day-ahead edged 2.2% higher to 47.58 p/therm, while the contract for Q2 2019 traded 1.2% up at 45.47 p/therm.
 
European day-ahead power prices soared on Wednesday, following a decline in wind power output. The German spot power price surged by 8.2% to 49.09 EUR/MWh. The equivalent French contract ended 1.5% higher at 50.45 EUR/MWh
 
Along the forward curve, electricity prices dipped on Wednesday tracking Tuesday's strong decline in carbon prices. The German power contract for delivery in 2020 lost 1.8% to close at 45.69 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract traded 1.6% down at 48.64 EUR/MWh.
 
After hitting a 2-month low on Tuesday, EU carbon prices reversed trend on Wednesday, as bargain-hunters reversed this week's downward evolution. The price for the 2021-expiry contract increased by 1.1% to 21.87 EUR/tonne.
