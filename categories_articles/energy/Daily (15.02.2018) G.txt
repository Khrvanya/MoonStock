Daily (15.02.2018) German day-ahead electricity price dropped by 14% due to strong wind power supply 
15022018

1128
DailyOil prices soared on Wednesday, recovering after earlier losses as U.S. crude stocks rose less than predicted. Brent futures added $1.64, or 2.6 percent, to end at $64.36 a barrel. Meanwhile, WTI crude settled 2.4% higher at $60.60 per barrel.British wholesale gas prices dropped on Wednesday as forecasts for milder weather reduced demand, leaving the gas system oversupplied. UK day-ahead gas price tumbled by 3.3% to 50.17 pence per therm. NBP Summer 2018 delivery contract traded 0.3% lower at 42.17 pence per therm.

European wholesale spot electricity prices declined on Wednesday because of robust wind power supply and a brief temperature increase. German baseload electricity price for day-ahead delivery slumped by 14.3% to 34.16 euros/MWh, while the French equivalent contract settled 4.6% down at 42.59 euros/MWh.

Forward along the curve, prices bolstered due to cold winter temperatures. German Cal 19', the European benchmark, traded 0.8% higher at 34.30 euros/MWh, while the French equivalent contract ended 0.1% up at 39.42 euros/MWh. 

EU carbon prices slumped after a weak auction in Wednesday, after surpassing the EUR10 peak for the first time since November 2011. The carbon contract expiring in 2020 closed almost 3% down at 9.75 euros/tonne.
