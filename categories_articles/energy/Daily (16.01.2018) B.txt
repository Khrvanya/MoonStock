Daily (16.01.2018) Brent crude settled close to a 3-year peak above $70 a barrel on tightening supplies 
16012018

1124
DailyOil prices jumped to near a three-year peak above $70 a barrel on Monday on signs that output reductions by OPEC and Russia are tightening supplies, although analysts warned of a "red flag" due to surging U.S. production.Brent crude futures settled 39 cents, or 0.6%, higher at $70.26, after having risen to a high of $70.37 a barrel earlier in the session. U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude futures gained 52 cents, or 0.8%, at $64.82 a barrel. Both benchmarks reached levels not seen since December 2014, although trading was thin due to a holiday in the United States.
 
An oversupplied system due to strong wind power production led to a decline in British wholesale gas prices on Monday. The day-ahead gas price fell 4.6%, or 2.50 pence, to 52 p/therm. February 2018 contract closed 3.8% down at 53.84 p/therm.
 
European wholesale prices for day-ahead delivery dropped on Monday due to robust wind supply, while curve contracts fell along with carbon prices, as Germany's latest climate policy decisions point to remaining overcapacities.

The German baseload electricity contract for day-ahead delivery tumbled 19.4$ to 27.22 euros/MWh, while its French equivalent declined by 15 percent to 33.54 euros. 

Further ahead, Germany's Cal '19 baseload contract dropped by 0.8% to a five-week low of just under 35.34 euros/MWh. Its less liquid French counterpart lost 0.6 percent to end at 40.46 euros/MWh, the lowest since Nov. 20.

EU carbon prices dropped in a quiet, rangebound session on Monday, as observers gave a bearish outlook for the week ahead. The carbon emission allowances expiring in 2018 settled 0.8% lower at 7.86 euros a tonne.
