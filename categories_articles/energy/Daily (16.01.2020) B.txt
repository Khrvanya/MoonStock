Daily (16.01.2020) British spot gas down 1.9% on robust wind output, healthy flows from Norway 
16012020

1047
DailyOil prices declined slightly on Wednesday after the release of a report showing a sharp increase in refined products in the United States and a rally in black gold production to a record level. However, the losses were curtailed by the signature of the Phase 1 U.S.-China trade agreement.Brent crude for March delivery fell by 49 cents, or 0.8%, to settle at $64 a barrel on ICE London. WTI crude for February delivery slipped 42 cents, or 0.7% to end at $57.81 on NYMEX.

Higher wind generation, healthy flows from Norway and robust LNG supplies weighted on the British day-ahead gas price on Wednesday. UK spot tumbled by 1.9% to trade at 28.85 p/therm.
 
Forward contracts saw a downward trend, with the gas price for Q2 2020 delivery down 0.6% at 27.46 p/therm, while the gas for Q3 delivery 0.9% lower at 28.44 p/therm.
 
Electricity spot prices strengthened on Wednesday due to reduced power capacity in France aftermath ongoing strikes over pension reform and weaker wind supply in Germany. The day-ahead power price in Germany skyrocketed 53% to stand at 38.17 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent contract in France hiked 17.5% at 37.14 EUR/MWh.
 
Year-ahead power prices increased as well, tracking a bullish energy complex. German CAL'21 power contract price rose slightly at 44.38 EUR/MWh, while the same contract in France added 0.4% at 45.87 EUR/MWh.
 
EUAs expiring in 2021 rallied 2.8% to 24.73 EUR/tonne, spurred by a strong demand for EUAs in market auctions.
