Daily (16.02.2018) WTI crude surged by 1.2% on Thursday as investors covered short positions 
16022018

1144
DailyBrent crude slightly decreased on Thursday due to a weaker dollar. Brent futures ended down by 3 cents, or 0.1%, at $64.33 a barrel. U.S. West Texas Intermediate crude hiked because of covered short positions by investors. WTI surged 1.2 percent, to end at $61.34.  British wholesale gas prices fell on Thursday as robust imports from Belgium and the Netherlands led to an oversupplied system. UK day-ahead gas price fell 2% to close at 49.15 p/therm. Summer 2018 delivery contract traded 0.9% lower at 41.80 p/therm.
 
European spot electricity prices soared on Thursday due to predicted decline in wind power generation. German baseload power price for day-ahead delivery jumped by 18.2 percent to end at 40.39 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract rose by 1.3 percent to 43.15 euros/MWh.

Power price on the curve decreased despite strong nuclear and hydro power supply. The German Cal '19, European benchmark, traded 0.7 percent lower at 34.05 euros/MWh. Meanwhile, the French equivalent contract dipped by 0.7 percent to 39.15 euros/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices edged lower on Thursday as analysts predicted a further decline due to the absence of fundamental support. The carbon contract expiring in 2020 settled 0.8% down at 9.69 euros/tonne.
