Daily (16.03.2018)  British prompt gas prices surged by 9% on Thursday due to expectations of lower temperatures 
16032018

1204
DailyOil prices increased on Thursday supported by investors' bullish sentiment on refreshed equity markets.Brent crude soared by $0.23, or 0.4%, to settle at $65.12 a barrel. U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude futures enhanced by 23 cents, or 0.4%, to $61.19 a barrel.
 
British prompt gas prices went up on Thursday due to expectations of lower temperatures. NBP spot gas price surged by 5.35 pence, or 9%, to close at 64.45 p/therm. The Q1 2018 delivery contract was traded 2% up at 46.73 p/therm.
 

European prompt electricity prices hiked on Thursday due to decline in wind power generation and weaker nuclear supply.  German baseload contract for day-ahead delivery rose by 19% to 29.32 euros a megawatt hour (MWh). French spot price increased by 28.5% to 46.64 euros on weaker nuclear power delivery.
 
Further along the curve, electricity contracts have slightly changed. German Cal 19' European benchmark was 0.6% up at 34.86 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract edged 0.7% up at 39.86 euros/MWh.

EUAs increased slightly on forecasts for a bullish evolution despite yesterday's weak auction. The carbon contract closing in 2020 traded 0.1% up at 11.33 euros a tonne.
