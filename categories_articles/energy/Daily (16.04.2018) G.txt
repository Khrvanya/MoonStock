Daily (16.04.2018) German and French forward electricity contracts hit new record highs on Friday 
16042018

1100
Energy MarketOil prices were boosted on Friday by concerns about the possibility of Western military action in Syria and reports of declining global oil inventories.Brent futures recovered from earlier losses, increasing by 56 cents, or 0.8%, to close at $72.58, and to post the biggest weekly gain since July. Meanwhile, U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude rose by 32 cents, or 0.5%, to settle at $67.39 a barrel, registering an 8.6% hike for the week.
 
British prompt gas prices were bearish on Friday, due to forecasts for higher wind generation over the weekend, with NBP day-ahead gas price down by 3.8% at 51 p/therm. Further along the curve, undersupply and increased demand supported gas contracts. Winter 2018 delivery contract traded 1.7% higher at 56.11 p/therm.

European forward electricity contracts were boosted on Friday by gains in related markets for coal, carbon emissions, oil and gas, with German Cal '19 setting a record, approximately one-fifth above its 2018 low attained only two months ago. German 2019 baseload, a European power curve benchmark, surged by 1.4% to end at 38.57 euros ($47.24) a megawatt-hour (MWh). The equivalent French contract for 2019 gained 1.3% to 43.05 euros/MWh.

Spot electricity prices for Monday delivery tumbled, due to rising temperatures. German Monday baseload power price slumped by 8.4 percent at 35.3 euros/MWh. The same French contract lost around 18% at 36.90 euros/MWh.

EU carbon prices hiked for the sixth consecutive session on Friday, climbing above EUR14 level near their seven-year peak, even though some analysts averted that weak auction demand might predict a correction. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 settled 2.7% higher at 14.08 euros a tonne.
