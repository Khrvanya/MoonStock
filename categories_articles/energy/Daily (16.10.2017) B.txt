Daily (16.10.2017) Brent crude surged 1.6% on Friday due to Middle East tensions and higher Chinese oil imports 
16102017

1036
DailyBullish news from robust Chinese oil imports and tensions in the Middle East supported oil prices on Friday, putting Brent on course for a weekly gain of around 3 percent. Brent crude futures ended $0.92, or 1.6 percent, up at $57.17 a barrel. Meanwhile, U.S. crude was up 85 cents, or 1.7 percent, at $51.45 per barrel.British prompt gas prices slightly increased on Friday as stronger exports to Belgium led to an undersupplied system. NBP spot gas price rose 0.35 pence, or 0.8%, to settle at 43.85 p/therm. Along the forward curve, gas contracts traded insignificantly lower. For instance, December 2017 delivery contract slipped 0.12 pence, or 0.2%, to end at 51.62 p/therm.

European wholesale electricity prices for day-ahead delivery soared on Friday as predictions of lower wind power production in Germany and reduced French nuclear production tightened the supply outlook. Germany's baseload contract for Monday delivery gained 25.4 percent to trade at 38.55 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract ended up 26.4 percent at 46.11 euros/MWh.

Power curve contracts were constant to higher as coal, gas and oil prices gains offset weaker CO2 emissions rights. Germany's year-ahead baseload contract, the European benchmark, was 1.3% up at 36.24 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract for 2018 delivery was almost flat at 42.69 euros/MWh.

Carbon prices dropped slightly on Friday but preserved most of the week's gains despite the news that discussions between negotiators for EU nations and the European Parliament over reforms to the EU Emissions Trading System (ETS) after 2020 ended without an agreement. December 2018 expiry carbon permits were 1.1 percent down at 7.35 euros a tonne.
