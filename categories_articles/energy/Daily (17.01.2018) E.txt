Daily (17.01.2018) EU carbon prices soared by 3.2% on Tuesday defying predictions of a January sell-off 
17012018

1113
DailyOil prices dropped off three-year highs on Tuesday as traders booked profits, but strong demand underpinned prices near $70 per barrel, a level not seen since the market decline in 2014.Brent futures dropped by $1.11, or 1.6 percent, to settle at $69.15 a barrel after hitting a session low of $68.83. The global benchmark reached a peak of $70.37 on Monday, matching a high from December 2014 at the beginning of a three-year market decline. 
 
Meanwhile, U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude futures ended at $63.73 a barrel, down 57 cents, or 0.9 percent, after having hit a December 2014 peak of $64.89 earlier in the session.
 
British prompt gas prices slumped on Tuesday, because of oversupply. Day-ahead gas price tumbled by 0.80 pence, or 1.5%, to 51.20 p/therm. Further along the curve, contracts were bearish as well. February 2018 delivery contract settled 1.6% down at 53 p/therm.
 
European wholesale spot power prices for day-ahead delivery rose on Tuesday on predictions of higher consumption stimulated by cold weather, particularly in France. Germany's baseload power for Wednesday delivery settled at 29.04 euros/MW, up 6.7 percent. French spot power gained over 10%, or 3.52 euros, to 37.06 euros/MWh.

Along the forward curve, Germany's Cal '19 baseload contract ended closed slightly higher at 35.36 euros/MWh. The French equivalent traded 0.1% lower at 40.41 euros/MWh.

EU carbon prices surged above EUR8 on Tuesday for the first time since the opening session of 2018, as prices continued to defy predictions of a January sell-off. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 settled 3.2% higher at 8.11 euros a tonne.
