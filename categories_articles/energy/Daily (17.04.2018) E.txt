Daily (17.04.2018) European carbon prices hit new 7-year highs on Monday as observers forecasted further gains 
17042018

1126
DailyOil prices fell on Monday following a higher U.S. drilling activity and due to worries related to increasing tensions in the Middle East after air strikes on Syria over the weekend. Brent futures lost $1.16, or 1.6%, to end at $71.42 a barrel. WTI crude slumped by 1.7% to $66.22 a barrel.British wholesale gas prices declined on Monday as higher temperatures weakened demand. UK gas spot price decreased by 0.70 pence, or 1.4%, to 50.30 p/therm. Meanwhile, Q2 2018 delivery contract traded 0.6% lower at 48.15 p/therm.

European power prices continued the bullish evolution on Monday, bolstered by hikes in related carbon and coal prices. German baseload contract closed 24.1% higher at 43.8 euros/MWh, while the French spot baseload price jumped by 18.7% at 43.8 euros/MWh.

Further along the curve, German Cal 19' European benchmark ended 0.6% higher at 38.81 euros/MWh to hit a 4  1/2  - year peak. Meanwhile, the French equivalent contract gained 0.7% at 43.36 euros/MWh.

EU carbon prices reached new seven-year peaks on Monday, surging for the seventh consecutive session as observers predicted further gains. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 traded 0.5% higher at 14.15 euros/tonne.
