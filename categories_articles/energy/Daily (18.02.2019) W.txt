Daily (18.02.2019) WTI crude soared on Friday by 2.2% buoyed by U.S. sanctions on Venezuela and Iran 
18022019

1127
DailyOil prices soared on Friday, buoyed by U.S. sanctions against Venezuela and Iran. At the same time, OPEC-led supply cuts also supported prices. Brent crude settled 2.6% higher at 66.25 USD/bbl, or the highest value this year. At the same time, WTI crude climbed by 2.2% to 55.59 USD/bbl.British day-ahead gas price remained unchanged, closing at 46.90 p/therm on Friday. Further along the curve, gas contracts were bullish despite oversupply and slightly milder weather. The contract for March 2019 traded 1.2% higher at 47.64 p/therm.
 
European spot power prices posted losses on Friday, as a result of lower demand due to above-normal temperatures for the season. The German day-ahead power contract plunged by 17.6% to 39.22 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract price tumbled by 9.9% to 44.03 EUR/MWh.
 
Further on the curve, electricity prices gained ground, tracking higher coal and carbon prices. The German power contract for delivery in 2020 rose by 1.4% to 46.47 EUR/MWh. The equivalent French contract inched 1.4% higher at 49.78 EUR/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices recovered on Friday, after last week's bearish evolution, when it hit the lowest value in two months. The price for the 2021-expiry contract rose by 3.2% to 21.39 EUR/tonne.
