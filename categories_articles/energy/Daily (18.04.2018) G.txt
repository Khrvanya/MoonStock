Daily (18.04.2018) German spot power contract down 6.3% on Tuesday due to milder weather and lower consumption 
18042018

1137
DailyOil prices surged on Tuesday, backed by the probability of supply interruption and a strong equities market, compensating the effects of profit-taking after last week's upward trend to three-year peaks. Brent crude added 16 cents, or 0.22%, to close at $71.58 a barrel. WTI futures soared by 0.5% to settle at $66.52.British wholesale gas prices dropped on Tuesday due to forecasts for warmer weather and lower demand. UK day-ahead gas price lost 2.25 pence, or 4.5%, to settle at 48.05 p/therm. Meanwhile, May 2018 delivery contract closed 1.9% down at 48.67 p/therm.

European electricity prices declined on Tuesday driven by forecasts for lower consumption due to milder weather, while French nuclear power availability is likely to improve.

German spot price for day-ahead delivery fell by 6.3% to 41.06 euros/MWh. French baseload contract settled 6.4% lower at 41.00 euros/MWh.

Further along the curve, German Cal 19' European benchmark closed 2.3% down at 37.92 euros/MWh. Meanwhile, the French equivalent contract ended 1.6% lower at 42.69 euros/MWh.

European carbon prices finished the run of seven consecutive days of gains on Tuesday, sliding back under EUR14 level, principally because of the downward evolution of the key energy prices. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 traded 1.8% lower at 13.89 euros/tonne.
