Daily (19.04.2018) WTI crude jumped by 3% on Wednesday to hit a peak since late 2014 
19042018

1125
DailyOil prices jumped on Wednesday due to a decline in U.S. crude inventories and after sources announced that top exporter Saudi Arabia wishes to see the oil price close to $100 a barrel. Brent crude added $1.90, or 2.7 percent, to end at $73.48 a barrel. WTI futures were $1.95, or 2.9 percent, up at $68.47 a barrel, their highest level since late 2014.Lower Norwegian flows and forecasts for a decrease in wind speeds this week which bolstered demand for gas-fired electricity production buyout British wholesale gas prices on Wednesday. UK day-ahead gas prices surged by 2.2% to end at 49.10 p/therm. Meanwhile, Q3 2018 delivery contract settled 2.5% higher at 48.45 p/therm.

European spot electricity prices for day-ahead delivery dropped on Wednesday on predictions of lower consumption due to warmer weather, while future delivery contracts rose despite a hike in wind and hydro power generation.

German spot power price declined by 3% to 39.84 euros/MWh. The French baseload contract ended 2% down at 40.19 euros/MWh.
Further along the curve, German Cal 19' European benchmark closed 0.3% higher at 38.02 euros/MWh. Meanwhile, the French equivalent contract settled 0.1% up at 42.74 euros/MWh.  

EU carbon prices restarted their bullish evolution on Wednesday to surge again above EUR14 mark despite another weak auction, as traders said purchasers were looking to fully cover their positions before the annual compliance deadline. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 traded 1.1% higher at 14.08 euros/tonne.
