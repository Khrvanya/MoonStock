Daily (19.10.2018)  EU carbon contracts recovered on Thursday in a strong auction 
19102018

1123
DailyOil crude prices fell on Thursday following data revealing ample U.S. crude inventories. Brent crude futures dropped by 1% to close at $79.29 a barrel, while the U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude futures declined by 1.6% to trade at $68.65 a barrel.British wholesale gas prices were slightly bullish on Thursday in a balanced system, as flows from Norway decreased, and colder weather boosted demand forecasts. NBP spot price surged by 1.2%, or 1.1 pence/therm, to 66.5 p/therm. Along the curve, the 2019 delivery contract traded at 64.90 p/therm, or 0.2 % higher.

European spot power prices rose on Thursday, backed by forecasts for a decline in wind electricity generation and low French nuclear power output. The electricity price for day-ahead delivery in Germany jumped by 7% to settle at 67.61 EUR/MWh. Meanwhile, the equivalent contract in France was only 0.5% up to end at 66.53 EUR/MWh. 

Along the forward electricity curve, contracts traded slightly higher on Thursday tracking carbon emissions prices. German power contract for delivery in 2019 edged 0.3% up at 52.63 EUR/MWh. The French equivalent contract ended 0.2% higher at 56.65 EUR/MWh.

EU carbon contracts rebounded above the 20-euro mark on Thursday in a strong auction with more buyers. For instance, the contract expiring in 2020 was 2.7% up at 20.75 euros a tonne.
