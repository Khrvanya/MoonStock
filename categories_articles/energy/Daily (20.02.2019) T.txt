Daily (20.02.2019) The German spot power price jumped by over 10% influenced by weaker wind power production 
20022019

1042
DailyAfter hitting 2019 highs, oil prices calmed on Tuesday, as concerns about global crude oil demand, and uncertainty around U.S.-China trade talks, counterbalanced OPEC's supply cuts and U.S. sanctions on Venezuela and Iran. Brent crude settled 0.1% lower at 66.45 USD/bbl, whereas WTI futures inched up by 0.1% to 56.09 USD/bbl.Despite milder weather forecasts, British spot gas price edged slightly higher on Tuesday, influenced by weaker Norwegian gas flows to Britain. NBP spot price rose by 0.3% to 46.05 p/therm, while the Q2 2019 contract traded 1% up at 44.83 p/therm.
 
European spot power prices were bullish on Tuesday. The German day-ahead power price hiked by 10.4% to 45.60 EUR/MWh, supported by reduced wind power production and weaker export demand from neighboring countries. Meanwhile, the equivalent French contract traded 0.3% higher at 47.16 EUR/MWh.
 
Along the curve, electricity prices also posted gains following stronger coal and carbon emission markets. The German power contract for delivery in 2020 traded 0.4% higher at 46.31 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract edged 0.1% higher at 49.52 EUR/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices posted minor gains on Tuesday, following a decent auction result. The price for the 2021-expiry contract increased by 1% to 21.18 EUR/tonne.
