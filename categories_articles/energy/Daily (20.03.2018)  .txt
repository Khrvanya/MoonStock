Daily (20.03.2018)  German Spot Power Price Spiked by 979% on Monday Due to Lower Than Expected Temperatures 
20032018

1210
DailyOil prices lowered in choppy trade on Monday due to investor's cautious sentiment about crude supply increase and uncertainties in relations between Saudi Arabia and Iran. 
Brent crude slipped by 16 cents, or about 0.2%, to settle at $66.05 a barrel. U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude futures fell by $0.28, or 0.5 percent, to settle at $62.06 per barrel.
 
British wholesale gas prices dropped on Monday as a rise in temperatures is forecasted for the following days and due to an oversupplied system. NBP day-ahead gas price slipped by 0.50 pence, or 1%, to close at 54.50 p/therm. Summer 2018 delivery contract traded 0.3% up at 44.91 p/therm, on concerns over low gas storage levels.
 
European prompt power prices were mostly bearish on Monday on milder weather forecast, while in Germany negative temperatures brought a huge spike in prices and higher demand. The benchmark German day-ahead spot price for Monday delivery shot 38.96 euros up, or almost 979%, to close at 42.94 euros/megawatt-hour (MWh). The equivalent French contract declined by 5.7% to 48.83 euros/MWh.
 
Further along the curve, electricity contracts traded down on higher wind power production in Germany and low demand in France. Germany's Cal '19 baseload contract for 2019 delivery, the European benchmark, was down by 1.5 percent at 34.13 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract slipped by 0.8 percent to a 39.41 euros/MWh close.
 
European carbon prices kept the downtrend on Monday despite stronger auction. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 edged 1% lower to 11.13 euros a tonne.
