Daily (20.12.2017) Brent oil rose 0.6% on Tuesday, buyout by a North Sea pipeline outage and forecasts of lower U.S. crude inventories 
20122017

1132
DailyOil edged up toward $64 a barrel on Tuesday, supported by a North Sea pipeline outage, OPEC-led supply reductions and expectations that U.S. crude inventories had decreased for a fifth week.Brent crude closed 39 cents, or 0.6 percent, higher at $63.80 a barrel. U.S. crude ended 40 cents or 0.7 percent up at $57.56.
 
British wholesale gas prices declined on Tuesday due to robust Norwegian flows and lower demand because of increasing temperatures. NBP day-ahead gas price dropped by 0.95 pence, or 1.6%, to 59.25 p/therm. The January contract traded 1.6% down at 58.96 p/therm.
 
European spot electricity prices for day-ahead delivery increased on Tuesday despite forecasts showing a decline in consumption due to milder weather. German baseload electricity price for Wednesday delivery surged by 1.9 percent, to 61.83 euros/MWh. The French contract soared by 3.3 percent to 66.50 euros/MWh.

Contracts along the forward curve fell, tracking lower coal prices, while carbon emissions added gains. German year-ahead Cal'18 baseload contract, the European benchmark, dropped by around 1% to 37.83 euros/MWh. The French year-ahead contract was 1 percent lower at 43.60 euros/MWh.

European carbon prices surged for a fourth consecutive day to hit a fresh two-week peak on Tuesday, as the market entered its annual supply-constrained holiday period. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 settled at 7.60 euros a tonne.
