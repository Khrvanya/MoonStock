Daily (22.05.2018) WTI futures settled 1.4% to new peak on Monday following geopolitical tensions and postponed U.S and China trade war. 
22052018

1318
DailyOil prices rose on Monday, rebounding from earlier losses after geopolitical tensions led to stronger crude market and a trade war between the U.S. and China was declared on hold. Brent futures added $0.71, or 0.9% to close at $79.22 a barrel. WTI crude gained $0.96, or 1.4% to close at $72,24 a barrel, new high.British wholesale gas prices hiked on Monday as predicted peak wind output will fall. UK day-ahead gas price surged by 0.85 pence, or 1.5%, to close at 57.00 p/therm. Meanwhile, June 2018 delivery contract traded 1.9% higher at 56.52 p/therm.

European wholesale power prices soared on Monday to new record high tracking gains in carbon emissions and fuel prices and predicted higher demand. The German day-ahead power price rose by 17.6% to close at 43.76 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract jumped by 104.9% to close at 46.37 euros/MWh.

Further along the curve, German Cal 19' European benchmark settled 0.6% higher at 42.20 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract closed 0.4% higher at 47.01 euros/MWh.

EU carbon prices jumped to a new seven-year high above EUR15.30 level on Monday, as a continental European holiday preserved volume low and an auction off the calendar. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 closed 1.9% up at 15.73 euros/tonne.
