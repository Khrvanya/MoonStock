Daily (22.10.2018) European power contracts on the curve posted gains on Friday ahead of the colder season 
22102018

1135
DailyOil prices rose on Friday, supported by expectations for rising demand from China. On the other side, the increase in inventories and U.S. - Sino trade tensions capped further gains, with Brent crude price trading 0.6% higher at $79.78 a barrel. Meanwhile, WTI crude futures soared by 0.7% to $69.12 a barrel.Cooler weather forecasts and consequently, increased demand for heating, boosted the UK gas contracts on Friday. NBP spot price jumped by 3.2% to 68.60 p/therm. Along the forward curve, the 2019 delivery contract closed 2.9% up at 66.80 p/therm.
 
On Friday, European prompt power prices dropped, due to predictions of additional wind supply in Germany and increased nuclear production in France. The Germany baseload price for Monday delivery tumbled by 12.2% to 59.38 euros/MWh. At the same time, the French equivalent contract ended 9.4% lower at 60.31 euros/MWh.
 
Prices on the forward curve rose, tracking the bullish oil and gas markets. The German Cal' 19 electricity contract traded 3.2% higher at 54.29 euros/MWh. The equivalent French contract gained 2.8% to settle at 58.24 euros/MWh.
 
Friday saw a downward evolution in European carbon contracts, following a weaker auction. The price for the contract expiring in 2021 edged 0.2% lower at 21.64 euros a tonne, posting overall a 6% loss week-on-week.
