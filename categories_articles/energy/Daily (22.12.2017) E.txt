Daily (22.12.2017) EU carbon prices hiked by 2.7% to hit a two-year peak on Thursday 
22122017

1015
DailyOil prices rose slightly on Thursday, erasing preceding losses after the operator of Britain's Forties pipeline in the North Sea declared it was expected to restart at the beginning of January after repairs over Christmas. Brent futures settled 34 cents, or 0.5 percent, higher at $64.90 a barrel. Meanwhile, U.S. West Texas Intermediate crude was up 27 cents, or 0.5 percent, at $58.36 per barrel.
 
British wholesale gas prices dropped on Thursday, pressured by milder weather forecasts and robust Norwegian flows. The Christmas days, traditionally characterised by lower gas demand and electricity consumption, was an additional reason that prices were down, according to a trader. NBP day-ahead gas price decreased by 0.75 pence, or 1.3%, to end at 57.75 p/therm. The January contract traded 1.44 pence, or 2.4%, down at 58.78 p/therm.
 
European spot power prices for day-ahead delivery declined on Thursday weighed down by a forecast drop in demand ahead of the Christmas weekend and improving nuclear power availability in France. German baseload electricity contract for Friday delivery slumped by 8.3 percent to 39.49 euros/MWh. The French spot power price for Friday delivery tumbled over 13 percent to settle at 55.34 euros/MWh.
 
Electricity prices were mixed along the forward curve. The German year-ahead contract, the European benchmark, slipped 0.4 percent to 37.95 euros/MWh. The French 2018 delivery contract was slightly higher at 44.01 euros/MWh.

European carbon prices surged for a sixth straight day on Thursday, extending their year-end rally to flirt with a two-year peak. The carbon contract expiring in 2020 closed 2.7% up at 8.18 euros a tonne.
