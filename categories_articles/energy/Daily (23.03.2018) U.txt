Daily (23.03.2018) UK day-ahead gas price fell by almost 5% due to milder weather forecasts 
23032018

1121
DailyOil prices fell on Thursday because investors booked profits after this week's rally, but ongoing efforts of OPEC and its partners to cut supplies limited losses. Brent crude dropped by 0.8 percent, to close at $68.91 a barrel. WTI futures declined by 1.3% to end at $64.30 a barrel.UK wholesale gas prices dropped on Thursday, due to forecasts for higher temperatures and increased British supplies following the arrival of two liquefied natural gas vessels this month. NBP spot price slumped by 4.8% to close at 54.00 p/therm. Meanwhile, Q2 2018 delivery contract settled 0.6% lower at 47.19 p/therm.


European spot power prices were mixed on Thursday as expectations of a sharp decline in wind generation boosted German day-ahead price, while lower-than-expected demand weakened French spot contract. 

German baseload contract for day-ahead delivery traded 21.3% higher at 52.89 euros/MWh. The French spot price tumbled by 12.2% to close at 53.90 euros/MWh.
 
Further along the curve, German Cal 19' European benchmark edged 0.3% lower at 35.55 euros/MWh, while the French equivalent contract was 0.1% down at 40.48 euros/MWh.

EU carbon prices decreased on Thursday after hitting the EUR13 level following a strong auction, as French President Emmanuel Macron discussed about the necessity for a European carbon price floor. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 traded 2.4% down at 12.41 euros/tonne.
