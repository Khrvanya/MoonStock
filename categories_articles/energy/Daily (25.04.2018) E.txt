Daily (25.04.2018) European carbon prices rose by 2.7% on Tuesday after a stronger auction 
25042018

1133
DailyOil prices fell on Tuesday from 3  1/2  - year peaks reached the previous session, as increasing U.S. fuel inventories and production pressured an otherwise bullish market. Brent futures lost $0.85, or 1.1%, to close at $73.86. WTI crude dropped by 1.4% to end at $67.70 a barrel.An oversupplied system led to a decline in British wholesale gas prices on Tuesday. UK day-ahead gas price decreased by 0.20 pence, or 0.4%, down to 50.80 p/therm. Meanwhile, May 2018 delivery contract settled 2% lower at 50.60 p/therm.

European power prices dropped on Tuesday because of higher wind generation and reduced demand. German electricity spot price tumbled by 20.5% to end at 27.29 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract traded 20% lower at 28.48 euros/MWh, mainly due to improved nuclear supply.

Further along the curve, German Cal 19' European benchmark was almost constant at 38.08 euros/MWh. Meanwhile, French equivalent contract lost 0.2% to settle at 42.79 euros/MWh.

European carbon prices recovered on Tuesday after a stronger auction encouraged buyers, causing a session of short-covering. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 traded 2.7% higher at 13.39 euros/tonne.
