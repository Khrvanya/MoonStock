Daily (25.07.2019) NBP spot plunged by 3.7% on Wednesday, as Sterling hit a one-month high versus the euro 
25072019

1039
DailyCrude oil prices nudged down on Wednesday, despite a large decrease in U.S. crude stockpiles as investors are concerned about global oil demand. Saudi Arabia's and Kuwait's plans to resume oil production in the Saudi-Kuwaiti Neutral Zone also put pressure on oil prices.  As a result, Brent crude inched 1% down at $63.18 a barrel, while WTI futures settled 1.6% lower at $55.88 a barrel.British gas prices declined on Wednesday as carbon prices continued their downward evolution and Sterling hit a one-month high versus the euro, which might make British gas less attractive for continental buyers. As a result, NBP spot corrected its earlier bullish trend, dropping by 3.7% for the day, to 28.30 p/therm. Further along the curve, the contract for delivery in Q4 2019 traded 3.2% lower at 46.64 p/therm.
 
European day-ahead power prices were in a bearish mood on Wednesday. The price for the German day-ahead power contract fell by 0.8% to 50.23 EUR/MWh, due to stable supply ahead of the heatwave. Meanwhile, the equivalent French contract settled 5% lower at 50.26 EUR/MWh due to rising wind power supply.
 
Meanwhile, forward electricity prices lost ground on Wednesday, tracking  weaker carbon prices. The price for the German power contract for delivery in 2020 decreased by 1.3% to 52.78 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract traded 1% lower at 54.58 EUR/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices declined on Wednesday, amid a bearish energy complex. As a result, the price for the 2021-expiry contract closed 2.1% down at 30.13 EUR/tonne.
