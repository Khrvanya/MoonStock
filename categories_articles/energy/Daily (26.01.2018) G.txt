Daily (26.01.2018) German day-ahead power price surged by 37% on Thursday due to forecasts for declining wind generation 
26012018

1139
DailyOil prices declined on Thursday before the end of the peak-demand winter season in the northern hemisphere, as crude market fundamentals are expected to weaken. Brent crude decreased by 11 cents, or 0.2%, to close at $70.42 per barrel.
 
British wholesale gas prices were mixed on Thursday. NBP Winter 2018 delivery contract traded 0.3% down at 50.78 p/therm, because of mild temperatures and predictions for increased wind generation. NBP day-ahead price rose 2% to 47.83 p/therm, due to an outage at Vesterled pipeline.
 
European spot electricity prices for day-ahead delivery posted robust gains on Thursday as forecasts indicated wind power generation tambling day-on-day, while consumption will increase in France because of lower temperatures.
 


The benchmark German day-ahead spot price for Friday delivery jumped by 12.18 euros, or approximately 37 percent, to end at 45.15 euros/megawatt-hour (MWh). The equivalent French contract soared by more than 31 percent to 45.14 euros/MWh.

Year-ahead power curve prices diverged. Germany's Cal '19 baseload contract for 2019 delivery, the European benchmark, was up by 0.2 percent at 35.62 euros/MWh.

The less-liquid French contract slipped 0.86 percent to a 40.20 euros/MWh close.

EU carbon dropped in a choppy trade on Thursday after a 23% raise during the last two weeks. The carbon contract expiring in 2020 ended 2.3% lower at 9.39 euros a tonne.
