Daily (26.09.2019) NBP spot tumbled by 6.7% on Wednesday weighed by robust LNG send-outs 
26092019

1122
DailyOil prices extended losses on Wednesday, after an unexpected rise in U.S. crude stockpiles and a faster-than-awaited recovery of Saudi Arabia's oil production. A rally in the dollar index, which moves inversely with oil, also put pressure on crude futures. As a result, WTI futures dropped by 1.4% to 56.49 USD/bbl, while Brent crude ended 1.1% lower at 62.39 USD/bbl.British wholesale gas prices lost ground on Wednesday, due to higher LNG send-outs from the Isle of Grain terminal. NBP spot price plunged by 6.7% to 25.00 p/therm. On the curve, the October 2019 delivery contract price decreased by 4% to 30.92 p/therm.
 
European prompt power prices continued the downward trend on Wednesday. The price for the German day-ahead power contract tumbled by 11.1% to 41.57 EUR/MWh, due to a sharp rise in wind production. Meanwhile, the equivalent French contract ended 1.5% lower at 39.31 EUR/MWh, influenced by weaker demand and increased nuclear power availability.
 
Forward electricity prices also posted losses, tracking lower gas and carbon prices. The price for the German power contract for delivery in 2020 dropped by 2% to 48.46 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract traded 1.5% lower at 51.11 EUR/MWh.
 
European carbon prices declined on Wednesday amid a bearish energy complex. The 2019-expiry contract settled 1.3% lower at 25.21 euros/tonne.
