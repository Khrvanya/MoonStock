Daily (27.02.2020) British gas prices jumped on Wednesday, spurred by colder weather and an undersupplied system 
27022020

1000
DailyCrude oil futures collapsed to their lowest level in more than a year on Wednesday amid mounting concerns that the higher number of new coronavirus cases in Europe and the Middle East would hamper oil demand around the world.Brent crude for April delivery slipped by $1.52, or 2.77%, to settle at $53.43 a barrel on ICE London. WTI crude ended at $48.73 a barrel, down $1.17, or 2.34% on NYMEX.

The British gas market had a bullish evolution  on Wednesday, spurred by colder weather and an undersupplied system.
NBP spot rose by 1.4% to 25.10 p/therm.
 
Further along the curve, the gas price for March delivery firmed by 2.3% to 23.06 p/therm, while the gas for April delivery soared by about 2% to 21.47 p/therm.
 
European spot power prices edged higher on Wednesday amid colder weather in Germany and weaker nuclear availability in France. The German day-ahead power price surged by 21.5% to 38.47 EUR/MWh, while the price for the equivalent contract in France rallied by 11.6% to 34.59 EUR/MWh.
 
Along the year-ahead curve, prices lost ground on Wednesday tracking a bearish energy complex. German Cal'21 power shed 0.6% to 41.03 EUR/MWh, while the same contract in France declined by 0.5% to 42.60 EUR/MWh.

EUAs expiring in 2021 rose by 0.3% to 24.45 EUR/tonne amid a stronger auction.
