Daily (27.05.2019) Brent crude recovered by 1.4% on Friday, posting however its biggest loss of 2019 
27052019

1207
DailyCrude oil prices rose on Friday due to the long US and UK holiday weekends, offsetting concerns about rising inventories and weak global demand. As a result, Brent crude climbed by 1.4% to $68.69 a barrel on Friday, while WTI futures added 1.2% to settle at $58.63 a barrel.Gas prices varied on Friday. NBP day-ahead edged 0.2% higher at 29.25 p/therm, due to rising gas-for-power demand. Meanwhile, forward prices posted losses, on expectations for warmer weather and an oversupply in the system. The contract for Q1 20 delivery closed 0.8% down at 57.71 p/therm.
 
European prompt power prices lost ground on Friday weighed by lower demand. The price for the German day-ahead power contract decreased by 9.3% to 36.68 EUR/MWh on rising wind production. At the same time, the equivalent French contract closed 5.5% down at 36.68 EUR/MWh, due to stable nuclear supply.
 
Forward electricity prices, also declined, tracking weaker carbon and gas prices. The price for the German power contract for delivery in 2020 edged 0.1% down at 48.52 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract traded 0.4% lower at 51.47 EUR/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices lost momentum on Friday, clearing some of its previous gains, following the announcement about UK Prime Minister's resignation that intensified Brexit uncertainty, leading to a bearish sentiment in the market. The price for the contract expiring in 2021 dropped by 2.2% to 26.03 EUR/tonne.
