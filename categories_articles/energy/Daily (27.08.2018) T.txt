Daily (27.08.2018) The 2021-expiry carbon contract hit another 10-year peak at 22.18 euros a tonne on Friday 
27082018

1105
DailyOil prices rose on Friday, on signs that Iran sanctions might weigh on global supply, while China's demand for U.S. crude doesn't seem to be affected by trade disputes. Brent crude surged by $1.09, or 1.5%, to settle at $75.82 a barrel. Meanwhile, U.S. crude closed $0.89, or 1.3%, higher at $68.72.Decreased flows from Norway due to maintenance at Kollsnes gas processing plant led to an undersupplied market, and consequently to a rise in British wholesale gas prices on Friday. NBP spot price jumped by 1.25 pence, or 1.9%, to end at 67 pence per therm. Along the forward curve, Winter 2018 gas contract was 1.1% higher at 72.15 p/therm.
 
European spot power prices dropped on Friday, on forecasts for stronger wind power generation over the weekend and due to lower consumption in France. The German baseload power contract for delivery on Monday traded 6.9% down at 53.65 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract lost 4.7% to end at 59.24 EUR/MWh. 
 
Nevertheless, electricity prices on the curve had an opposite trend, following the bullish coal and carbon markets. The German Cal'19 delivery contract closed 0.4% higher at 49.27 EUR/MWh, while the French equivalent contract gained 0.3% to settle at 54.09 EUR/MWh.
 
EUAs continued to gain momentum on Friday, prolonging the 10-year highs attained in the previous session, on expectations for another week of curtailed auctions and higher correction risks. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 was 1.6% up at 20.95 euros a tonne.
