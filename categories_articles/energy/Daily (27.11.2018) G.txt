Daily (27.11.2018) German spot power price hiked by 22% on Monday, on lower wind generation forecasts 
27112018

1140
DailyOil prices gained ground on Monday, on expectations for a reduction in Saudi Arabia's output, which hit a record level of approximately 11 million barrels per day this month. Moreover, crude inventories at the delivery point for WTI at Cushing, Oklahoma rose less-than-predicted from Tuesday to Friday.However, gains were capped by persisting uncertainty over global economic growth. Brent crude was at $60.48 a barrel, while WTI crude ended at $51.63 a barrel, adding 2.9% and 2.4% respectively. 
 
Increased LNG supplies and robust flows from Norway created an oversupplied system and put pressure on most of the UK gas contracts on Monday. NBP spot dropped 1.8% to 64.80 p/therm, while the price for December 2018 delivery contract slumped by 2.3% to 66.07 p/therm. Still, cooler weather predictions supported several contracts on the curve, with the Summer 2019 contract closing at 55.38 p/therm.
 
European spot power prices for day-ahead delivery were bullish on Monday, due to reduced wind generation and higher demand. The baseload price for day-ahead delivery in Germany surged by 21.9% to 75.51 euros/MWh. Meanwhile, the French spot electricity contract traded 16.6% up at 77.50 euros/MWh.
 
Along the forward curve, electricity prices had a downward evolution due to a sharp decline in carbon and gas markets. The German contract for next-year delivery was at 51.28 euros/MWh and the French Cal'19 power contract closed at 56.11 euros/MWh. Both contracts lost 2.3%.
 
On Monday the European carbon contracts continued their bearish trend. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 was 1.9% down at 20.19 euros a tonne.
