Daily (28.05.2018) UK day-ahead gas price dropped by 5.6% on Friday due to warmer weather forecasts. 
28052018

1125
DailyOil prices fell on Friday as Saudi Arabia and Russia discussed about the possibility to reduce production cuts that have boosted prices to their highest levels since 2014. Brent crude lost $2.35, or 3%, to close at $76.44 a barrel. WTI futures ended $2.83, or 4%, lower at $67.88 a barrel.British wholesale gas prices dropped on Friday on forecasts for warmer weather and due to the restart of some flows from Norway. UK day-ahead gas price fell by 3.25 pence, or 5.6%, to end at 55.10 p/therm. Meanwhile, June 2018 delivery contract settled 3.6% lower at 55.17 p/therm.

European wholesale electricity prices fell on reversed coal, oil and carbon emissions rights markets. German day-ahead power price tumbled by 8.1% to close at 37.08 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract lost 21.3% to settle at 33.69 euros/MWh.

Further along the curve, German Cal 19' European benchmark ended 1.9% lower at 42.01 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract closed 2.1% lower at 46.71 euros/MWh.

European carbon prices slumped from the previous session's 7-year peak on Friday, staying close to EUR16 mark after the day's auction ended at its biggest ever absolute discount to the secondary market. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 settled 1.8% down at 16.19 euros/tonne.
