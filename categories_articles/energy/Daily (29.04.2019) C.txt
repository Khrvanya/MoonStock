Daily (29.04.2019) Crude oil prices plunged by almost 3% on Friday, after Donald Trump urged OPEC countries to raise output 
29042019

1006
DailyCrude oil prices lost ground on Friday, after Donald Trump urged OPEC countries to raise output as to stop further gains in crude prices. Brent crude plunged by 3% to $72.15 a barrel, while WTI futures traded 2.9% lower at $63.30 a barrel.British gas prices posted gains on Friday, as colder weather forecasts increased demand. NBP day-ahead price rose by 1.7% to 32.75 p/therm. Meanwhile, on the curve, the contract for delivery in Q4 2019 lost 0.8% closing at 53.95 p/therm, due to strong wind generation that reduced gas-for-power demand.
 
European spot power prices tumbled on Friday due to warmer weather. The price for the German day-ahead power contract slumped by 26.5% to 31.99 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract traded 39.3% lower at 35.73 EUR/MWh, on higher-than usual nuclear power availability.
 
Along the curve, electricity prices posted losses tracking lower gas and carbon prices. The price for the German power contract for delivery in 2020 dropped by 1.7% to 49.58 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract lost 1.8% to close at 52.49 EUR/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices slumped on Friday, amid aggressive selling before the end of the annual compliance season. The price for the 2020-expiry contract traded 5.2% lower at 26.15 EUR/tonne.
