Daily (29.05.2018) French day-ahead power price soared by around 40% on Monday on strong carbon emissions rights market 
29052018

1141
DailyOil prices continued their bearish trend on Monday as Saudi Arabia and Russia announced they might boost supplies, while U.S. production gains are not slowing. Brent crude lost $1.14, or 1.5%, to end at $75.30 a barrel. WTI futures closed $1.36, or 2%, down at $66.52 a barrel.UK wholesale gas prices were unchanged on Monday, as no trading occurred due to a Bank Holiday.

European wholesale electricity prices soared on Monday due to strong carbon emissions rights markets. German day-ahead gas price surged by 15.7% to end at 42.91 euros/MWh. The French spot power price jumped by 39.4% to settle at 46.97 euros/MWh.

Further along the curve, German Cal 19' European benchmark closed 0.8% higher at 42.35 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract gained 0.7% to hit 47.04 euros/MWh.

European carbon prices recovered on Monday despite weak auction. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 settled 1.9% higher at 16.50 euros/tonne.
