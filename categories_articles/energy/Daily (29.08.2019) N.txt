Daily (29.08.2019) NBP gas prices surged on Wednesday amid a bullish energy complex 
29082019

1139
DailyOil prices rose on Wednesday after data showing a strong decline in U.S. stockpiles softened worries about lower oil demand caused by the trade war between the U.S and China. Brent crude added 1.7% to close at $60.49 a barrel. WTI futures gained 1.6% to settle at $55.78 a barrel.Most of the British wholesale gas prices had an upward trend on Wednesday, buyout by a hike in oil and carbon prices. UK day-ahead gas price surged by 0.4% to 27.80 p/therm, while the September 2019 delivery contract ended 1.8% higher at 28.77 p/therm.
 
European prompt electricity prices dropped on Wednesday due to a subdued demand in Germany and France, amid predictions for higher thermal and nuclear electricity production. The German spot power price lost 12.5% to close at 44.49 euros/MWh. The French day-ahead power price fell by 8.8% to 42.68 euros/MWh. 
 
Forward electricity prices surged due to higher carbon prices. The German Cal 2020 delivery contract traded 1.2% higher at 47.48 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract closed 1% up at 49.06 euros/MWh.
 
European carbon prices were bullish on Wednesday reaching the EUR26 level as higher oil prices supported the entire energy complex, pulling carbon out of its technical weakness. The carbon contract expiring in 2020 added 2.3% to trade at 26.26 euros/tonne.
