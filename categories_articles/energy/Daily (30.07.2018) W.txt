Daily (30.07.2018) WTI crude dropped by 1.3% on Friday due to a fall in the U.S equities market 
30072018

1252
DailyOil prices fell on Friday, due to a decline in the U.S. equities market. Brent futures dropped by 25 cents, or 0.3%, to close at $74.29 a barrel, posting nevertheless a 1.8% weekly gain for the first time in four weeks supported by reduced trade tensions and a provisory closure of an important crude oil shipping lane. WTI futures declined by 1.3% to close at $68.69 a barrel.Expectations for cooler weather led to an insignificant rise in British day-ahead gas price on Friday to 57.75 p/therm. On the curve, prices dropped on forecasts for higher wind output and an oversupplied gas system due to higher flows from Norway. 2019 delivery contract ended 0.4% down at 56.08 p/therm.

European power prices decreased on Friday weighted down by predictions for lower demand. German day-ahead electricity price plunged by 13.5% to end at 46.40 euros/MWh. French spot power price tumbled by 15.8% to close at 47.35 euros/MWh.

Further along the curve, bearish power prices tracked carbon, oil, gas and coal markets. German Cal 19' European benchmark traded 0.6% down at 44.40 euros/MWh. The French equivalent contract edged 0.3% lower at 49.20 euros/MWh.

EU carbon prices tumbled on Friday due to a weak auction, remaining above the EUR17 level. The carbon contract expiring in 2019 settled 1% lower at 17.37 euros/tonne.
