Daily (30.10.2017) Oil prices surged 2% on Friday due to expected extension of output reduction 
30102017

1012
DailyOil prices jumped about 2 percent on Friday, with global benchmark Brent crude surging above $60 per barrel, on support among the world's major producers for prolonging a deal to reduce output. Brent futures increased by $1.14, or 1.9 percent, to end at $60.44 a barrel, after hitting a session peak of $60.53, the highest since July 2015 and more than 35% above 2017 lows touched in June.
 
Meanwhile, U.S. West Texas Intermediate crude oil (WTI) closed the session up $1.26, or 2.4 percent, at $53.90 after attaining a session peak of $53.98 a barrel, the highest since early March.
 
British prompt gas prices ended almost unchanged on Friday. NBP gas price for day-ahead delivery was constant at 46.10 p/therm. Along the forward curve, Summer 2018 delivery contract traded 0.5% up at 42.48 p/therm, as traders considered that market fundamentals were bullish. 

European prompt electricity prices on Friday tracked anticipated wind generation patterns in Germany, the main market. Mid-term and prices along the curve surged on news of nuclear restart postponements in France and due to several religious holidays in the region next week. 

German baseload power price for Monday delivery was at 31.94 euros/MWh, around 5 percent above the price paid for Friday.  French Monday electricity was at 51.89 euros, 6 percent less than the 55.75 euros paid for Friday.

German baseload electricity contract for delivery in 2018 settled 1% up at 36.7 euros/MWh. The equivalent French contract rose 1.2 percent to 42.27 euros.

EU carbon prices traded heavily, but failed to respond to strong gains in electricity prices on Friday and have now dropped for six out of the past seven sessions. December 2020 expiry EU carbon permits shed 0.4 percent to 7.38 euros a tonne.
