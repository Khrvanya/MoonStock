Daily (7.03.2019) European carbon prices declined by 3.3% on Wednesday due to a bearish energy complex 
07032019

1022
DailyCrude oil prices were mixed on Wednesday, after U.S. data showed an increase in weekly U.S. crude stockpiles. A slump in U.S. equity indexes also weighed on the market. WTI futures traded 0.6% lower at $56.22 a barrel. However, Brent crude edged 0.2% higher at $65.99 a barrel, after EIA data indicated a larger-than-expected draw in U.S. distillate inventories. British gas prices continued to decline on Wednesday as warmer weather and more LNG arrivals left the system oversupplied. NBP day-ahead price dropped by 1.6% to 42.70 p/therm, while the Q2 2019 delivery contract traded 1.8% lower at 41.97 p/therm.
 
European day-ahead power prices tumbled on Wednesday, due to an expected increase in wind generation. The price for the German spot power contract plunged by 16.5% to 29.02 EUR/MWh. At the same time, the equivalent French contract traded 9.3% lower at 32.65 EUR/MWh, influenced by steady nuclear availability. 
 
Along the curve, electricity prices reversed trend on Wednesday, tracking lower carbon and coal prices. The price for German power contract for delivery in 2020 dropped by 1.3% to 48.14 EUR/MWh, while the equivalent French contract settled 1.4% down at 51.18 EUR/MWh.
 
EU carbon prices continued to lose ground on Wednesday, amid a bearish energy complex. The price for the 2020-expiry contract ended 3.3% lower at 22.53 EUR/tonne.
