Alcoa Opens Lithium-Aluminum Aerospace Plant, Novelis Shows Off Recycling ProwessAlcoa officially unveiled the world's largest aluminum-lithium facility, a massive warehouse on a 170-acre, multi-building campus that took $90 million and 2 years of construction to create this week. Located adjacent to the company's rolling mill in Nachterstedt, Germany, the $258 million (EUR200 million) recycling center will process up to 400,000 metric tons of aluminum scrap annually, turning it back into high-value aluminum ingots to feed the company's European manufacturing network.
