Alcoa, Inc. Pulls Out of Australian Deal and Closes SmeltersAlcoa Inc. said Monday it would close a large aluminum smelter in Australia, cutting total capacity by 4.8%, as stubbornly low raw aluminum prices mean "the 50-year-old smelter has no prospect of becoming financially viable," according to a statement.FREE Download: The Monthly MMI(r) Report - covering the Aluminum market.Alcoa said the Point Henry aluminum smelter at Geelong in Victoria state will close in August. The company owns 60% of the smelter through its local joint venture with Australia's Alumina Ltd.
