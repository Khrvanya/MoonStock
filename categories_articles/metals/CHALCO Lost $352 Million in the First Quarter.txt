CHALCO Lost $352 Million in the First QuarterAluminum Corp. of China (CHALCO), the nation's biggest producer of the metal, said its first-quarter loss widened as industry overcapacity caused prices to fall. The Beijing-based company posted a loss of 2.2 billion CNY ($352 million), or 0.16 CNY a share, in the three months ended March 31 from 975 million CNY, or 0.07 CNY a share, a year ago, it said in a statement to the Shanghai Stock Exchange. Sales rose 3 percent to 35.2 billion CNY.
