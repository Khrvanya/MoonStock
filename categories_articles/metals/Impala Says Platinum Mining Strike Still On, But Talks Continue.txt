Impala Says Platinum Mining Strike Still On, But Talks ContinueThe world's three largest platinum companies and the biggest union at their mines in South Africa will this week continue talks to end a strike that has crippled production for the last five months.FREE Download: The Monthly MMI(r) Report - covering the Precious markets.While members of the Association of Mineworkers and Construction Union late last week accepted a pay proposal "in principle" from Anglo American Platinum, Impala Platinum Holdings and Lonmin, the AMCU has said it has conditions to put to the companies before agreeing to any deal. A Reuters report that stated the parties had reached an agreement was incorrect, Johan Theron, a spokesman for Impala, told Bloomberg Businessweek by phone today."Discussions are expected between the union and the companies in the day or two ahead," Charmane Russell, a spokeswoman for the producers at Johannesburg-based Russell and Associates, said in an e-mailed response to questions.
