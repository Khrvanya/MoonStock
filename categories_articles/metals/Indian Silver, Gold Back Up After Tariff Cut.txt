Indian Silver, Gold Back Up After Tariff CutThe government of India recently slashed import tariffs on gold to $391 per 10 grams and silver to $551 per kg following weak global price trends. That cut was finally reflected in prices this week as both beleaguered precious metals posted strong gains for the first time in weeks.FREE Download: The Monthly MMI(r) Report - covering the Precious markets.As a result of the stronger performance, the tariff value on imported gold was fixed at $401 per 10 grams and on silver at $575 per kg, the Times of India reported.At INR 37,348 ($609.22) per kilogram, Indian silver moved up 6.0% on Monday, November 17, the day's biggest change. One of the day's biggest movers was Chinese silver which rose 3.5% to close at CNY 3,525 ($573.94) per kilogram. US silver prices rose 2.6% to $16.07 per ounce.
