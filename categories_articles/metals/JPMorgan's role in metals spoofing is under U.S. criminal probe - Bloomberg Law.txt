JPMorgan's role in metals spoofing is under U.S. criminal probe - Bloomberg LawFeb 5 (Reuters) - U.S. authorities that accused six JPMorgan Chase & Co employees of rigging precious-metals futures are building a criminal case against the bank itself, Bloomberg Law reported on Wednesday, citing people familiar with the matter. No formal accusations have been made against the bank, the report bit.ly/36WJwPP added. The probe raises the prospect of criminal charges and significant fines against the bank, the report said. JPMorgan was not immediately available for a Reuters request for comment. (Reporting by Bharath Manjesh in Bengaluru; Editing by Maju Samuel)
