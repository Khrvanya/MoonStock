Los Broncos Miners’ Strike in Chile Sends Copper HigherCopper futures rose after a mine worker strike in Chile, the world's top copper producer, turned violent. After contract-worker protests disrupted operations at Anglo American PLC's Los Broncos mine in Chile. The company chose to shut down the mine on Monday to ensure safety, a company spokesman told The Wall Street Journal via email. Work at the mine was restarted today, and Anglo American expects production to return to normal levels. The cash price of primary copper rose 1.6 percent on Monday on the LME.
