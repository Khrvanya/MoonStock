METALS-London copper eases as virus toll continues to riseFeb 14 (Reuters) - Copper prices on the London Metal Exchange (LME) fell on Friday, as the rising death toll from the coronavirus in China dented the demand outlook for the metal. Three-month copper on the LME declined 0.3% to $5,775.50 a tonne by 0227 GMT, while aluminium dipped 0.3% to $1,743 a tonne, nickel fell 0.7% to $13,195 a tonne and lead fell 1.2% to $1,869.50 a tonne. The death toll in China's Hubei province, the epicentre of the epidemic, has risen by 116, with the total number of cases up by nearly 5,000, health officials said on Friday.
