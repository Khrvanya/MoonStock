Nickel Up on LME as Governments Worry About InflationGovernments around the world, especially in the emerging countries, are beginning to worry about inflation and currency devaluation and nothing could be better news for the value of globally-traded nickel. Turkey and India have taken emergency measures to increase rates and here in the US the Fed has announced the beginning of the end of its bond-buying program later this year.The nickel 3-month price saw the biggest upwards shift for the day, rising 1.0 percent on the LME to close at $16,160 per metric ton on Tuesday, March 25. On the LME, the spot price of nickel rose 0.9 percent to $16,130 per metric ton.
