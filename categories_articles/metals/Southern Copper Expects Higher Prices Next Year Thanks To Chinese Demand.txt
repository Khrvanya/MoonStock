Southern Copper Expects Higher Prices Next Year Thanks To Chinese DemandStrong demand from China for copper and an economic recovery in other parts of the world should help keep prices for the metal firm next year, the CEO of one of the world's largest copper mining companies said.FREE Download: The Monthly MMI(r) Report - covering the Copper market.Southern Copper Corp. Chief Executive Oscar Gonzalez Rocha told the Wall Street Journal he expects copper prices to average around $3 a pound next year as demand in China remains strong, down from an expected average of about $3.11 a pound this year. The average copper price in 2013 was $3.34 a pound.
