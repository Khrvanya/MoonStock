Steel Imports Surge Nearly 26% in First Quarter, Domestic Steel Prices FlatUS steel imports surged 25.7 percent in the first quarter, fueling concerns that it is foreign producers, rather than American manufacturers, that are reaping the benefits of the shale gas revolution, the Financial Times reported. The jump in steel imports has been accompanied by a drop in the price of US-made steel products and is being used by steel producers, along with their unions, trade lawyers and political allies, to press the US government to confront the problem more aggressively.
