Vale Says Samarco Costs Have Already Exceeded Insurance CapsFallout from the Samarco mine disaster in Brazil continues, and construction input prices are at their lowest cost, overall, since 2011.The cost of a deadly dual dam burst at an iron ore mine in Brazil run by Samarco has already exceeded the insurance cap for civil damages, co-owner Vale SA said on Monday.Free Sample Report: Our Annual Metal Buying OutlookSamarco, owned by Vale and BHP Billiton has been fined 250 million Brazilian reals ($65.5 million) and forced to pay for accommodations for the dispossessed, after two dams burst earlier this month, killing at least seven people, with 15 still missing.The Producer Price Index for inputs to construction industries declined for a fourth consecutive month in October, according to an analysis of the Bureau of Labor Statistics data by the Associated Builders and Contractors (ABC) of America.Free Download: The November MMI ReportThe index stands at its lowest level since the first quarter of 2011 as prices for construction inputs declined 0.2% on a monthly basis and 4.6% on a year-ago basis. Nonresidential construction input prices exhibit a similar pattern, falling 0.3% since last month and 5.1% over the past 12 months.
