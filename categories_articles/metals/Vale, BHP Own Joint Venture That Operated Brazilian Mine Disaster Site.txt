The British steel industry is urging the EU to enact dumping curbs against China.More than two dozen people remain missing days after a deadly mining accident in Brazil involving two of the world's largest mining groups, authorities in the country said on Sunday.Free Sample Report: Our Annual Metal Buying OutlookRescue services said three people were confirmed to have died when two dams burst and engulfed a small town in mining waste, and the whereabouts of another 28 people -- including 13 mine workers -- remained unknown.Brazilian media reported on Sunday that what is being called the "mud tsunami" had hit areas more than 60 miles away from the dams.Free Download: The October MMI ReportBHP Billiton said its chief executive Andrew Mackenzie would travel to Brazil this week as the Anglo-Australian miner confronts the aftermath of the accident along with Vale SA, its Brazilian joint-venture partner.
